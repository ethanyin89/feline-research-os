# Structured Abstract Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-ckd-026`
Title: Serum fibroblast growth factor 23 (FGF-23): associations with hyperphosphatemia and clinical staging of feline chronic kidney disease
Journal: Journal of Veterinary Diagnostic Investigation
Year: 2021
DOI: 10.1177/1040638720985563
Extraction Round: 2 (full Crossref abstract)

## Study Design

- Study type: original cross-sectional study
- Sample: 304 cats (196 CKD, 108 healthy controls)
- CKD staging: IRIS guideline (Stage 1: 34, Stage 2: 74, Stage 3: 51, Stage 4: 37)
- Controls stratified by age: 0-2y, 3-6y, 7-10y, 11-14y, ≥15y
- Measurements: FGF-23 and intact PTH (iPTH) by ELISA

## Key Quantitative Findings

### FGF-23 by CKD Stage

- FGF-23 was higher in cats in all CKD stages than in controls
- No statistical difference in FGF-23 among age groups in healthy cats (p = 0.15)
- No sex difference in FGF-23 in healthy cats

### Phosphorus by CKD Stage

- Higher serum phosphorus observed in stage 3 (p = 0.04) vs controls
- Higher serum phosphorus observed in stage 4 (p < 0.01) vs controls
- Stages 1-2 did not show statistically different phosphorus vs controls

### FGF-23 and iPTH Correlation

- Positive linear relationship between FGF-23 and iPTH
- Control group: r = 0.70, p < 0.01
- CKD group: r = 0.46, p = 0.02

### Assay Validation

- Intra- and inter-assay CVs ≤ 15%
- Percentage recovery of FGF-23 and iPTH stable for up to 7 days at −20°C and −80°C

## Core Claims

### Claim 1: FGF-23 elevates earlier than hyperphosphatemia

- Support: FGF-23 was higher in all CKD stages vs controls, but hyperphosphatemia only appeared in stage 3-4
- Implicit premise: FGF-23 may be an earlier biomarker than serum phosphorus
- Evidence level: B (source-supported conclusion from cross-sectional design)

### Claim 2: FGF-23 correlates with iPTH across CKD spectrum

- Support: Positive linear correlation in both healthy and CKD cats
- Implicit premise: FGF-23 and iPTH are mechanistically linked in feline CKD
- Evidence level: B

### Claim 3: Age does not independently affect FGF-23 in healthy cats

- Support: No statistical difference in FGF-23 among 5 age groups (p = 0.15)
- Implicit premise: Age-related confounding may be less problematic for FGF-23 interpretation
- Evidence level: B

## Boundary / Limits

- Cross-sectional design cannot establish temporal precedence
- Cannot prove FGF-23 predicts progression; it shows association only
- Sample is single-center (China); external validity unconfirmed
- No treatment intervention; this is observational only
- "May precede" is the authors' inference, not a proven causal claim

## Claim-Fit for CKD Module

### Safe to use

- FGF-23 as an early-stage CKD biomarker candidate
- FGF-23 and phosphorus staging interaction
- FGF-23 and iPTH correlation evidence

### Not safe to use yet

- Universal FGF-23 cutoff recommendations
- FGF-23 as a treatment decision driver
- Progression prediction claims
