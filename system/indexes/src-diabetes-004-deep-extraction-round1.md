# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-004`  
Title: `Neurological Complications Associated with Spontaneously Occurring Feline Diabetes Mellitus`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on PubMed/Oxford abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata.

## Core Takeaway

`spontaneous feline diabetes can produce clinically visible sensorimotor neuropathy with structural and biochemical nerve abnormalities, making neuropathy a real complication branch`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- neurological complication anchor
- 19 diabetic cats compared with 28 nondiabetic cats
- plantigrade posture and sensorimotor neuropathy framing
- Schwann-cell injury, demyelination, and severe-case axonal degeneration

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract reports physical/neurologic exams, electrophysiology, biochemical testing, and nerve/muscle biopsy analysis.
- Diabetic cats showed functional, structural, and biochemical defects.
- Plantigrade posture was the most notable severe impairment.
- Sensorimotor neuropathy affected pelvic and thoracic limbs.
- Schwann-cell injury and demyelination were prevalent; axonal degeneration appeared in severe cases.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Diabetic neuropathy is not only owner-visible gait abnormality; it has electrophysiologic and tissue-level correlates.
- Neuropathy should be represented in endpoint and complication pages.
- This source pairs with `src-diabetes-018` for nerve pathology and microvascular pathology.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not define routine screening for neuropathy in all diabetic cats.
- It does not establish reversibility after glycemic control.
