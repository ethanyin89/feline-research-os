| Problem | Context |
|---------|---------|
| Human HNSCC prognosis | Poor |
| Clinical advancement | Limited in recent years |

**Key insight:** Both species share environmental (tobacco) and viral (papillomavirus) risk factors, strengthening the model validity.

## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: Multi-Level Model Validation

FOSCC is validated as a HNSCC model at multiple levels:
1. **Etiologic:** Shared risk factors (tobacco, papillomavirus)
2. **Molecular:** Shared markers (EGFR, VEGF, p53)
3. **Clinical:** Similar behavior and outcomes

This multi-level correspondence strengthens the translational case.

### Theme B: Tobacco as Shared Risk Factor

The association with tobacco (secondhand smoke in cats) parallels human smoking-related HNSCC:
- Supports environmental etiology hypothesis
- May inform prevention messaging (keep cats away from smoke)
- Provides exposure-response research opportunity

### Theme C: Molecular Targets

The shared expression of EGFR and VEGF suggests:
- EGFR inhibitors (cetuximab, erlotinib) might be testable in cats
- Anti-VEGF therapy might be applicable
- p53 status may predict response

**Boundary:** These are research directions, not treatment recommendations.
