# FIP Mechanism-Diagnostic Bridge Memo

- Date: `2026-04-09`
- Scope: `src-fip-003`, `src-fip-004`, `src-fip-009`, `src-fip-010`, `src-fip-014`, `src-fip-018`, `src-fip-022`

This memo exists to stop the FIP module from confusing `why the disease exists` with `how diagnosis becomes operationally usable`.

## Core Position

1. Mechanism evidence should shape diagnostic interpretation, not replace diagnostic architecture.
2. The mechanism branch explains why simple enteric-coronavirus exposure is not enough.
3. The diagnostic branch still has to be led by clinicopathologic suspicion and bounded supportive strengthening.
4. The bridge should be modeled as `mechanism constrains interpretation`, not `mechanism creates certainty`.

## Why This Bridge Matters

FIP creates a recurring pressure inside the vault:

- mechanism papers are unusually central
- mutation papers can look diagnostically tempting
- treatment progress makes the disease feel more operationally settled than it really is

Without an explicit bridge, those three forces drift into one overcompressed story.

## Current Bridge Architecture

### Layer 1: Background And Emergence

This is where the mechanism branch establishes that:

- feline coronavirus background is not the same thing as overt FIP
- endemic enteric exposure is the background state, not the disease conclusion
- within-host emergence logic matters

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-fip-003`
- `src-fip-009`

### Layer 2: Functional Mechanism Shaping

This is where the mechanism branch becomes more clinically relevant without becoming diagnostic certainty:

- gut competence versus systemic disease competence
- systemic spread versus overt disease
- recombinant and outbreak logic that pressures single-route stories

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-fip-004`
- `src-fip-014`
- `src-fip-018`

### Layer 3: Diagnostic Interpretation Boundary

This is where the workup layer must stay disciplined:

- mechanism-origin does not equal mutation-test certainty
- spread-associated mutation signals do not settle who truly has FIP
- diagnostic mutation utility remains bounded and context-sensitive

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-fip-010`
- `src-fip-022`

## What The Bridge Actually Allows

### Stronger Reading

- mechanism explains why the module must resist flat enteric-coronavirus thinking
- mechanism explains why mutation-related support cannot be read in a vacuum
- mechanism supports a branch-structured workup rather than one-test simplification
- recombinant and spread-boundary logic justify keeping diagnostic ambiguity explicit

### Bounded Reading

- mechanism does not identify the sick cat by itself
- mutation-origin does not become clinical certainty by proximity
- systemic-spread logic does not collapse into overt-FIP proof
- even a strong mechanism spine still needs clinicopathology to lead suspicion

## Current Working Hierarchy

| Bridge Layer | Current Position In Vault | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Enteric background -> FIP emergence | core disease-context layer | explains why exposure is not diagnosis |
| Gut/systemic competence and spread boundary | interpretation-shaping mechanism layer | explains what mutation-associated signals may be tracking |
| Mutation-based supportive testing | bounded downstream diagnostic branch | belongs under workup, not above it |
| Clinicopathologic suspicion architecture | lead operational layer | still converts possibility into usable diagnostic action |

## What This Branch Is Not Allowed To Become

- It is not allowed to turn mechanism elegance into diagnostic certainty language.
- It is not allowed to demote clinicopathology beneath mutation storytelling.
- It is not allowed to treat spread-associated signals as proof of overt disease.
- It is not allowed to erase the recombinant branch just because the classical mutation-origin branch is strong.

## What The Vault Can Now Say More Clearly

1. FIP mechanism evidence matters diagnostically because it shapes what laboratory signals can and cannot mean.
2. The right bridge is `mechanism-informed diagnostic interpretation`, not `mechanism-led diagnosis`.
3. Mutation-associated evidence is most useful when read inside a wider branch architecture.
4. FIP is one of the clearest diseases in the vault where explanatory strength and diagnostic certainty must stay explicitly separated.
