## Theme: Early mineral disturbance may precede easier routine signals

The most important forward-looking point is that feline CKD-MBD may begin showing meaningful regulatory change before the classic azotaemic picture is obvious. This matches the vault's broader habit of distinguishing upstream disturbance from late operational signals.

### Hard Information

- FGF23 rises in advance of azotaemic CKD
- serum calcium relationships to nephrocalcinosis/nephrolithiasis remain limited

## Theme: CKD-MBD management is not a one-variable optimization problem

Dietary phosphate restriction remains a key intervention logic, but the review adds a real caution: some cats may develop hypercalcaemia in that context. That makes mineral management more like a balance problem than a single-target problem.

### Hard Information

- dietary phosphate restriction may contribute to hypercalcaemia in some cats
