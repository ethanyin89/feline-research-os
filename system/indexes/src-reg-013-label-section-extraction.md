# Label Section Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-reg-013`  
Title: `DailyMed Current Label: Senvelgo (velagliflozin oral solution)`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on DailyMed current-label section extraction, not clinical guideline synthesis.

## Core Takeaway

`Senvelgo label control is dominated by boxed DKA/euglycemic ketoacidosis warning, insulin-naive candidate selection, pre-treatment ketonuria screening, early ketonuria checks, and discontinuation-to-insulin transition gates`

## High-Value Label Sections

### Label Identity

- DailyMed lists Senvelgo as a prescription animal drug label.
- DailyMed label page was updated April 1, 2026.
- Senvelgo is a 15 mg/mL velagliflozin oral solution for oral use in cats only.
- It is an SGLT2 inhibitor.

### Indication / Candidate Boundary

- The indication is improved glycemic control in otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.
- This is the controlling indication phrase for the vault.
- Do not generalize to complicated, insulin-treated, or insulin-dependent diabetes.

### Dosing / Form Boundary

- The label describes 1 mg/kg once daily regardless of blood glucose level.
- The solution may be administered directly by mouth or with a small amount of wet food, but should not be mixed into food.
- This should be treated as label information, not patient-specific instruction.

### Pre-Treatment Screening

- Before treatment, the veterinarian should ensure the cat is alert, active, eating, and drinking.
- Pre-treatment workup includes physical examination, medical history, CBC, serum chemistry, serum fructosamine, and urinalysis including ketonuria evaluation.
- If treatment is delayed more than a week after diabetes diagnosis, the cat should be re-evaluated before starting.

### Monitoring And Discontinuation Gates

- Sudden hyporexia/anorexia, lethargy, dehydration, or weight loss should trigger discontinuation and DKA assessment regardless of blood glucose level.
- Ketonuria should be evaluated 2-3 days after initiation, around 7 days after initiation, and whenever illness signs occur.
- If ketonuria is present, discontinue Senvelgo and treat with insulin even if blood glucose is normal.
- During the first 4 weeks, physical examination, blood glucose curve, serum fructosamine, and body weight are assessed at 1 and 4 weeks.
- Poor glycemic control after 4 weeks should lead to discontinuation and consideration of insulin.
- Ongoing monitoring includes blood glucose, fructosamine, urinary ketones, serum chemistry, body weight, hydration status, and clinical signs.
- Increasing triglycerides/cholesterol, elevated fPL, unintentional weight loss, or illness signs route to further evaluation and possible discontinuation.

### Contraindications / Warnings

- Do not use in cats previously treated with insulin, receiving insulin, or with insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus.
- Do not initiate in cats with anorexia, dehydration, lethargy, ketonuria/ketonemia, suspected or historical DKA, recent pancreatitis suspicion, chronic/unresponsive diarrhea, cachexia, bilirubin above label threshold, or creatinine above label threshold.
- Evaluate for concurrent disease including pancreatitis, infectious disease, UTI, neoplasia, and hypersomatotropism before and during treatment.
- Euglycemia may persist for 2-3 days after stopping Senvelgo.

### Adverse-Reaction Signal

- The field study enrolled 252 cats and evaluated safety in 252 cats treated with at least one dose.
- Common adverse reactions included diarrhea/loose stool, weight loss, vomiting, polyuria, polydipsia, and elevated BUN.
- The adverse-reaction table reports DKA or euglycemic DKA in 18/252 cats and death/euthanasia in 13/252 cats.
- The label notes all but five DKA/euglycemic DKA cases occurred within 2 weeks of starting, and twelve were euglycemic DKA.
- Ketonuria occurred in 14/252 cats and all but one developed ketonuria within a week of starting; cats discontinued Senvelgo and transitioned to insulin.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- The Senvelgo branch should be label-controlled by early ketonuria monitoring and DKA/euglycemic DKA warning.
- Monitoring claims should remain product-specific because Senvelgo uses 2-3 day and 7 day ketonuria checks plus 1/4-week glycemic assessment.
- The label supports route/form facts but does not support route superiority.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not compare Senvelgo with Bexacat or insulin.
- It does not establish use in complicated diabetes.
- It does not create owner-facing treatment instructions.
