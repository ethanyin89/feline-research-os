# CKD Treatment Product Archetype Memo

Date: 2026-04-08  
Scope: current treatment-ranking layer plus route-level regulatory framing

This memo converts the current feline CKD intervention map into first-pass product archetypes.

## What This Memo Is For

The treatment-ranking memo answers:

- which intervention classes are more supportable

This memo answers a different question:

- if one of those classes had to become a real asset, what kind of product would it actually be

This is the bridge between:

- treatment logic
- evidence package logic
- regulatory path logic

This page is an English working memo.
It is not a bilingual summary and it should not be read as a final asset-selection recommendation.

## Current Archetype Set

The current vault supports thinking in six first-pass archetypes.

## Archetype 1: Renal Diet As Foundational Product

### What it is

- purpose-built feline renal diet
- nutrition-positioned baseline management product

### What claim logic it fits best

- supportive management
- progression-related burden reduction
- phosphorus-control contribution

### Why it matters

- this is currently the most supportable treatment archetype in the vault
- it already sits at Tier 1 in the treatment-ranking memo

### Main strength

- strongest current combination of practical use and supportability

### Main weakness

- hard to convert into a strong “disease modification” claim without overclaim

## Archetype 2: Phosphorus-Control Adjunct

### What it is

- phosphate-restricted diet extension
- intestinal phosphate binder add-on

### What claim logic it fits best

- phosphorus lowering
- progression-risk reduction logic
- CKD-MBD burden management

### Why it matters

- phosphorus is one of the clearest bridge variables in the whole vault
- this archetype sits close to the center of mechanism, endpoint, and treatment layers

### Main strength

- clear endpoint logic

### Main weakness

- current corpus is stronger for the diet branch than for binder-specific product proof

## Archetype 3: Hemodynamic Management Product

### What it is

- antihypertensive product
- CKD plus hypertension management asset

### What claim logic it fits best

- blood pressure control
- target-organ-damage risk reduction
- management of a high-risk CKD subgroup

### Why it matters

- systolic blood pressure is unquestionably first-wave in this vault
- CKD with hypertension is already a distinct clinical management fork

### Main strength

- clear subgroup and clear operational endpoint

### Main weakness

- broader renal-outcome claims remain more fragile than blood-pressure claims

## Archetype 4: Proteinuria-Oriented Renoprotective Adjunct

### What it is

- ACE inhibitor / RAAS-oriented proteinuria-management product
- possible telmisartan-like positioning logic at the archetype level

### What claim logic it fits best

- proteinuria reduction
- risk-context improvement
- adjunctive renoprotective positioning

### Why it matters

- proteinuria is now a core bridge variable and a pathology-aware signal

### Main strength

- coherent endpoint and subgroup logic

### Main weakness

- current vault supports management relevance more strongly than hard survival or disease-modification claims

## Archetype 5: Supportive Symptom / Complication Package

### What it is

- symptomatic supportive-care asset
- anaemia-directed support
- GI- and appetite-oriented support
- hydration-support logic

### What claim logic it fits best

- quality of life
- clinical stability
- complication burden reduction

### Why it matters

- some of the most clinically common CKD management actions live here

### Main strength

- aligns well with trial-outcome themes like quality of life and food intake

### Main weakness

- this archetype is heterogeneous and easy to oversimplify
- some branches, such as long-term subcutaneous fluids, have weak evidence in the current corpus

## Archetype 6: Next-Generation Disease-Modifying Branch

### What it is

- MR antagonist strategy
- other mechanism-led renal-injury attenuation concepts

### What claim logic it fits best

- renal-injury attenuation
- fibrosis-related burden reduction
- disease-modification watchlist logic

### Why it matters

- this is where the current vault starts to point beyond standard supportive CKD care

### Main strength

- translational interest is real

### Main weakness

- current support is still more mechanistic than feline efficacy-grade

## Best Archetypes By Current Goal Type

| Goal Type | Best-Fit Archetype Right Now | Why |
|---|---|---|
| baseline CKD management | renal diet | strongest current supportability |
| progression-linked biochemical control | phosphorus-control adjunct | phosphorus is one of the strongest bridge endpoints |
| subgroup management | hemodynamic management product | hypertension gives a clear treatment fork |
| renoprotective adjunct positioning | proteinuria-oriented renoprotective adjunct | proteinuria is central but still overclaim-sensitive |
| quality-of-life support | supportive symptom / complication package | best fit for broader trial-outcome framing |
| future disease modification | next-generation disease-modifying branch | promising, but still watchlist-stage |

## Archetypes Most Ready For Route-Level Regulatory Thinking

### Highest readiness

- renal diet as a foundational product
- phosphorus-control adjunct
- hemodynamic management product

Reason:

- each has clearer immediate-use logic and clearer operational readouts

### Medium readiness

- proteinuria-oriented renoprotective adjunct
- supportive symptom / complication package

Reason:

- clinically important, but claim wording and product definition are more variable

### Low readiness

- next-generation disease-modifying branch

Reason:

- current problem is not route choice first
- current problem is proof depth first

## Where Product-Archetype Clarity Still Breaks Down

1. some intervention classes are still too broad
2. product-specific evidence has not yet been separated from class logic
3. route-level regulatory framing exists, but archetype-to-route mapping is still first-pass only
4. nutritional, pharmaceutical, and adjunctive-support assets still need clearer boundaries in later memos
5. this memo still compresses multiple product questions into archetype-level buckets

## What This Memo Now Lets The Vault Say More Clearly

1. Not every CKD intervention class is the same kind of product problem.
2. Renal diet and phosphorus-control assets are currently the cleanest archetypes.
3. Hypertension and proteinuria branches are more viable as subgroup or adjunct products than as broad disease-reversal claims.
4. Supportive-care packages may be easier to justify on quality-of-life logic than on structural-disease logic.
5. Mechanism-led assets remain future-facing until evidence depth improves.
