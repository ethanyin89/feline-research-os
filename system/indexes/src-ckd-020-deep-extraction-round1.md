- core_claim: standard B-mode ultrasonography cannot reliably distinguish benign from malignant focal lesions.
- implicit_premise: imaging realism requires preserving uncertainty, not just listing modality strengths.

- tension_or_surprise: even when lesions are visible, diagnostic certainty is still conditional.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Ultrasound belongs in the workup layer, not the core endpoint layer

This review is most useful for clarifying that imaging is a real part of renal assessment, but not in the same role as creatinine, USG, blood pressure, or phosphorus. Ultrasound helps contextualize structure and differential diagnosis rather than serving as a default CKD severity endpoint.

### Hard Information

- reference imaging modality
- more accurate than radiography
- accessible and real-time

## Theme: Diffuse CKD remains harder to characterize than focal renal disease

The most important boundary is that diffuse renal changes are harder to read than focal disease. That means ultrasound should improve realism and completeness, but should not be sold as a standalone solution to feline CKD interpretation.

### Hard Information

- focal/multifocal easier
- diffuse changes harder
- B-mode limited for lesion typing
