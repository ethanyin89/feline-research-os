# Diabetes Endocrine-Secondary Diabetes Memo

- Date: `2026-04-24`
- Scope: `src-diabetes-013`, `src-diabetes-020`, with treatment interpretation pressure from `src-diabetes-024`

This memo exists to keep feline diabetes from being flattened into one default type-2-like pathway.

## Core Takeaway

`type-2-like feline diabetes is the default frame, not the universal explanation; hypersomatotropism and other endocrinopathies need a named secondary-diabetes gate`

## Why This Branch Exists

`src-diabetes-013` makes the structural point:

- most diabetic cats may fit a type-2-like frame
- a significant proportion have other specific diabetes types
- hypersomatotropism, acromegaly, and hyperadrenocorticism are named endocrine causes
- this branch affects presentation, diagnosis, and therapy

`src-diabetes-020` updates the branch:

- diabetes is framed as the second-most common feline endocrinopathy
- about 1/200 cats are affected by diabetes in the current compiled statement
- around 15-25% of diabetic cats are framed as secondary to growth-hormone-induced insulin resistance
- HST-associated diabetes is challenging to manage, with variable insulin response or high-dose needs
- IGF1 is the diagnostic test of choice, but marginal values and chronic insulin exposure complicate interpretation

## Current Gate

Endocrine-secondary diabetes should be considered a named branch when the module discusses:

- difficult glycemic control
- high insulin dose requirements
- variable insulin response
- unexpected non-remission
- suspected acromegalic phenotype
- treatment studies that exclude suspected hypersomatotropism cases

## Why It Changes Treatment Interpretation

`src-diabetes-024` excluded four cats suspected of hypersomatotropism from insulin efficacy evaluation.

That matters because it shows secondary endocrine disease can alter how treatment evidence should be read.

The current rule is:

`do not interpret insulin or diet response without checking whether secondary endocrine disease has been excluded, included, or ignored`

## What The Module Can Say Now

- endocrine-secondary diabetes is a cross-cutting branch, not a minor comorbidity footnote
- hypersomatotropism-induced diabetes is the highest-priority named endocrine-secondary branch in the current seed set
- recognition, mechanism, endpoint, and translation pages should all preserve this branch
- IGF1 testing is important but not always cleanly decisive from the current compiled layer alone

## What The Module Should Not Say Yet

- do not provide a full screening algorithm from the current compiled layer alone
- do not make exact prevalence claims without full-text confirmation
- do not claim a treatment hierarchy for hypersomatotropism-induced diabetes
- do not treat marginal IGF1 interpretation as solved
