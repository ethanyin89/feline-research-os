# CKD Round 2 Tier A Synthesis Memo

Date: 2026-04-08  
Scope: `src-ckd-024`, `src-ckd-013`, `src-ckd-022`, `src-ckd-017`, `src-ckd-016`

This memo compresses the first Tier A Round 2 reading cluster into one working-state summary.

## What This Cluster Changed

Round 2 Tier A did not rebuild the whole CKD vault.

It strengthened five previously thin branches:

1. SDMA and biomarker positioning
2. treatment-trial outcome logic
3. explicit experimental-model evidence
4. proteinuria / pathology bridge
5. older-cat morphology / pathogenesis framing

## Paper-By-Paper Value

### `src-ckd-024`

This review made SDMA language more defensible.

- creatinine remains limited for early CKD detection
- SDMA is a meaningful GFR-related biomarker
- specificity remains limited
- GFR remains a reference-standard concept more than a routine workflow marker
- UPC stays useful, but context-sensitive

Net effect:

- the vault can now position SDMA more clearly as `useful adjunctive biomarker`
- the vault still should not present SDMA as a solved screening shortcut

### `src-ckd-013`

This paper upgraded the translation layer from endpoint discussion to trial-outcome logic.

- feline CKD treatment trials lacked agreement on minimum outcomes
- the final core outcome set contains 29 parameters in 9 themes
- trial logic must include quality of life, food intake, CKD progression, survival time, and cause of death

Net effect:

- the vault now distinguishes `routine operational endpoints` from `minimum trial outcomes`

### `src-ckd-022`

This paper gave the model layer one real experimental anchor.

- unilateral 90-minute ischemic event
- 6-month follow-up
- persistent functional decline plus chronic structural lesions
- lesion pattern includes fibrosis, tubular atrophy, inflammation, and glomerular change

Net effect:

- model layer is no longer purely observational
- but it is still thin and should remain labeled `natural-disease dominant with one bounded experimental anchor`

### `src-ckd-017`

This paper made proteinuria interpretation more compartment-aware.

- proteinuric cats often had glomerular disease
- ICGN was common in this subset
- UPC magnitude varied across glomerular disease categories

Net effect:

- UPCR is not just a substaging number
- proteinuria can point toward distinct disease compartments, not only generic CKD severity

### `src-ckd-016`

This review strengthened the aged-cat disease frame.

- feline CKD is primarily a geriatric disease
- typical lesion pattern is tubulointerstitial and fibrosis-centered
- primary glomerulopathies with marked proteinuria are rare in the broad aged-cat frame
- aging and ischemia fit better as initiation-side logic
- phosphorus, proteinuria, and anemia fit better as progression-side logic

Net effect:

- older-cat recognition is now more tightly linked to pathogenesis and morphology
- the fibrosis-centered backbone is now better grounded as an aged-cat natural-history story

## Cross-Cluster Conclusions

### 1. The endpoint layer is now more stratified

The vault should now think in at least three endpoint classes:

- operational CKD monitoring markers
- bridge variables that connect structure, progression, and treatment
- broader trial-grade outcomes

### 2. The model layer is better, but still not dense

The strongest accurate wording now is:

`natural disease and clinicopathology dominant, with one explicit ischemic experimental anchor`

### 3. Proteinuria sits closer to the center of interpretation

The vault should treat proteinuria as:

- a progression-relevant bridge variable in broad CKD
- a possible compartment signal in proteinuric subsets

### 4. Older-cat surveillance is now more than a screening heuristic

The aged-cat frame is no longer only about “watch older cats more carefully.”

It is now connected to:

- lesion identity
- pathogenesis framing
- initiation versus progression logic
