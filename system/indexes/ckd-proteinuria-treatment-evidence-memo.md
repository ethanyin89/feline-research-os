# CKD Proteinuria Treatment Evidence Memo

- Date: `2026-04-09`
- Scope: `src-ckd-003`, `src-ckd-007`, `src-ckd-017`, `src-ckd-004`, `src-ckd-005`, `src-ckd-009`, `src-ckd-010`

This memo is not here to prove that one renoprotective strategy has already won in feline CKD.
It is here to do four narrower things:

1. clarify why proteinuria matters in treatment reasoning
2. separate proteinuria as endpoint from proteinuria as disease-compartment signal
3. bound what ACE inhibitor / RAAS-oriented evidence can actually support
4. prevent overclaim driven by routine use, glomerular pathology, or human-CKD carryover

## Core Position

1. Proteinuria belongs near the center of feline CKD interpretation because it connects progression, blood-pressure logic, pathology correlation, and treatment reasoning.
2. Proteinuria is not one thing. In some cats, especially in the protein-losing nephropathy subset, it also points toward primary glomerular disease rather than generic diffuse CKD.
3. Proteinuria-oriented treatment logic is real and clinically meaningful.
4. Current feline evidence still does not justify broad survival-level or disease-modification overclaim for RAAS-oriented therapy.

## Why Proteinuria Matters More Than A Substage Label

Proteinuria is one of the few feline CKD variables that keeps reappearing across otherwise separate layers:

- it matters in screening and early recognition
- it matters in hypertension/comorbidity logic
- it matters in pathology correlation
- it matters in treatment selection and monitoring

That makes it more than a substage descriptor.
In the current vault, proteinuria behaves like a bridge variable between disease structure and management decisions.

## What `src-ckd-017` Changed

`src-ckd-017` tightened this branch in an important way because it forced the vault to stop treating all feline proteinuria as one clinical story.

What it added:

- this is a proteinuric / protein-losing nephropathy subset, not a generic CKD population
- `58%` of proteinuric cats had ICGN
- `74%` of protein-losing nephropathy cats had ICGN
- median UPC was higher in non-ICGN glomerular disease than in ICGN (`14.5` vs `6.5`)

What that means for the treatment layer:

- proteinuria is not only a progression marker
- in some cats it is also a pathology-compartment clue
- very high UPC values increase suspicion, but do not eliminate heterogeneity

So the treatment branch has to stay bounded.
Proteinuria-oriented management is real, but “proteinuria therefore diffuse CKD therefore broad renoprotective success” is not a defensible shortcut.

## What The Current Treatment Sources Actually Support

### Stronger Support

- proteinuria matters enough to affect treatment and monitoring logic
- proteinuria is tied to blood-pressure and progression reasoning
- RAAS-oriented management has genuine clinical relevance in the feline literature and in practice

### Bounded Support

- management value is clearer than survival benefit
- `src-ckd-003` gives a limited positive signal for benazepril through appetite improvement
- the same source does not justify a survival-level efficacy claim

### What This Means

Proteinuria-oriented treatment belongs in the CKD map.
It does not yet belong in a “broad renoprotection proven” bucket.

## What This Branch Is Not Allowed To Become

- It is not allowed to become a blanket survival claim for ACE inhibitor therapy.
- It is not allowed to become a claim that all proteinuric cats sit in the same disease compartment.
- It is not allowed to import human CKD renoprotection language without feline evidence support.
- It is not allowed to confuse common field use with proof depth.

## What The Vault Can Now Say More Clearly

1. Proteinuria matters because it sits at the junction of endpoint logic, pathology logic, and treatment logic.
2. Proteinuria-oriented management is clinically meaningful and belongs in Tier 2 treatment reasoning.
3. The best current support is for management relevance, not broad survival benefit.
4. `src-ckd-017` makes the glomerular-disease subset impossible to ignore in proteinuric cats.
5. UPC is useful and sometimes highly informative, but it is not a one-number pathology classifier.

## What We Should Not Overstate

- We should not write proteinuria-oriented therapy as proven disease modification.
- We should not write ACE inhibitor use as if routine adoption resolved the evidence question.
- We should not collapse proteinuric feline CKD into a single pathology story.
- We should not use glomerular subset findings to overgeneralize the entire CKD population.
