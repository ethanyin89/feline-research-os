Feline CKD is currently best read as a fibrosis-centered, naturally occurring chronic disease. Diagnosis and earlier recognition depend on serial surveillance rather than one single elegant biomarker, and phosphorus, proteinuria, and blood pressure matter disproportionately because they bridge structural injury, monitoring, and treatment reasoning.
