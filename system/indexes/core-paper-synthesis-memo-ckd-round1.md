# Core Paper Synthesis Memo

Scope: `src-ckd-004`, `src-ckd-010`, `src-ckd-011`  
Purpose: compress the three highest-value deep extractions into one reusable internal memo.

## What These Three Papers Jointly Establish

Together, these three papers provide the current vault's strongest internal backbone for feline CKD.

- `src-ckd-004` gives the operational clinical framework
- `src-ckd-010` gives the primary-study clinicopathology bridge
- `src-ckd-011` gives the lesion-and-mediator mechanism frame

That means they are not redundant.
They answer different layers of the same question:

1. how CKD is recognized and managed in practice
2. which routine markers actually map to structural renal injury
3. why fibrosis is the most defensible mechanism anchor, and how far mediator claims can safely go

## Core Theme 1: Fibrosis Is The Current Backbone

The strongest shared conclusion across the three papers is that `renal tubulointerstitial fibrosis` is the most stable organizing lesion for feline CKD in the current vault.

- `src-ckd-011` argues directly that fibrosis is the most frequently reported pathological diagnosis and the lesion best correlated with function.
- `src-ckd-010` gives the primary-study anchor showing that interstitial fibrosis is the lesion best correlated with azotemia, hyperphosphatemia, and anaemia.
- `src-ckd-004` does not foreground fibrosis mechanistically, but its diagnostic and management framework is compatible with a disease that must be tracked through persistent dysfunction and repeated reassessment rather than one-off acute events.

### What We Can Safely Say

- fibrosis is the best current lesion-level backbone for feline CKD
- fibrosis is strong enough to organize mechanism, endpoint, and translation pages

### What We Should Not Overstate

- fibrosis alone explains all initiating causes of feline CKD
- mediator-level claims are as strong as lesion-level claims

## Core Theme 2: CKD Should Be Read Through Multiple Marker Axes, Not One Kidney Number

These papers jointly reject a flattened “single kidney marker” mindset.

- `src-ckd-004` shows that diagnosis and monitoring depend on a composite of creatinine, USG, UPCR, SBP, imaging, and serial change.
- `src-ckd-010` shows that different markers trace different structural lesion patterns:
  - fibrosis aligns with azotemia, hyperphosphatemia, and anaemia
  - proteinuria aligns with fibrosis and glomerular hypertrophy
  - time-averaged systolic blood pressure aligns with glomerulosclerosis and hyperplastic arteriolosclerosis
- `src-ckd-011` reinforces that phosphate and proteinuria belong inside the mechanistic story, not only in treatment summaries.

### What We Can Safely Say

- phosphorus, proteinuria, and blood pressure are bridge variables
- clinical markers in feline CKD do not all represent the same lesion axis
- endpoint interpretation should remain multi-axis

### What We Should Not Overstate

- one marker can substitute for the whole renal workup
- pathology-linked markers automatically become the best early-screening markers

## Core Theme 3: Early Detection Is A Serial-Surveillance Problem

`src-ckd-004` is the strongest operational paper here, and it makes one thing clear:
early detection is not solved by a single elegant biomarker.

- older cats should be followed repeatedly
- serial change matters
- classic diagnostic signals are still central
- advanced tools such as SDMA are promising but bounded

This theme is not the main point of `src-ckd-010` or `src-ckd-011`, but neither paper contradicts it.
Instead, they make the surveillance logic more understandable by showing why structure and function may diverge or only partially overlap.

### What We Can Safely Say

- early CKD recognition should be framed as repeated surveillance in older/high-risk cats
- SDMA is useful as an adjunct, not a one-test shortcut

### What We Should Not Overstate

- SDMA replaces creatinine/USG interpretation
- the vault already contains a complete screening algorithm

## Core Theme 4: Treatment Logic Is Uneven, But Phosphorus Sits Surprisingly High

`src-ckd-004` and `src-ckd-011` together help explain why phosphorus keeps rising in importance across the vault.

- the guideline gives renal diet and phosphate control unusually high practical priority
- the fibrosis review keeps phosphate inside the injury/progression story
- the pathology study links hyperphosphatemia to fibrosis severity

This is why phosphorus has become one of the strongest bridge endpoints in the vault.

### What We Can Safely Say

- phosphorus belongs in mechanism, endpoint, and treatment layers simultaneously
- phosphate control is more central than a casual CKD summary would suggest

### What We Should Not Overstate

- detailed product-specific phosphate-binder hierarchy from the current corpus
- direct proof that every phosphorus intervention modifies structural disease

## Core Theme 5: Mediator-Level Expansion Is Real, But Still Capped

`src-ckd-011` is the key paper here.

It supports a next-step move from `fibrosis` to `TGF-beta`, but it also imposes a ceiling:
only a few fibrosis mediators have actually been investigated in cats.

That means the vault can now say:

- TGF-beta is the strongest current mediator-level expansion target
- TGF-beta is not yet a fully validated feline CKD intervention target in this corpus

This distinction is important because it protects the vault from over-importing human or rodent fibrosis narratives.

## Most Reusable Cross-Layer Conclusions

1. `Fibrosis` is the most defensible current lesion backbone.
2. `Phosphorus`, `proteinuria`, and `SBP` are bridge variables, not minor monitoring extras.
3. `Early detection` should currently be framed as serial surveillance, not one-test screening.
4. `SDMA` is promising but bounded.
5. `TGF-beta` is the first serious mediator-level expansion target, but still sits below fibrosis in certainty.

## Current Safe Summary

If someone asked what the current best-supported internal view of feline CKD is, these three papers together support the following answer:

feline CKD is best read as a fibrosis-centered, naturally occurring chronic disease in which diagnosis and early recognition depend on serial surveillance, and in which phosphorus, proteinuria, and blood pressure are unusually valuable because they bridge structural injury, practical monitoring, and treatment reasoning.
