## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Cellular immunity is now a first-class FCV endpoint branch

This paper upgrades cellular cross-immunity from a speculative add-on into a real FCV vaccine endpoint family. It gives the module a cleaner explanation for why some vaccine benefit can coexist with limited cross-neutralising titres.

### Hard Information

- cellular but not humoral cross-immunity in the reported setting
- Th1 cytokine responses
- IFN-gamma-releasing PBMC responses

## Theme: Neutralisation breadth is necessary but not sufficient

This paper does not replace breadth papers. It complements them by explaining why humoral assays alone may underdescribe vaccine benefit.

### Hard Information

- heterologous current field-strain challenge setting
- stronger cellular than humoral cross-immunity signal

## Theme: Vaccine interpretation needs endpoint separation

The study is most useful when placed inside a hierarchy: challenge protection, neutralisation breadth, cellular immunity, and persistence control are related but not interchangeable.

### Hard Information

- experimental challenge design
- cellular-immunity readouts
- heterologous field-strain framing
