# src-ibd-017 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`fecal S100A12 is a noninvasive disease-versus-health marker, not a boundary discriminator`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first stool-based inflammatory support-marker anchor in the IBD module
- support for a noninvasive signal that detects abnormal intestinal disease burden
- a reminder that not every useful noninvasive marker separates IBD from lymphoma

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 49 cats with chronic enteropathy and 19 healthy control cats.
- Nineteen chronic enteropathy cats were diagnosed with IBD or chronic inflammatory enteropathy, and 30 were diagnosed with alimentary lymphoma.
- Fecal S100A12 concentrations differed between lymphoma and controls and between IBD and controls.
- No significant difference was found between IBD and lymphoma.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Fecal S100A12 belongs in a noninvasive support-marker layer for chronic enteropathy.
- Its best current role is disease-versus-health inflammatory support, not IBD-versus-lymphoma separation.
- Noninvasive marker branches should be layered below tissue-centered workup and below frontier metabolomic separation claims.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove fecal S100A12 can separate IBD from lymphoma.
- It does not replace biopsy, imaging, or pathology review.
- It does not yet establish a definitive monitoring threshold for routine care.
