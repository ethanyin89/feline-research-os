# CKD Uremic-Toxin and Microbiome Bridge Memo

## Decision

The current feline evidence supports a `research branch`, not a probiotic treatment recommendation.

`src-ckd-027` establishes that feline CKD is associated with broad metabolic disruption and higher circulating gut-derived uremic toxins. `src-ckd-030` tests one microbiota-directed intervention, but its six-cat completer analysis does not demonstrate significant toxin reduction or controlled kidney benefit.

## Evidence Bridge

| Question | `src-ckd-027` | `src-ckd-030` | Current safe read |
|---|---|---|---|
| Are gut-derived uremic toxins altered in feline CKD? | Yes; observational CKD/healthy comparison with 94/84 cats | Baseline and post-treatment toxin assays in six completers | The toxin branch is real as a disease-associated research signal. |
| Is impaired excretion the only mechanism? | Likely central, but altered microbial production remains possible | Microbiome and predicted functions changed after treats | Production-versus-retention causality remains unresolved. |
| Does microbiome treatment lower toxins? | Not tested | TMAO, IS, PCS, and PS changes were not statistically significant | No demonstrated feline toxin-lowering efficacy. |
| Does the intervention improve CKD? | Not tested | Single-arm creatinine shift, `p = 0.06`; one apparent stage change | Hypothesis-generating only; uncontrolled clinical benefit cannot be inferred. |
| What can control future trial design? | Metabolite pathways and candidate toxin panel | Attrition, palatability, phosphate increase, paired microbiome/toxin endpoints | Use as endpoint and failure-mode input for a controlled trial. |

## Branch Architecture

1. **Disease association:** tryptophan, tyrosine, carnitine, urea-cycle, and gut-derived toxin changes are associated with feline CKD.
2. **Causal uncertainty:** renal retention, diet, microbial production, medications, and disease severity remain entangled.
3. **Intervention hypothesis:** selected microbiota-directed strategies may alter microbial composition.
4. **Unmet proof:** controlled toxin reduction, renal outcome benefit, durability, safety, and generalizability have not been established.

## Trial-Design Requirements

- randomized concurrent control or placebo
- prespecified primary endpoint
- adequate sample size and attrition handling
- standardized or stratified diet and concurrent CKD therapy
- absolute toxin quantification
- creatinine plus SDMA, phosphorus, UPC, body weight, and validated quality-of-life measures
- adherence, palatability, and adverse-event accounting for all enrolled cats
- follow-up long enough to distinguish transient biomarker movement from disease modification

## Guardrail

Do not compress this branch into:

- “probiotics treat feline CKD”
- “microbiome modulation lowers uremic toxins in cats”
- “these treats improve CKD stage”
- “dietary protein causes feline CKD”

The evidence currently justifies a controlled research program, not owner-facing guidance.
