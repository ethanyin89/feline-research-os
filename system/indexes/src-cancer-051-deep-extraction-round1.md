# Deep Extraction Worksheet: src-cancer-051

**Source:** Development of a questionnaire assessing health-related quality-of-life in dogs and cats with cancer
**Journal:** Veterinary and Comparative Oncology (2011)
**DOI:** 10.1111/j.1476-5829.2010.00244.x
**PMID:** 21848620
**Evidence Level:** original-study (instrument development)

## Phase 0: Context

**Access status:** Abstract available from PubMed.

**Source scope:** 2011 instrument development study for health-related quality-of-life (HRQoL) assessment in canine and feline oncology patients.

**Key contribution:** Validated questionnaire tools for clinical oncology QoL assessment.

**Critical boundary:** Mixed dog/cat study; not feline-specific validation.

## Phase 1: Sequential Micro-Analysis

### 1.1 Study Design

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Setting | Veterinary oncology referral service |
| Species | Dogs and cats with cancer |
| Respondents | Pet owners and attending clinicians |
| Goal | Assess practicality and robustness of HRQoL tools |

### 1.2 Instruments Developed

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Cancer Treatment Form | Comprehensive QoL assessment during treatment |
| Owner Minitest | Quick owner-completed evaluation |
| Clinician Minitest | Quick clinician assessment |

### 1.3 Validation Results

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Owner agreement | 98% (82/84) |
| Clinician reception | Well received |
| Accuracy perception | Form accurately reflected pet's QoL |

**Key finding:** High owner concordance suggests face validity for clinical use.

### 1.4 Limitations

| Limitation | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Mixed species | Cannot separate dog vs cat performance |
| Referral setting | May not generalize to primary care |
| Minor improvements needed | Not final instrument |

## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: QoL Assessment Tools Available

Practical HRQoL questionnaires exist:
- Designed for oncology context
- Owner and clinician versions
- High acceptance and perceived accuracy
- Useful for treatment decision support

### Theme B: Owner-Clinician Agreement

Dual-respondent design:
- Captures both perspectives
- Owner knows daily behavior
- Clinician knows disease trajectory
- Together provide fuller picture

### Theme C: Clinical Workflow Integration

Tools designed for practical use:
- Referral oncology setting
- Treatment monitoring context
- Both quick (minitest) and comprehensive options
- Can guide treatment decisions

**Boundary:** Not disease-specific (applies across cancer types).
