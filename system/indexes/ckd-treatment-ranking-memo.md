# CKD Treatment Ranking Memo

Date: 2026-04-09  
Scope: `src-ckd-003`, `src-ckd-006`, `src-ckd-007`, `src-ckd-009`, `src-ckd-013`, `src-ckd-014`, `src-ckd-017`, `src-ckd-021`

This memo compresses the current feline CKD treatment-oriented corpus into one intervention hierarchy.

## What This Memo Is For

This page does not claim that every intervention has been settled by strong feline primary-study evidence.

It does something narrower and more useful:

- rank what is most supportable in the current vault
- separate baseline-supported care from context-dependent adjuncts
- separate translational watchlist branches from routine management
- keep implementation realism separate from efficacy ranking

This page is an English working memo.
It is not a bilingual summary and it is not a decision-grade treatment recommendation.

## Current Ranking Logic

The current hierarchy is driven by four questions:

1. does the intervention have repeated feline support
2. does it connect directly to progression-relevant endpoints
3. does the current corpus support outcome-level claims, not only mechanistic plausibility
4. is it easy to overclaim because it is common in practice

## Tier 1: Strongest Current Support

### Renal diet

- strongest current intervention anchor in the vault
- repeatedly supported across treatment-oriented sources
- tied to phosphorus control, clinical outcome framing, and survival-oriented logic

Supported use:

- renal diet is the clearest baseline-supported feline CKD intervention in the current system

Do not overclaim:

- do not overstate renal diet as proven structural disease reversal

### Phosphorus-focused dietary control

- phosphorus is one of the strongest bridge variables across mechanism, endpoint, and treatment layers
- stage 2-3 cats have meaningful support for phosphate-restricted diet
- phosphorus burden is tied to progression and secondary hyperparathyroidism logic

Supported use:

- phosphorus control is not just biochemical housekeeping; it is a central treatment logic branch

Do not overclaim:

- do not flatten phosphate binders and diet into one evidence tier

## Tier 2: Important, But Context-Dependent

### Antihypertensive therapy

- strong clinical importance when CKD and hypertension coexist
- systolic blood pressure is first-wave, not optional
- the intervention logic is solid, but broader outcome claims still need careful wording

Supported use:

- use when systemic hypertension is confirmed and target-organ risk is relevant

Do not overclaim:

- do not present antihypertensive therapy as broad disease modification across all CKD cats

### Proteinuria-oriented RAAS strategy

- ACE inhibitor or related RAAS-oriented management has real relevance when proteinuria is present
- current vault supports management value more clearly than survival benefit
- the new bounded evidence memo makes it clearer that this branch is partly an endpoint branch and partly a pathology-sensitive subset branch
- easy to overclaim because routine use is broader than proof

Supported use:

- proteinuria-oriented RAAS management belongs in Tier 2 because proteinuria matters clinically, but the safest current wording is still management relevance rather than broad renoprotective proof

Do not overclaim:

- do not write this branch as if routine ACE inhibitor use had already resolved survival benefit or disease-modification uncertainty across feline CKD

### Potassium supplementation

- important when hypokalaemia is present
- clinically meaningful supportive intervention
- not a top-tier disease-modification claim in the current corpus

Supported use:

- correct documented hypokalaemia as a complication-directed intervention

Do not overclaim:

- do not rank potassium supplementation as a general CKD disease-modifying therapy

### Symptomatic and complication-directed support

- uraemic gastroenteritis management
- anaemia-directed support
- assisted feeding and broader supportive care logic

Supported use:

- these interventions matter for quality of life and late-stage stability

Do not overclaim:

- do not present symptom control as equivalent to progression reversal

## Tier 3: Operationally Relevant, But Evidence Thinner Or More Conditional

### Intestinal phosphate binders

- clearly relevant in practice
- evidence is thinner than for diet
- no licensed veterinary phosphate binder medications were identified in the reviewed source set

Supported use:

- consider when diet alone does not control phosphorus burden

Do not overclaim:

- do not treat binder evidence as equivalent to the survival-linked renal-diet branch

### Long-term subcutaneous fluid therapy

- common in practice
- explicitly weak grade IV evidence in the current evidence-based review

Supported use:

- use as individualized supportive care when hydration needs and tolerance justify it

Do not overclaim:

- do not present routine long-term fluids as strongly proven to slow CKD progression

### Screening and early-treatment package

- strategically important
- but still better supported as earlier recognition logic than as a mature intervention package

Supported use:

- use earlier recognition to trigger complete staging and management review

Do not overclaim:

- do not describe screening itself as a validated treatment package or outcome benefit

Net effect:

- these branches should stay in the treatment map, but below the most defensible baseline interventions

## Tier 4: Watchlist / Future-Oriented Branches

### Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist strategy

- biologically plausible
- mechanism-to-target logic is credible
- current strongest support comes from models and human CKD rather than direct feline efficacy evidence

Supported use:

- retain as a translational research and trial-design watchlist branch

Do not overclaim:

- do not recommend MR antagonists as established feline CKD therapy

### Other future disease-modification branches

- anything framed mainly through mediator plausibility rather than direct feline intervention evidence should stay here for now

Supported use:

- use mechanistic plausibility to prioritize research questions and evidence intake

Do not overclaim:

- do not convert pathway plausibility into clinical treatment recommendations

Net effect:

- watchlist does not mean unimportant
- it means translationally interesting but not yet first-wave recommendation territory

## What Changes Ranking The Most

### Strong upward pressure

- direct feline support
- repeated relevance across progression-linked endpoints
- outcome-oriented benefit, not only lab movement

### Strong downward pressure

- common use without strong evidence
- endpoint improvement without clear patient-level consequence
- pathway plausibility without feline efficacy confirmation

## Implementation Modifiers

These factors do not change evidence rank, but they change real-world execution:

- blood pressure measurement is still underperformed in practice
- diet recommendation does not guarantee adherence
- owner constraints change follow-up cadence
- concurrent hyperthyroidism can distort interpretation timing

So the right mental model is:

`evidence rank` is one layer  
`implementation friction` is another layer

They should not be collapsed into one score.

## What This Memo Now Lets The Vault Say More Clearly

1. Feline CKD treatment evidence is not flat.
2. Renal diet and phosphorus control sit above most other intervention branches in the current vault.
3. Blood pressure and proteinuria matter because they support management forks, not only monitoring.
4. Many widely used therapies still require explicit evidence-strength labeling.
5. Trial-grade outcome logic is ahead of intervention-grade certainty for several branches.
6. Implementation friction should modify execution plans, not inflate efficacy claims.
7. Proteinuria-oriented treatment now has a dedicated bounded-evidence memo, so this branch no longer has to be carried only by one ranking line.

## Main Remaining Gaps

- intervention-by-intervention feline primary-study extraction is still incomplete
- product archetype and product-specific evidence remain underdeveloped
- no submission-grade treatment package strategy yet
- treatment ranking is now compiled, but not yet linked to regulatory route logic
- this memo is still a ranking layer, not a final treatment decision layer
