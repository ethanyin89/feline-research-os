- core_claim: the proposed outcome set is built through a structured process involving literature review plus Delphi-style consensus and an in-person consensus meeting.
- implicit_premise: trial outcome selection should include multiple stakeholder perspectives rather than being decided only by one professional group.
- relation_to_previous: moves from problem statement to method.
- hard_details: systematic review + eDelphi + in-person consensus meeting; international stakeholder panel.
- tension_or_surprise: this gives the paper more practical authority for trial design than a routine review article.

- core_claim: the final core outcome set does not stop at laboratory values. It spans clinical examination, quality of life, serum biochemistry, complete blood count, urinalysis, food intake, progression, survival time, and cause of death.
- implicit_premise: treatment success in feline CKD cannot be captured adequately by biochemistry alone.
- relation_to_previous: moves from methodology to actual trial-design content.
- hard_details: 104 unique published parameters found initially; final core outcome set totalled 29 parameters grouped into 9 core themes.
- tension_or_surprise: this is broader than the current vault's first-wave operational endpoint shortlist, by design.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Treatment trials need a wider outcome architecture than routine CKD workups

The most important message in this paper is that trial outcomes should be standardized and broad. The core outcome set is not just a list of renal chemistry markers. It explicitly includes quality of life, food intake, progression, survival time, and cause of death in addition to routine clinical and laboratory categories. That means the vault should stop pretending that “what we monitor in clinic” and “what a treatment trial should minimally measure” are the same question.

### Hard Information

- 104 unique published parameters initially identified
- final core outcome set: 29 parameters
- 9 core themes

## Theme: The paper defines minimum breadth, not a universal priority order

This paper is strongest when read as a minimum architecture paper.
It helps answer what should minimally be captured across feline CKD treatment trials.
It does not, by itself, tell every intervention study which outcome should carry the most weight.

### Hard Information

- 24 of 104 parameters crossed the eDelphi inclusion threshold
- 20 additional parameters were proposed by panelists
- 3 of those 20 reached the inclusion threshold during the eDelphi process
- 6 borderline parameters were later included at the consensus meeting

## Theme: Owner-visible and burden outcomes now have formal trial status

This paper gives formal trial-design status to outcomes that are easy to underweight in a lab-centered CKD workflow.

### Hard Information

- the 9 core themes explicitly include quality of life
- the 9 core themes explicitly include total amount of food eaten
- the 9 core themes explicitly include survival time
- the 9 core themes explicitly include cause of death

## Theme: Consensus methodology matters because it makes the output reusable

This paper is useful not only because of which outcomes it names, but because of how they were selected. A structured multi-round consensus process makes the output more reusable as a trial-design scaffold than a normal narrative review would be.

### Hard Information

- systematic review
- eDelphi
- in-person consensus meeting
- international stakeholder panel
