## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: The paper is about protein-losing nephropathy, not generic CKD

- core_claim: the study focuses on feline protein-losing nephropathy, meaning proteinuria secondary to glomerular disease, rather than all feline CKD.
- implicit_premise: compartment-specific pathology matters for interpreting proteinuria.
- relation_to_previous: opening scope definition.
- hard_details: biopsy/necropsy samples from proteinuric cats were reviewed retrospectively.
- tension_or_surprise: this is narrower than the vault's general CKD map, which is exactly why it can refine the proteinuria branch.

#### Unit 2: Many proteinuric cats had immune-complex glomerulonephritis

- core_claim: immune-complex glomerulonephritis is common within this proteinuric feline disease subset.
- implicit_premise: at least some feline proteinuria should be read as primary glomerular disease, not only secondary CKD noise.
- relation_to_previous: moves from scope to the main disease-compartment result.
- hard_details: 58% of proteinuric cats had ICGN; 74% of cats with protein-losing nephropathy had ICGN.
- tension_or_surprise: this gives the proteinuria branch a more glomerular flavor than the broader tubulointerstitial CKD backbone would suggest.

#### Unit 3: UPC is important, but disease-compartment interpretation matters

- core_claim: higher UPC values were seen in glomerular diseases other than ICGN, showing that UPC magnitude alone does not collapse pathology into one category.
- implicit_premise: UPC is central, but still not self-interpreting.
- relation_to_previous: deepens the proteinuria result into endpoint interpretation.
- hard_details: median UPC 14.5 in non-ICGN glomerular diseases versus 6.5 in ICGN cats (P <0.001).
- tension_or_surprise: a central marker is still not enough without compartment context.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Proteinuria is a serious branch, not a side note

This paper reinforces that feline proteinuria should not be treated as a minor staging detail. It can reflect serious glomerular disease, including immune-complex disease, and carries its own clinicopathologic and outcome implications.

### Hard Information

- biopsy/necropsy reviewed
- ICGN common
- UPC differs across glomerular disease categories

## Theme: UPC is valuable, but compartment-aware interpretation is required

The paper strengthens UPC as a major endpoint, but it also warns against lazy interpretation. A higher UPC does not automatically mean one pathology over another, and broader feline CKD can still produce proteinuria secondarily through tubular dysfunction or glomerulosclerosis.

### Hard Information

- UPC 14.5 vs 6.5 in non-ICGN vs ICGN
- tubular and glomerular causes both exist
