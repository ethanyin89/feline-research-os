# Label Section Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-reg-012`  
Title: `DailyMed Current Label: Bexacat (bexagliflozin tablets)`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on DailyMed current-label section extraction, not clinical guideline synthesis.

## Core Takeaway

`Bexacat label control is dominated by DKA/euglycemic DKA risk, insulin-naive candidate selection, pre-treatment screening, and early glycemic-monitoring discontinuation gates`

## High-Value Label Sections

### Label Identity

- DailyMed lists Bexacat as a prescription animal drug label.
- DailyMed label page was updated January 25, 2023.
- Bexacat is a 15 mg flavored tablet for oral use in cats only.
- It is an SGLT2 inhibitor.

### Indication / Candidate Boundary

- The indication is improved glycemic control in otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.
- This is the controlling indication phrase for the vault.
- Do not generalize to complicated, insulin-treated, or insulin-dependent diabetes.

### Dosing / Form Boundary

- The label describes one tablet by mouth once daily for cats at least 3.0 kg.
- Dosing is with or without food and regardless of blood glucose level.
- This should be treated as label information, not patient-specific instruction.

### Monitoring And Discontinuation Gates

- Sudden hyporexia/anorexia, lethargy, dehydration, or weight loss should trigger discontinuation and DKA assessment regardless of blood glucose level.
- Routine monitoring includes blood glucose, fructosamine, BHBA, fPL, liver parameters, cholesterol, triglycerides, body weight, and clinical signs.
- fPL or liver-parameter elevations should trigger evaluation for pancreatitis and/or hepatic disease and consideration of discontinuation.
- BHBA is identified as the predominant ketoacid in DKA.
- During the first 8 weeks, the label calls for glycemic-control and clinical-improvement assessment.
- Physical examination, 8-hour blood glucose curve, fructosamine, and body weight are assessed at 2, 4, and 8 weeks.
- Poor glycemic control at 8 weeks should lead to discontinuation and consideration of insulin.

### Contraindications / Warnings

- Do not use in cats previously treated with insulin, receiving insulin, or with insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus.
- Do not use in cats with evidence of hepatic disease or reduced renal function.
- Do not initiate in cats with anorexia, dehydration, or lethargy at diagnosis.
- Do not initiate with pancreatitis indicators, DKA-consistent labs, or BHBA thresholds described by the label.
- Screen and monitor for urinary tract infections.
- Consider temporary discontinuation during decreased caloric intake.

### Adverse-Reaction Signal

- In the 180-day field study, 84 cats received at least one dose and 72 completed the study.
- Common adverse reactions included elevated BUN, vomiting, elevated USG, elevated fPL, diarrhea, anorexia, lethargy, and dehydration.
- The field-study table reports DKA in 3/84 and euglycemic DKA in 2/84.
- Nine serious adverse reactions associated with Bexacat administration occurred, including three cats who died or were euthanized.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- The Bexacat branch should be label-controlled by candidate selection and DKA/euglycemic DKA warnings.
- Monitoring claims should remain product-specific because Bexacat uses BHBA and 2/4/8-week monitoring details.
- The label supports route/form facts but does not support route superiority.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not compare Bexacat with Senvelgo or insulin.
- It does not establish use in complicated diabetes.
- It does not create owner-facing treatment instructions.
