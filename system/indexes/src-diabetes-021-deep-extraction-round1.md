## Source-Supported Facts

- PubMed abstract frames feline diabetes as multifactorial, involving genetic and environmental factors.
- Diet, excess body weight, and physical inactivity are named as pathogenesis factors.
- Type 2 diabetes is described as most common in cats.
- Most cats are insulin-dependent at diagnosis.
- Early good glycemic control is linked to clinical remission in a substantial proportion of diabetic cats.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source bridges mechanism and clinical overview before the 2014 JFMS review.
- It supports body weight and inactivity in risk architecture.
- It supports early glycemic control as remission-relevant, but not as a precise remission predictor.

## What It Does Not Prove
