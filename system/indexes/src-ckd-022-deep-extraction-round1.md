## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: The study asks whether one acute injury can create lasting CKD-like change

- core_claim: a single unilateral ischemic event may produce chronic renal structural and functional changes well beyond the short-term fibrosis window shown previously.
- implicit_premise: chronic feline CKD-like lesions can emerge from a discrete experimental insult.
- relation_to_previous: opening research question.
- hard_details: previous work showed fibrosis at 70 days; this study extends observation to 6 months after a 90-minute unilateral ischemic event.
- tension_or_surprise: the model is not repetitive chronic injury, but one acute insult with long follow-up.

#### Unit 2: Functional decline persists over months

- core_claim: the injured kidneys are not just histologically abnormal; renal function also worsens over time.
- implicit_premise: the model is more relevant if both function and structure deteriorate.
- relation_to_previous: moves from question to functional result.
- hard_details: six adult female cats; significant reduction in GFR; serum creatinine elevation of 31% and 42% over time.
- tension_or_surprise: one event is enough to leave a measurable functional legacy months later.

#### Unit 3: The lesion pattern includes both tubulointerstitial and glomerular change

- core_claim: the chronic lesion pattern is not limited to fibrosis. It includes inflammation, tubular atrophy, interstitial fibrosis, obsolescent glomeruli, and later glomerulosclerosis.
- implicit_premise: the model may be useful because it touches more than one lesion axis.
- relation_to_previous: deepens the pathologic meaning of the functional decline.
- hard_details: segmental interstitial inflammation, tubular atrophy, interstitial fibrosis; variable numbers of obsolescent glomeruli; atubular glomeruli and subsequent ischemic glomerulosclerosis.
- tension_or_surprise: this model may help explain how acute injury can evolve into chronic glomerular as well as tubulointerstitial pathology.
