# src-ibd-014 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`hydrolysed diet response is the cleanest current early treatment anchor, but it still sits inside a mixed IBD-versus-food-response frame`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first direct early-treatment anchor in the IBD module
- support for diet-first practical management logic in selected chronic-enteropathy cats
- a reminder that early treatment evidence still overlaps with food-responsive disease rather than pure idiopathic IBD alone

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 8 of 28 cats with chronic vomiting or diarrhea were diagnosed with chronic enteropathy.
- All cats underwent a complete diagnostic work-up including endoscopy.
- A hydrolysed protein diet was used as sole therapy.
- Clinical signs resolved within 4 to 8 days in all eight cats.
- After challenge with the previous diet, clinical signs recurred and resolved again in seven cats when the test diet was reintroduced.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Hydrolysed diet response belongs in the lead practical treatment branch of the current IBD module.
- The most defensible treatment framing is still `diet-first chronic-enteropathy management`, not drug-first idiopathic-IBD certainty.
- Early treatment anchoring should remain wider than strict idiopathic IBD because food-response overlap remains built into the evidence.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove hydrolysed diet uniquely treats idiopathic IBD as opposed to food-responsive disease.
- It does not establish a complete long-term treatment hierarchy.
- It does not outrank exclusion-first diagnostic architecture.
