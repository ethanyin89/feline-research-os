## Phase 1: Key Themes

### Theme 1: First Molnupiravir Case Series
This is the foundational publication establishing molnupiravir as a viable FIP treatment. Prior to this, only GS-441524 had clinical evidence.

### Theme 2: High Success in Completers
100% remission among cats completing 84-day treatment. The 22% mortality was within first 7 days, suggesting severe disease at presentation.

### Theme 3: Comparable Protocol to GS-441524
84-day duration and BID dosing mirrors established GS-441524 protocols, facilitating clinical adoption.

### Theme 4: Acceptable Safety Profile
Only adverse event: transient ALT elevation in 3/18 (17%) cats, self-resolving.

## Phase 3: Promotion Boundaries

### Safe to Promote

- Molnupiravir as FIP treatment option
- 10-20 mg/kg BID dosing range
- 84-day treatment duration
- High remission rate in completers
- Transient hepatic changes as main adverse event

### Not Safe to Promote

- Optimal dose within 10-20 mg/kg range
- Superiority or equivalence to GS-441524 (case series only)
- Long-term outcomes beyond 206 days
- Commercial formulation efficacy (compounded drug used)

### Design Limitations

- Case series (no control group)
- Single center
- Compounded drug (not standardized formulation)
- Short follow-up (max 206 days)
- Small sample size (18 cats)

## Phase 4: Integration Notes

### Module Impact

This source provides the first clinical evidence for molnupiravir in FIP, complementing the GS-441524 evidence (src-fip-016, src-fip-017). Together with src-fip-028 (comparative study), it supports molnupiravir as a validated treatment alternative.

### Timeline in FIP Antiviral Evidence
