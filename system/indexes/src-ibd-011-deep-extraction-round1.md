# src-ibd-011 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`shared epithelial COX-2 upregulation is not a clean boundary marker`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first COX-2 tissue-expression anchor in the IBD-versus-lymphoma branch
- support for a shared epithelial response layer across inflammatory and neoplastic disease
- a warning against confusing tissue-expression intensity with practical disease separation

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 11 cats with IBD and 9 cats with low-grade alimentary lymphoma after exclusions.
- Significant difference was found for epithelial COX-2 intensity in both IBD and lymphoma when compared with controls, but not between the two disease groups.
- No difference was found for lamina propria COX-2 immunoexpression between groups.
- There were no correlations between epithelial or lamina propria COX-2 expression and FCEAI or histologic alterations.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Epithelial COX-2 behaves more like a shared intestinal response marker than a boundary-discriminating marker.
- COX-2 immunoexpression may have biologic or explanatory value without providing practical separation between IBD and lymphoma.
- Tissue-marker depth should be modeled as subordinate to biopsy architecture and site-selection logic.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove COX-2 can distinguish IBD from lymphoma in practice.
- It does not show strong linkage to clinical severity.
- It does not justify COX-2-centered workup leadership.
