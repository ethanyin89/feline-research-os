# src-ibd-019 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`metabolomics is the strongest current frontier-separation signal, but it is still not routine-ready workup leadership`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first true omics frontier anchor in the IBD-versus-lymphoma boundary branch
- support for a metabolomic layer that is stronger than generic shared-support markers
- a bridge from disease-boundary difficulty to a plausible future stratification architecture

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports fecal metabolomics in 14 healthy cats and 22 cats with chronic enteropathy, including 11 with IBD and 11 with small-cell lymphoma.
- Principal component analysis and heat-map analysis showed distinct clustering between chronic enteropathy and healthy controls.
- Random forest classification showed good prediction for healthy cats and cats with chronic enteropathy, with overall out-of-bag error 16.7%.
- The abstract states that polyunsaturated fatty acids held discriminatory power in differentiating IBD from small-cell lymphoma.
- Metabolomic profiles showed alterations related to tryptophan, arachidonic acid, and glutathione pathways.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Metabolomics currently belongs in a frontier-marker layer above ordinary support markers.
- The metabolomic branch appears more disease-class-informative than vitamin D or S100A12, but it still does not outrank core workup architecture.
- The strongest current role of metabolomics is likely future stratification and biologic separation, not frontline diagnosis.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove fecal metabolomics is ready for routine clinical differentiation of IBD and lymphoma.
- It does not replace biopsy, histopathology, imaging, or biopsy-site strategy.
- It does not prove all identified metabolites are individually stable diagnostic markers.
