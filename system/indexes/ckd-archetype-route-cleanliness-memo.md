# CKD Archetype-Route Cleanliness Memo

- Date: `2026-04-10`
- Scope: comparison layer above the first four CKD route memos

This memo exists to stop the CKD regulatory branch from drifting into one vague sentence like:

`the best treatment archetype is the best regulatory path`

That sentence sounds efficient.

It is not true enough for this vault.

## Core Takeaway

`for CKD, treatment supportability and route cleanliness are related, but they are not the same ranking`

The current vault is now strong enough to compare those two axes directly.

## Why This Memo Exists

The current CKD vault already has:

- a treatment product archetype memo
- four product-specific route memos

That means the repeated confusion is no longer:

- what the archetypes are

It is now:

- which archetype is strongest in treatment logic
- which archetype is cleanest in route-fit logic
- where endpoint-fit is stronger than category-fit
- where route-fit stays usable while claim-fit becomes fragile

## Current Comparison Read

### Renal Diet

Treatment supportability:

- strongest overall

Route cleanliness:

- weaker than its treatment strength suggests

Why:

- the current official-source pack is medicine-centered
- nutrition-positioned product framing remains category-blurry in the current corpus

### Hemodynamic Management

Treatment supportability:

- not the strongest overall

Route cleanliness:

- cleanest current route-fit archetype

Why:

- clear subgroup
- clear operational endpoint
- best match to the current medicine-centered regulatory corpus

### Proteinuria-Oriented Renoprotective Adjunct

Treatment supportability:

- meaningful, but overclaim-sensitive

Route cleanliness:

- usable, but less clean than hemodynamic management

Why:

- route-fit can stay usable while claim-fit drifts out of bounds

### Phosphorus-Control Adjunct

Treatment supportability:

- high endpoint strength

Route cleanliness:

- intermediate

Why:

- endpoint-fit is strong
- category-fit remains blurrier than the endpoint itself

## Comparison Table

| Archetype | Treatment Supportability | Route Cleanliness | Current Main Bottleneck |
|---|---|---|---|
| renal diet | strongest overall | relatively weak | product-category mismatch with current official-source pack |
| hemodynamic management | moderate-to-high | strongest current route fit | still not indication-specific enough for recommendation |
| proteinuria-oriented renoprotective adjunct | moderate and overclaim-sensitive | intermediate | claim-fit becomes fragile faster than route-fit |
| phosphorus-control adjunct | high endpoint strength | intermediate | endpoint-fit is cleaner than category-fit |

## What This Memo Changes

This memo makes one point explicit:

`the current CKD regulatory branch should now be read as a comparison problem, not as four disconnected route memos`

That means:

- the best treatment archetype is not automatically the best route archetype
- the cleanest route archetype is not automatically the strongest treatment archetype
- different branches fail for different reasons

## What We Can Say Now

1. Renal diet is still the strongest treatment archetype overall.
2. Hemodynamic management is currently the cleanest route-fit archetype in the existing official-source pack.
3. Proteinuria renoprotection exposes claim-fragility more than route-fragility.
4. Phosphorus-control adjunct exposes category-fragility more than endpoint-fragility.

## What We Should Not Say Now

- do not collapse treatment ranking into route ranking
- do not promote hemodynamic management into a final jurisdiction recommendation
- do not treat route cleanliness as proof of evidence superiority
- do not assume the current official-source pack is equally informative for all CKD product classes
