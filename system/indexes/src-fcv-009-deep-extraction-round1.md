## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: FCV diagnosis needs `best tool` and `bounded interpretation` together

This paper strengthens the diagnostic layer because it gives the module a clean sentence for PCR: use it first, but do not confuse it with closure.

### Hard Information

- FCV can best be detected by RT-PCR
- negative result does not rule out infection
- healthy cats can test positive

## Theme: Vaccination belongs to disease control, not sterilizing immunity

The paper is especially useful because it makes the vaccine boundary explicit in modern review language.

### Hard Information

- all cats should be vaccinated
- vaccination protects from disease, not infection
- infection-induced immunity is not lifelong and not all-strain protective

## Theme: Multicat environments are the natural FCV control setting

This source keeps FCV from being written like a simple one-cat pathogen problem. Density, environment, and management context matter.

### Hard Information

- infections particularly problematic in multicat environments
- virus resistant to many disinfectants
- environmental tenacity is explicit in the review framing
