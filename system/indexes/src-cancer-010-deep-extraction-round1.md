## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: Treatment Innovation Need

The review frames oncolytic virotherapy as addressing an unmet need — despite diagnostic advances, treatment outcomes remain limited. This establishes the rationale for novel therapeutic approaches in veterinary oncology.

### Theme B: Mechanism of Action

Oncolytic viruses work through two complementary mechanisms:
1. **Direct cytotoxicity:** Selective infection and lysis of cancer cells
2. **Immune activation:** Tumor-specific immunity induction

This dual mechanism may explain potential synergy with other immunotherapies.

### Theme C: Investigational Status

This is a 2014 review of experimental therapy. The technology remains investigational and is not standard veterinary oncology practice. Any feline-specific applications require full-text verification.
