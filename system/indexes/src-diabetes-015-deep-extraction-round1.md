# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-015`  
Title: `Comparison of a low carbohydrate-low fiber diet and a moderate carbohydrate-high fiber diet in the management of feline diabetes mellitus`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on Crossref abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata, not full section-by-section extraction of the complete article text.

## Core Takeaway

`low-carbohydrate/low-fiber diet improved non-insulin-dependent outcome versus moderate-carbohydrate/high-fiber diet in this 16-week randomized blinded-owner study, but the result should not become a universal diet rule without full-text review`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- a direct diet-composition comparison in diabetic cats
- glycemic-control and insulin-independence endpoints
- trial design details: 63 cats, 16 weeks, owner blinding, two canned diet arms

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 63 diabetic cats, with 32 assigned to moderate-carbohydrate/high-fiber food and 31 to low-carbohydrate/low-fiber food.
- The study ran for 16 weeks.
- Serum glucose and fructosamine decreased in both diet groups.
- By week 16, 22/31 cats in the low-carbohydrate/low-fiber group and 13/32 cats in the moderate-carbohydrate/high-fiber group had reverted to a non-insulin-dependent state.
- The abstract reports no significant difference in body-weight change between diet groups.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This is the strongest current diet-comparison original-study anchor in the diabetes seed set.
- The source supports separating glycemic improvement from insulin-independence/remission-like outcomes.
- The source supports a low-carbohydrate branch, but only within this study design and population.
- Diet claims should retain study-design context: canned diets, 16-week duration, insulin adjustment, and owner blinding.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove all diabetic cats should receive one diet formulation.
- It does not isolate carbohydrate from fiber as the only causal variable.
- It does not replace the remission systematic review as the remission boundary owner.
