# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-008`  
Title: `Treatment of feline diabetes mellitus using an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor and a low-carbohydrate diet`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on Crossref/PMC abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata.

## Core Takeaway

`acarbose plus low-carbohydrate diet was associated with improved glycemic control and reduced exogenous insulin dependence in a small client-owned cat series, but attribution is confounded by diet plus drug plus insulin adjustments`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- adjunct therapy branch
- acarbose plus low-carbohydrate diet combination
- 18 client-owned cats in the acarbose/diet group
- responder/non-responder framing

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract reports 18 client-owned diabetic cats in the study group.
- Cats received a low-carbohydrate canned diet plus acarbose every 12 hours with meals.
- Most cats also received insulin therapy.
- Patients were classified as responders when insulin was discontinued.
- Serum fructosamine and glucose decreased in both responder and non-responder groups over 4 months.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source supports an adjunct-treatment branch, not a standalone acarbose recommendation.
- Because diet, acarbose, and insulin adjustment are combined, causal attribution needs care.
- It should be interpreted alongside diet-only and remission-boundary sources.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not isolate acarbose effect from low-carbohydrate diet.
- It does not define a general first-line protocol.
- It does not replace modern insulin/SGLT2 evidence.
