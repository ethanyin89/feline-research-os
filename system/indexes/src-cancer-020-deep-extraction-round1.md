## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: Aggressive Disease Characterization

FMC is defined by aggressive behavior:
- **Rapid growth:** Tumors progress quickly
- **High recurrence:** Even after treatment, tumors frequently return
- **Poor survival:** Outcomes are generally unfavorable

This characterization remains consistent with modern understanding (see src-cancer-005, src-cancer-009, src-cancer-015).

### Theme B: Management Philosophy

The management triad established in 1985 reflects a pragmatic response to aggressive disease:
1. **Early diagnosis:** Because tumors grow fast, early detection is crucial
2. **Aggressive surgery:** Because recurrence is high, thorough surgical intervention is needed
3. **Frequent follow-up:** Because recurrence is common, close monitoring catches it early

### Theme C: Knowledge Evolution Context

The 1985 paper acknowledges knowledge gaps:
- Prognostic factors were being identified (now well-characterized, e.g., tumor size)
- Treatment protocols were evolving (now include chemotherapy options)
- Etiology was uncertain (now understood to include hormonal, molecular factors)
