# HCM Frontier Augmentation Memo

- Date: `2026-04-10`
- Scope: `src-hcm-011`, `src-hcm-017`, `src-hcm-023`

This memo exists to stop the current feline HCM frontier branch from collapsing into one vague sentence like:

`AI, novel biomarkers, and targeted therapy are the future of HCM`

That sentence captures direction, but it destroys the hierarchy.

## Core Takeaway

`the HCM frontier branch is real, but it is structurally split between targeted therapeutic frontier, biomarker frontier, and AI augmentation, and all three remain below routine structural authority`

## Current Frontier Layers

### Layer 1: Targeted Therapeutic Frontier

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-hcm-011`

Current meaning:

- HCM now has a genuine mechanism-linked targeted-therapy branch
- acute LVOT-obstruction relief is structurally important
- targeted physiologic success should remain below final treatment hierarchy and durable outcome claims

### Layer 2: Frontier Biomarker Branch

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-hcm-017`

Current meaning:

- the endpoint map now extends beyond troponin and NT-proBNP
- novel biomarkers belong in a frontier-marker branch rather than a routine-marker branch
- novelty and confidence should be tracked together, especially when the source itself is caution-bound

### Layer 3: AI Augmentation Branch

This layer is mainly carried by:

- `src-hcm-023`

Current meaning:

- HCM recognition now has a real computational-augmentation subbranch
- AI belongs in screening augmentation or support, not in structural confirmation
- augmentation-versus-replacement must remain an explicit boundary

## What These Frontier Branches Clarify

- frontier does not mean flat
- targeted therapy, novel biomarkers, and AI do not belong in one generic innovation bucket
- all three branches currently deepen the module more than they reorder routine authority
- frontier papers are useful because they sharpen hierarchy, not because they erase it

## What The Module Can Already Say

- HCM has a real frontier layer rather than only conventional management and routine markers
- targeted therapy belongs in translation frontier, not in routine hierarchy
- novel biomarkers belong in endpoint frontier, not in routine confirmation
- AI belongs in recognition augmentation, not in phenotype-definition authority

## What The Module Should Not Yet Say

- there is no safe unified `frontier ranking` yet
- acute targeted-therapy success should not be promoted into broad intervention superiority
- novel biomarkers should not be promoted into routine-ready endpoint authority
- AI should not be promoted into first-wave clinical confirmation
