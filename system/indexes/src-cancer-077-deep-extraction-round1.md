# Deep Extraction Worksheet: src-cancer-077

**Source:** BNCT of 3 cases of spontaneous head and neck cancer in feline patients
**Journal:** Applied Radiation and Isotopes (2004)
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.apradiso.2004.05.016
**PMID:** 15308173
**Evidence Level:** case-series (n=3)

## Phase 0: Context

**Access status:** Abstract available from PubMed.

**Source scope:** 2004 feasibility study of boron neutron capture therapy (BNCT) using BPA as boron carrier in 3 terminal feline head/neck cancer patients.

**Key contribution:** First reported BNCT treatment of spontaneous feline head/neck cancer; demonstrates feasibility and safety.

**Critical boundary:** Very small sample (n=3), all terminal patients. Investigational therapy. Not standard of care.

## Phase 1: Sequential Micro-Analysis

### 1.1 Study Design

| Parameter | Value |

| Prior work | Hamster cheek pouch SCC model |

### 1.2 BNCT Mechanism

BNCT basics:
- Boron-10 compound (BPA) concentrates in tumor
- Neutron irradiation triggers nuclear capture reaction
- High-LET particles damage tumor cells
- Theoretically selective for boron-loaded tissue

### 1.3 Safety Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| Radiotoxicity | None observed |
| Boron delivery | Therapeutic levels in tumor |
| Normal tissue | No reported toxicity |

### 1.4 Efficacy Results

| Outcome | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| Tumor control | Partial |
| Tumor growth | Impaired |
| Necrosis | Partial |
| Survival | Prolonged beyond terminal condition |
| Clinical condition | Improved |

## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: BNCT Feasibility

Demonstrated in feline patients:
- BPA achieves therapeutic boron concentrations
- Neutron irradiation is tolerable
- No acute radiotoxicity
- Feasible in spontaneous tumors

### Theme B: Investigational Status

Limitations and context:
- n=3 is very small
- All patients were terminal
- No control group
- 2004 study; limited follow-up data since
- BNCT remains highly specialized (neutron source required)

### Theme C: Comparative Oncology

Translational value:
- Spontaneous feline tumors more relevant than rodent models
- Hamster cheek pouch work preceded this
- Supports feline cancer as human cancer model
- However, BNCT access is extremely limited

### Theme D: Historical Context

BNCT landscape:
- 2004 era of BNCT research
- Limited subsequent feline BNCT studies
- Not adopted as standard veterinary oncology treatment
- Remains experimental worldwide
