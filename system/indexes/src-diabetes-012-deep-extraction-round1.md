# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-012`  
Title: `Frequency of feline diabetes mellitus and breed predisposition in domestic cats in Australia`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on PubMed abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata, not full section-by-section extraction of the complete article text.

## Core Takeaway

`Australian feline-only clinic data support a Burmese predisposition signal, but clinic-based period prevalence needs careful generalizability limits`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- Australian frequency estimate from two feline-only clinics
- 5-year period prevalence denominator
- Burmese breed predisposition signal
- age-at-diagnosis comparison between Burmese and domestic short/longhaired cats

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 12,576 study cats and 93 diabetes cases over 5 years.
- Five-year period prevalence was 7.4 per 1000 cats.
- Burmese cats had a period prevalence of 22.4 per 1000 cats.
- Mean age at first diagnosis was higher in Burmese cats than domestic short/longhaired cats.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This is the key Australian breed-predisposition anchor.
- Burmese predisposition appears in both this source and the UK risk-factor paper, making it a high-priority synthesis point.
- Population structure matters: two feline-only clinics are not the same denominator as insured UK cats.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove the genetic or environmental mechanism behind Burmese predisposition.
- It does not establish global prevalence.
- It does not replace individual clinical risk assessment.
