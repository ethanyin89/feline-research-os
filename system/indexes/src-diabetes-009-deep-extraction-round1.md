# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-009`  
Title: `Feline diabetes mellitus in the UK: The prevalence within an insured cat population and a questionnaire-based putative risk factor analysis`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on Crossref abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata, not full section-by-section extraction of the complete article text.

## Core Takeaway

`UK insured-population data support prevalence and risk-factor branches, but questionnaire design and population selection mean these are risk signals, not individual prediction rules`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- UK insured-population prevalence estimate
- Burmese breed-risk signal
- risk-factor signals involving sex, neuter status, activity, weight, corticosteroid history, and megestrol acetate exposure

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports diabetes prevalence of 1 in 230 cats in a UK insured population.
- Burmese cats were 3.7 times more likely to develop diabetes than non-pedigree cats in this population.
- Univariate analysis identified male sex, neuter status, inactivity, weight of at least 5 kg, and corticosteroid history as significant risk factors.
- A multivariate tree model identified gender as the most important overall risk factor.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This is a key UK epidemiology anchor.
- Breed-risk, activity, weight, and medication exposure belong in the risk map.
- Risk-factor claims need population and design caveats.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove causality for questionnaire risk factors.
- It does not necessarily generalize outside insured UK cats.
- It does not replace Australian breed-prevalence data.
