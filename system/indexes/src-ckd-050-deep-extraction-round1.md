# src-ckd-050 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Source Access

- Full text verified through the publisher article and PubMed Central.
- DOI: `10.1186/s12917-018-1387-2`
- PMCID: `PMC5845201`
- Study type: primary feline renal cortical fibroblast culture and TGF-beta1 stimulation experiment.

## Phase A - Study Architecture

- Primary renal fibroblast cultures were established from the renal cortex of four normal cats.
- The investigators stimulated cells with TGF-beta1 for 24 hours and measured 20 fibrosis-associated genes.
- The TGF-beta receptor I inhibitor SB431542 was used to test whether selected transcriptional responses were receptor-mediated.

## Phase B - Main Findings

- TGF-beta1 altered 13 of the 20 profibrotic genes assessed.
- Increased transcripts included `ACTA2`, `COL1A1`, `COL4A1`, and `CTGF`, supporting induction of a myofibroblast and extracellular-matrix program.
- `MMP1` and `MMP3` decreased, consistent with reduced matrix-degradation signalling in this model.
- SB431542 attenuated selected TGF-beta1-induced responses, including changes in `ACTA2`, `COL1A1`, `COL4A1`, and `CTGF`.
- The study supplies direct feline, cell-level evidence that TGF-beta signalling can drive a profibrotic transcriptional response in renal cortical fibroblasts.

## Phase C - Evidence Boundaries

- The donor kidneys were normal, not spontaneous CKD kidneys.
- The donor count was four, and the experimental system was in vitro.
- Gene-expression changes do not establish tissue fibrosis, clinical progression, or treatment efficacy in living cats.
- Pharmacologic attenuation in culture is target-engagement evidence, not evidence supporting clinical use, dose, safety, or outcome benefit.
- The source is best used as a feline mechanism and model anchor, not as a therapeutic recommendation.

## Phase D - Repository Role

- Add direct feline in-vitro support to the TGF-beta fibrosis branch.
- Expand the CKD model map with a primary renal fibroblast culture model.
- Preserve the distinction between mechanistic plausibility and validated in-vivo intervention.

## Citation

Characterisation of feline renal cortical fibroblast cultures and their transcriptional response to transforming growth factor beta1. BMC Veterinary Research. 2018. doi:10.1186/s12917-018-1387-2.
