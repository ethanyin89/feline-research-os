# src-ibd-016 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`Bcl-2 is a lymphoma-leaning tissue marker, but not a clean stand-alone separator`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first stronger-than-COX2 tissue-marker signal in the IBD-versus-lymphoma branch
- support for a lymphoma-leaning apoptotic-survival marker layer
- a reminder that even statistically different markers can still be too overlapping for solo use

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports Bcl-2 immunolabeling from 55 cats with small-intestinal biopsy sections.
- Eight cats were diagnosed with IBD and 47 with gastrointestinal lymphoma.
- A significantly higher percentage of cells were positively immunolabeled for Bcl-2 in GI lymphoma than in IBD.
- The abstract states that the overall degree of positive immunolabeling tended to be high in both groups.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Bcl-2 offers a lymphoma-leaning tissue-marker signal stronger than the shared COX-2 signal.
- Even this stronger signal remains bounded because overlap remains substantial across groups.
- Tissue-marker interpretation should sit below morphology, biopsy strategy, and broader pathology review rather than replace them.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove Bcl-2 alone can distinguish IBD from lymphoma with operational confidence.
- It does not prove high Bcl-2 labeling is specific to lymphoma.
- It does not replace integrated pathology review.
