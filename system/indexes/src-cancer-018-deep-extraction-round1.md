## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: Feline-Specific Research Advantages

This review highlights that cats offer research opportunities not available in other species:

1. **Inflammation → Cancer:** FISS provides a naturally-occurring model for inflammation-driven tumorigenesis
2. **Viral Transformation:** FeLV-associated lymphomas model viral oncogenesis

These are "underexplored aspects of tumor biology" better studied in cats.

### Theme B: Reference Genome as Enabler

The development of a high-quality feline reference genome was transformative:
- Enabled molecular/cytogenomic studies
- Previously a barrier to feline cancer research
- Now allows integration with human cancer genomics

### Theme C: Technique Evolution

Cytogenomics has evolved from:
- Conventional karyotyping (chromosome counting)
- To genomic microarrays (CNV detection)
- And beyond (NGS, etc.)

This provides methodology context for interpreting feline cancer genomic studies.
