## Core Takeaway

`IGla-U300 showed promising short-term glycemic and clinical-sign improvement in a small prospective study, but hypersomatotropism exclusions and small sample size keep it below protocol-ranking status`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- a prospective clinical-trial anchor for insulin glargine 300 U/ml
- home blood-glucose curve and fructosamine monitoring details
- remission and hypoglycemia observations that need careful boundary language

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 13 client-owned diabetic cats completed the prospective clinical trial.
- Four cats suspected of hypersomatotropism were excluded from insulin efficacy evaluation.
- Cats were treated with IGla-U300 twice daily and fed a low-carbohydrate diet.
- Two cats achieved remission during the 8-week study and two more after the study ended.
- Biochemical hypoglycemia was detected in 13/104 blood-glucose curves, without reported clinical hypoglycemia signs.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This is the current glargine U300 treatment anchor in the seed set.
- It supports glargine U300 as promising, but not as a comparative winner.
- Hypersomatotropism is not a side issue; suspected cases were excluded from efficacy evaluation.
- Safety interpretation needs both biochemical hypoglycemia and clinical-sign reporting.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove superiority over other insulin protocols.
- It does not establish long-term remission rates.
- It does not remove the need to screen difficult-control cats for endocrine comorbidity.
