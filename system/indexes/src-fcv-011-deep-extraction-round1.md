## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: FCV challenge protection is a real and separate endpoint family

This paper upgrades the challenge branch from a loose idea into a high-signal endpoint family. It supports saying that certain strain choices can materially improve protection against a virulent systemic FCV challenge, without pretending that this closes the field-effectiveness question.

### Hard Information

- 78% mortality in unvaccinated controls after VS-FCV-33585 challenge
- vaccines containing FCV-21 reduced mortality to 0% in the reported model
- broader cross-neutralisation profile than common F9-centric logic

## Theme: Strain-selection language now has a stronger anchor

The paper is especially useful because it ties a broader neutralisation profile to actual challenge outcomes. That makes `strain selection matters` a supported FCV design claim rather than a speculative one.

### Hard Information

- FCV-21 broad serum cross-neutralisation profile
- mortality and clinical-score improvement in the challenge model

## Theme: Challenge success does not create present-market ranking

The paper still has a hard ceiling. It is a controlled challenge study and should not be promoted into current-market superiority, universal population benefit, or whole-program control language.

### Hard Information

- avirulent strain candidate
- challenge-model setting
- no direct real-world market comparison in the preserved source layer
