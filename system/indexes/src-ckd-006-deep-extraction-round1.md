#### Unit 2: Diet leads binder logic

- core_claim: phosphate-restricted diet has the strongest current support in stage 2-3 cats.
- implicit_premise: dietary control should remain above binder adjunct logic in current hierarchy.
- relation_to_previous: treatment hierarchy anchor.
- hard_details: `IRIS stage 2-3`.
- tension_or_surprise: the best-supported phosphate intervention is still diet.

#### Unit 3: Binders are common but thinly supported

- core_claim: intestinal phosphate binders are widely relevant but supported by few veterinary reports.
- implicit_premise: practical use should be kept separate from proof strength.
- relation_to_previous: adjunct branch below diet.
- hard_details: no binders licensed as medications for dogs or cats; aluminium toxicity concern.
- tension_or_surprise: binder use outpaces licensing and evidence strength.

#### Unit 4: PTH matters before phosphorus looks abnormal

- core_claim: early CKD may show increased PTH despite serum phosphorus remaining in range.
- implicit_premise: first-wave phosphorus logic has a hidden earlier layer.
- relation_to_previous: expands endpoint nuance.
- hard_details: `PTH`; normal serum phosphorus can coexist with retention.
- tension_or_surprise: visible phosphorus can lag underlying mineral burden.
