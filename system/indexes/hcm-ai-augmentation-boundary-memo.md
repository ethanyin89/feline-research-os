# HCM AI Augmentation Boundary Memo

- Date: `2026-04-11`
- Scope: `src-hcm-023`

This memo exists to stop the HCM AI branch from drifting into one vague sentence like:

`AI can diagnose feline HCM`

That sentence notices the branch, but it erases the boundary that currently matters most.

## Core Takeaway

`the current HCM AI branch is best treated as augmentation pressure on recognition and endpoint interpretation, not as replacement authority for structure-first phenotype definition`

## What This Memo Is Narrower Than

This memo is narrower than:

- [HCM frontier augmentation memo](hcm-frontier-augmentation-memo.md)
- [HCM biomarker use-case memo](hcm-biomarker-use-case-memo.md)

Those pages say where AI sits in the larger frontier map.

This page answers the tighter question:

`what exactly is AI allowed to do in the current HCM stack, and what should it still not be allowed to do?`

## Current Boundary

### Layer 1: Recognition Augmentation

- AI can deepen recognition architecture by making screening or support pressure explicit
- AI is currently useful as a reason to model an augmentation branch, not as a reason to dissolve structure-first workup
- computational diagnosis pressure makes hierarchy design more important, not less

### Layer 2: Input-Dependent Support

- AI authority depends heavily on what data it consumes
- image-linked or marker-linked models still inherit the limits of the underlying branch
- current extraction depth is strong enough to place AI in the stack, but not strong enough to grant routine-ready authority

### Layer 3: Non-Replacement Boundary

- AI should stay below structural confirmation
- AI should not be treated as a phenotype-definition engine
- AI should not be allowed to replace echo-led or morphometry-led recognition

## What The Module Can Already Say

- HCM has a real AI augmentation branch rather than only biomarker and imaging papers
- AI belongs closer to screening augmentation and support than to definitive confirmation
- augmentation-versus-replacement is currently the key boundary, not algorithm-versus-human competition rhetoric
- current AI evidence is useful for hierarchy design even before it is useful for routine workflow leadership

## What The Module Should Not Yet Say

- there is no safe claim that AI is first-wave routine diagnostic authority
- there is no safe claim that AI can replace structural phenotype confirmation
- there is no safe claim that AI already outranks routine biomarker or imaging interpretation across contexts
- there is no safe unified ranking yet between AI augmentation and frontier biomarker branches

## Short Operational Read

| Branch | Current role | What it helps with | What it should not claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI augmentation | bounded recognition-support branch | may improve screening or support interpretation | not phenotype-definition authority |
| Routine biomarkers | bounded marker branch | may flag severity or screening pressure | not structural confirmation |
| Structural phenotype | lead confirmation branch | defines current routine recognition authority | not replaceable by thin AI evidence |
