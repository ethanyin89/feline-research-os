## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: The field wants earlier detection, but not at the cost of false certainty

This review starts from a very practical problem: serum creatinine is useful, but too weak as an early marker. That pushes the field toward biomarkers that can detect earlier kidney dysfunction or injury. SDMA is the clearest current candidate in that direction, but the review does not let it become a clean replacement story because specificity is still limited.

### Hard Information

- creatinine limited for early CKD
- SDMA introduced as novel GFR biomarker
- specificity data still limited

## Theme: Biomarker work in feline CKD is now a field, not a single-marker update

The review broadens the biomarker landscape beyond creatinine and SDMA. It frames urinary and serum biomarkers as potentially reflecting glomerular dysfunction, tubular dysfunction, fibrosis, oxidative injury, and progression risk. That means the biomarker problem is not “what replaces creatinine,” but “which marker is useful for which question.”

### Hard Information

- KIM-1
- L-FABP
- NGAL
- FGF-23
- TGF-beta1

## Theme: Routine markers remain central, but must be interpreted with limits

The review does not abolish existing markers. Instead, it reinforces that UPC and GFR still matter, but each inside a bounded interpretive frame. GFR is ideal but impractical; UPC is useful but not specific enough to be read without clinical context. This aligns with the vault's current “multi-axis endpoint” direction.

### Hard Information

- GFR gold standard but research-heavy
- UPC prognostic usefulness
- UPC specificity reduced by non-renal disease
