# src-ibd-004 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`clinical activity tracking belongs to chronic-enteropathy management, not to disease-class discrimination`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first real operational activity-index anchor in the IBD module
- a bridge between clinical presentation, endoscopy, lab data, and histopathologic burden
- support for tracking treatment response without pretending the index can resolve the lymphoma boundary

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports a need for a clinically useful quantitative index for disease activity in feline chronic enteropathy.
- The study included 82 cats with chronic enteropathy.
- The abstract states that the variable combination with the best correlation to histopathologic inflammation included gastrointestinal signs, endoscopic abnormalities, serum total protein, serum alanine transaminase or alkaline phosphatase activity, and serum phosphorus concentration.
- The abstract states that this variable set comprised the feline chronic enteropathy activity index, or FCEAI.
- The abstract states that positive treatment responses were accompanied by significant reductions in FCEAI scores.
- The abstract concludes that FCEAI can be used for initial severity assessment in both IBD and food-responsive enteropathy and for measuring clinical response to treatment.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- FCEAI belongs in the lead operational endpoint branch for feline chronic enteropathy.
- The activity-index layer should be modeled above routine monitoring and treatment response, not as the main disease-identity layer.
- The broad IBD module should keep `activity`, `diagnosis`, and `boundary discrimination` as separate layers.
- The fact that FCEAI spans both IBD and food-responsive enteropathy reinforces that early workup should not jump too fast from chronic enteropathy to idiopathic IBD.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove FCEAI can distinguish IBD from small-cell lymphoma.
- It does not replace biopsy, histopathology, or site-selection decisions.
- It does not make chronic-enteropathy activity the same thing as etiologic diagnosis.
