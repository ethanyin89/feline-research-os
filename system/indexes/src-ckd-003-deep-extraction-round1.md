## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: Most therapy is consequence-management

- core_claim: feline CKD treatment usually targets consequences of reduced renal function rather than the underlying cause.
- implicit_premise: supportive management and disease modification should remain separate branches.
- relation_to_previous: opening treatment frame.
- hard_details: focus on adverse effects of reduced renal function.
- tension_or_surprise: treatment can still improve survival without correcting root cause.

#### Unit 2: Renal diet sits unusually high

- core_claim: renal diets restricting protein and phosphorus have the strongest current support.
- implicit_premise: the treatment layer is not flat.
- relation_to_previous: identifies the clearest baseline-supported intervention.
- hard_details: `protein`, `phosphorus`, survival and quality-of-life emphasis.
- tension_or_surprise: diet outperforms many more medication-like options in support strength.

#### Unit 3: Adjuncts matter, but not equally

- core_claim: phosphate binders, hypokalaemia control, hypertension treatment, and later anaemia/GI support all matter, but not with the same evidence weight.
- implicit_premise: treatment mapping should separate baseline-supported from context-dependent adjuncts.
- relation_to_previous: broadens the treatment branch below diet.
- hard_details: `phosphate binders`, `hypokalaemia`, `hypertension`, `anaemia`, `uraemic gastroenteritis`.
- tension_or_surprise: many clinically familiar branches remain secondary to diet in evidence strength.

#### Unit 4: Benazepril shows bounded value

- core_claim: benazepril improved appetite in proteinuric disease without improving survival.
- implicit_premise: symptomatic or intermediate benefit should not be promoted into survival certainty.
- relation_to_previous: adds a concrete cautionary branch.
- hard_details: `benazepril`, `appetite`, no survival improvement.
- tension_or_surprise: apparent treatment benefit does not necessarily change ultimate hierarchy.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: CKD treatment is useful but mostly not curative

This review is structurally important because it makes explicit that most feline CKD treatment is management of consequences. That helps keep later translational optimism from being misread as true disease reversal.

## Theme: Diet leads the current baseline hierarchy

The paper strengthens the vault-wide conclusion that renal diet is the clearest current first-line intervention branch. It sits above many adjuncts because the support is survival-linked, not merely plausibility-linked.

## Theme: Adjunct treatments need evidence labels

The paper widens the treatment map while making it obvious that supportive and adjunctive options should not be collapsed into one efficacy tier.
