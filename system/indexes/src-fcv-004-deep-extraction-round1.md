## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: The guideline is practical-control first

- core_claim: this paper organizes FCV around infection, disease signs, diagnosis, and management rather than around virology alone.
- implicit_premise: FCV needs a practical control layer, not only a vaccine or mechanism layer.
- relation_to_previous: opening operational frame.
- hard_details: the abstract explicitly structures the paper under infection, disease signs, diagnosis, and disease management.
- tension_or_surprise: the most reusable value here is not novelty but control architecture.

#### Unit 2: Shedding and carrier state sit at the front door

- core_claim: sick, acutely infected, or carrier cats shed FCV in oronasal and conjunctival secretions, and infection occurs mainly through direct contact.
- implicit_premise: FCV recognition and control must keep carrier-state biology visible from the start.
- relation_to_previous: moves from overall structure to transmission logic.
- hard_details: the abstract names oronasal and conjunctival secretions plus direct contact as the main infection route.
- tension_or_surprise: this is one reason PCR and symptom interpretation cannot be read in isolation.

#### Unit 3: Diagnosis is allowed, but certainty is bounded

- core_claim: virus isolation and RT-PCR can detect FCV, but positive PCR results must be interpreted carefully because persistently infected carriers may shed at low levels.
- implicit_premise: FCV detection and FCV causality are not interchangeable.
- relation_to_previous: translates carrier-state biology into diagnostic caution.
- hard_details: the abstract lists conjunctival and oral swabs, blood, skin scrapings, and lung tissue as PCR-detectable sample types.
- tension_or_surprise: this is not an anti-PCR statement; it is an anti-overclaim statement.

#### Unit 4: Management is supportive and environmental

- core_claim: supportive therapy, nursing care, palatable feeding, mucolytics/nebulisation, and broad-spectrum antibiotics for secondary infection are central management tools.
- implicit_premise: FCV management should not be written as a drug-first antiviral branch.
- relation_to_previous: shifts from diagnosis to care logic.
- hard_details: the abstract mentions fluid therapy, warmed/blended food, bromhexine or saline nebulisation, and prevention of secondary bacterial infection.
- tension_or_surprise: supportive care is not filler; it is the management backbone in this guideline.

#### Unit 5: Environmental persistence matters

- core_claim: FCV can persist in the environment for about 1 month and resists many common disinfectants.
- implicit_premise: hygiene and environmental control are structural parts of FCV prevention.
- relation_to_previous: extends management from the cat to the environment.
- hard_details: the abstract explicitly mentions environmental persistence and disinfectant resistance.
- tension_or_surprise: control language that ignores environmental persistence will be structurally incomplete.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: FCV control is composite

This guideline is important because it keeps transmission, diagnosis, nursing care, and environmental hygiene in one frame. It prevents FCV from being written as only a vaccine or only a laboratory-detection problem.

### Hard Information

- shedding occurs from sick, acute, and carrier cats
- infection occurs mainly by direct contact
- supportive care and nursing are essential

## Theme: PCR positivity is bounded by carrier-state biology

The guideline gives one of the clearest reusable cautions in the FCV seed set: detection can be real while causal interpretation still needs context.

### Hard Information

- virus isolation or RT-PCR can be used for diagnosis
- positive PCR requires caution because persistently infected carriers may shed at low levels

## Theme: Environment and disinfection belong in prevention language

This source stops prevention from being reduced to vaccination alone. Environmental persistence makes hygiene a real prevention branch.

### Hard Information

- FCV can persist in the environment for about one month
- the virus is resistant to many common disinfectants
