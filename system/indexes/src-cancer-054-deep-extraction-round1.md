# Deep Extraction Worksheet: src-cancer-054

**Source:** First description of feline inflammatory mammary carcinoma: clinicopathological and immunohistochemical characteristics of three cases
**Journal:** Breast Cancer Research (2004)
**DOI:** 10.1186/bcr790
**PMID:** 15217496
**Evidence Level:** case-series (first description, n=3)

## Phase 0: Context

**Access status:** Open access via BioMed Central.

**Source scope:** 2004 case series establishing feline inflammatory mammary carcinoma (IMC) as a distinct clinical entity. First description of this aggressive phenotype in cats.

**Key contribution:** Landmark paper defining feline IMC clinical/pathological characteristics.

**Critical boundary:** Only 3 cases. Descriptive only. Not prevalence or treatment guidance.

## Phase 2: Theme Reconstruction

### Theme A: IMC as Distinct Entity

Feline IMC defined by:
- Clinical inflammatory syndrome
- Absence of palpable tumor mass
- Dermal lymphatic invasion
- Postsurgical (secondary) presentation
- Extremely poor prognosis

**Parallel:** Human inflammatory breast cancer.

### Theme B: Clinical Recognition

Warning signs for IMC:
- Rapid postoperative inflammatory changes
- Diffuse erythema and edema
- Extreme pain out of proportion
- Suture rejection
- No distinct mass on palpation

### Theme C: Dismal Prognosis

All 3 cats died/euthanized rapidly:
- Days to weeks survival
- Metastatic at necropsy
- No effective treatment described

**Clinical implication:** IMC diagnosis indicates very poor prognosis.

### Theme D: Comparative Oncology

Feline IMC parallels human IBC:
- Lymphatic tumor embolization
- Inflammatory clinical syndrome
- Aggressive behavior
- Potential research model
