## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: CKD and hypertension are bidirectional

- core_claim: CKD and hypertension have an intermingled cause-and-effect relationship.
- implicit_premise: blood pressure is not a peripheral complication variable.
- relation_to_previous: opening comorbidity frame.
- hard_details: prevalence `~20% to 65%`.
- tension_or_surprise: hypertension is both consequence and driver.

#### Unit 2: Target-organ damage widens the stakes

- core_claim: hypertensive injury to eye, brain, heart, and kidney materially affects welfare.
- implicit_premise: blood pressure control is a disease-burden issue, not just a number target.
- relation_to_previous: broadens the hypertension branch.
- hard_details: eye; brain; heart; kidney.
- tension_or_surprise: CKD blood pressure control is partly extra-renal.

#### Unit 3: Proteinuria sits inside the hemodynamic branch

- core_claim: hypertension drives proteinuria, and proteinuria is an independent risk factor for progression and mortality.
- implicit_premise: proteinuria and SBP should not be separated too far.
- relation_to_previous: introduces the junction logic.
- hard_details: target SBP `<160 mmHg`.
- tension_or_surprise: proteinuria is not only renal-injury signal but also hemodynamic consequence.

#### Unit 4: Monitoring is underused despite clear relevance

- core_claim: blood pressure monitoring in CKD cats is still infrequently performed.
- implicit_premise: implementation gaps are part of translational strategy.
- relation_to_previous: moves from branch importance to real-world weakness.
- hard_details: `amlodipine`, `telmisartan`.
- tension_or_surprise: good branch logic does not guarantee field execution.
