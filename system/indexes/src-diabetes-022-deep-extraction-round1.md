# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-diabetes-022`  
Title: `Use of a high-protein diet in the management of feline diabetes mellitus`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on PubMed abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata.

## Core Takeaway

`a small high-protein/low-carbohydrate diet study supports reduced insulin requirements without loss of fructosamine-based control, but sample size and design keep it below diet-rule status`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- high-protein / low-carbohydrate diet branch
- 9 cats completing protocol
- insulin-requirement reduction and fructosamine control

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract describes adult diabetic cats transitioned from a high-fiber moderate-fat diet to a high-protein low-carbohydrate canned diet.
- Nine cats completed the study protocol.
- Insulin levels were decreased in 8/9 cats after transition.
- Insulin injections were stopped in 3 cats.
- Regression analysis suggested exogenous insulin could be reduced by more than 50% with no loss of fructosamine-based glucose control.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source adds protein emphasis to the diet architecture.
- It should be integrated with low-carbohydrate and high-fiber comparisons, not treated alone.
- It supports diet effects on insulin requirement, not a universal remission protocol.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not establish broad generalizability because only 9 cats completed the protocol.
- It does not isolate protein from carbohydrate reduction.
