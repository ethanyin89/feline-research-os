## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- DPP-4 inhibitor mention

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract reviews incretin hormones, GLP-1 analog pharmacology, and recent research in cats.
- GLP-1 effects include delayed gastric emptying, increased satiety, augmented insulin secretion during hyperglycemia, and glucagon suppression.
- Long-acting GLP-1 analogs are framed as potentially less frequent injections than insulin in diabetic cats.
- DPP-4 inhibitors and other investigational modalities are briefly discussed.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source should be used as a frontier-treatment map, not current standard-of-care ranking.
- GLP-1/DPP-4 should remain separate from SGLT2, insulin, and diet branches.
- The source helps explain why beta-cell residual function matters in newer therapies.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not establish a routine feline GLP-1 protocol.
- It does not prove superiority over insulin.
- It does not replace newer SGLT2 treatment and safety literature.
