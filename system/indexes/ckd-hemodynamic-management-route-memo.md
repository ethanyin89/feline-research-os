# CKD Hemodynamic Management Route Memo

Date: 2026-04-08  
Scope: hemodynamic-management archetype viewed through current route-level regulatory framework

This memo is the second product-specific route memo in the CKD vault.

It uses a more drug-like CKD product archetype than the renal-diet memo:

- antihypertensive management in feline CKD with hypertension

This page is an English route memo.
It is a route-fit working document, not a final jurisdiction recommendation and not a bilingual summary.

## Product Definition Used Here

### Product archetype

- hemodynamic management product
- veterinary medicine-style asset for cats with CKD and hypertension

### Best-fit claim style in the current vault

- blood pressure control
- reduction of target-organ-damage risk
- management of a high-risk CKD subgroup

### Claim style to avoid for now

- broad disease-modification claims
- strong renal-survival claims beyond the current corpus

## Why This Archetype Fits The Current Regulatory Corpus Better

- current route-level official sources are already medicine-centered
- the archetype has a clearer subgroup than renal diet
- the archetype has a clearer operational endpoint than many other CKD branches
- the current treatment corpus already names specific agents and blood-pressure targets

## What This Memo Changes

This memo shows the opposite pattern from the renal-diet route memo:

`the archetype is not the strongest treatment branch overall, but it is a better fit for the current regulatory corpus`

That makes this a more useful stress test for route logic.

## Best Current Internal Read

### What we can say now

- hemodynamic management is not the strongest treatment tier overall, but it is one of the cleanest medicine-style archetypes
- current FDA, EMA, and VMD route-level sources are materially more informative for this archetype than they were for renal diet
- subgroup definition plus clear endpoint logic make this a better candidate for jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction route thinking

### What we should not say now

- do not claim that a final jurisdiction recommendation is already available
- do not claim conditional approval, limited-market status, or a specific MA route is already justified for this exact CKD indication
- do not upgrade blood-pressure control into a proven broad renal-outcome package without more direct evidence
- do not read this memo as proof that subgroup clarity has already solved the evidentiary problem

## Route Readiness Ranking For This Archetype

### Highest current readiness

- FDA / USA

Reason:

- the current official-source pack already supports approval-path forks and study-design thinking for medicine-style products

### Medium current usefulness

- EMA / EU
- VMD / UK

Reason:

- both are more usable for this archetype than for renal diet, but still remain route-level rather than indication-specific

### Lower current clarity

- China

Reason:

- framework exists, but product-category detail and dossier expectations remain thin in the current vault

## Decision Logic This Memo Supports

1. if you want to test current regulatory-source usefulness, use a drug-like archetype first
2. if the claim is primarily blood-pressure control in CKD cats, the route discussion becomes much cleaner
3. if the claim expands into broad renoprotection or progression slowing, evidence problems reappear quickly
4. jurisdiction ranking should follow claim discipline, not precede it

## Most Important New Insight

The current regulatory corpus is not equally useful for all CKD product classes.

It is substantially better aligned to:

- subgrouped
- endpoint-driven
- medicine-style

CKD products than to nutrition-positioned archetypes.

That is a comparative route-fit conclusion, not a decision-grade regulatory recommendation.
