# IBD Claim-Fit Route-Fit Boundary Memo

- Date: `2026-04-10`
- Scope: product-archetype boundary work for later IBD regulatory compression

This memo exists to stop the current IBD regulatory branch from drifting into one vague sentence like:

`the cleanest product is the best route`

That sentence sounds practical.

It is not safe enough for this vault.

## Core Takeaway

`for IBD, claim-fit and route-fit should be read as separate pressures before any route-level memo is attempted`

The current module is strong enough to separate those two pressures.

It is not strong enough to collapse them.

## Current Boundary Read

### Diet-First Chronic-Enteropathy Management

- strongest present archetype overall
- cleaner on claim-fit than on route-fit
- still burdened by chronic-enteropathy overlap when route language gets too specific

### Broad Immunomodulatory Or Medical Management

- weaker present claim-fit than the diet branch
- may later become a cleaner route-fit candidate
- must not be mistaken for evidence superiority

### Microbiota-Directed Adjunct

- unstable on claim-fit
- unstable on route-fit
- too early for route compression

### Regenerative / Stem-Cell Translational Product

- weak on both axes
- translational only

### Diagnostic Or Stratification Assay

- strategically relevant
- still too unstable on both axes for early route work

## Comparison Table

| Archetype | Claim-Fit | Route-Fit | Current Safest Read |
|---|---|---|---|
| diet-first chronic-enteropathy management | relatively cleaner | still mixed | strongest overall archetype, not yet a clean route memo |
| broad immunomodulatory or medical management | mixed | potentially cleaner later | future route-fit candidate, weaker current claim branch |
| microbiota-directed adjunct | unstable | unstable | not ready on either axis |
| regenerative / stem-cell translational product | weak | weak | translational only |
| diagnostic or stratification assay | weak-to-moderate | weak-to-moderate | strategically relevant, still not route-safe |

## What This Changes

This memo makes one point explicit:

`the next IBD regulatory gain is not a route recommendation; it is a better map of which branches are claim-cleaner and which are route-cleaner`

## What We Can Say Now

1. The diet branch remains the safest overall archetype.
2. The medicine-style branch may later become the cleaner route-fit test.
3. Those are not the same conclusion.
4. Microbiota, regenerative, and assay branches remain too unstable for early route compression.

## What We Should Not Say Now

- do not write diet-first as the already-best route candidate
- do not write medicine-style management as evidence-superior because its future route-fit may be cleaner
- do not treat claim clarity and route readiness as the same object
- do not move into jurisdiction ranking from this memo
