## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract says feline diabetes management relies on insulin therapy and controlled dietary intake.
- The abstract says management goals shifted from glycemic control toward diabetic remission.
- Published remission rates up to 68% are mentioned in the abstract.
- The abstract says low-carbohydrate foods improve remission odds, while some cats respond better to high-fiber food.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source supports low-carbohydrate diet as a serious branch, not a universal rule.
- It explicitly preserves individual clinical judgment.
- It should be paired with `src-diabetes-015` and `src-diabetes-007`.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not eliminate high-fiber diet as an option.
- It does not provide a universal remission prediction rule.
