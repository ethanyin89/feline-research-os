Title: `Endoneurial microvascular pathology in feline diabetic neuropathy`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on PubMed/PMC abstract-level evidence and source-card metadata.

## Core Takeaway

`feline diabetic neuropathy includes endoneurial microvascular pathology, strengthening the complication branch beyond clinical gait signs`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- microvascular pathology branch
- 12 diabetic cats compared with 7 nondiabetic cats
- capillary size, luminal area, and vasoconstriction-index findings
- bridge to human/rodent diabetic neuropathy pathology

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract reports nerve biopsies from 12 diabetic cats and comparison nerves from 7 nondiabetic cats.
- Diabetic cats had elevated long-term glycemic markers, impaired motor nerve conduction, and decreased myelinated nerve fiber density.
- Capillary size and luminal area were significantly increased in diabetic cats.
- A vasoconstriction index was significantly decreased.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Neuropathy has microvascular correlates in cats.
- This source should be paired with `src-diabetes-004` for the complication mechanism branch.
- Complication pages should include tissue pathology, not only clinical signs.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not establish a treatment for neuropathy.
- It does not show whether microvascular changes are reversible.
