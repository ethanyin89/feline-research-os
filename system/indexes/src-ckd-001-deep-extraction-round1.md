## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Fibrosis is the most stable mechanism backbone

This review is important because it provides a stable mechanism center even while admitting that much upstream pathogenesis is extrapolated from human and experimental animal work. The safest first-wave mechanism reading is therefore not a fragile causal story, but a fibrosis-centered disease frame.

### Hard Information

- common final outcome: `tubulointerstitial fibrosis`
- upstream understanding still partly extrapolated

## Theme: Progression-linked variables are cross-layer objects

Proteinuria, phosphate, and creatinine are not just things to measure. In this review they act as disease-worsening variables and therefore belong in endpoint, prognosis, and translation logic simultaneously.

### Hard Information

- progression-linked factors: `proteinuria`, `plasma phosphate`, `plasma creatinine`

## Theme: Screening pressure comes from late visible disease

Because clinical signs usually appear late, the review naturally supports risk-based earlier recognition. This is one of the reasons CKD in this vault should be read as a serial-surveillance problem rather than a one-time diagnostic event.

### Hard Information

- signs usually occur in later stages
- high-risk settings include hypertension and hyperthyroidism
