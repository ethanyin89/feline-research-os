## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: This paper is a breadth paper, not a field-effectiveness paper

- core_claim: the study asks how vaccine antisera neutralise a panel of current UK FCV isolates in vitro.
- implicit_premise: cross-neutralisation breadth is one endpoint family, not a whole vaccine verdict.
- relation_to_previous: opening endpoint separation.
- hard_details: the source card preserves F9 antiserum neutralising 87.5% of tested isolates and strain 255 antiserum 75%.
- tension_or_surprise: the result is positive enough to matter but incomplete enough to block overclaim.

#### Unit 2: Broad coverage is real, but not complete

- core_claim: currently used FCV vaccine strains showed broad cross-reactivity against sampled field isolates, but not all isolates were neutralised.
- implicit_premise: FCV breadth language should be `broad but incomplete`, not `covers circulating strains`.
- relation_to_previous: sharpens the main take-home claim.
- hard_details: some isolates were not neutralised by either antiserum.
- tension_or_surprise: this is exactly the sort of paper that gets flattened into `vaccines still work`.

#### Unit 3: Geography and time remain part of the claim boundary

- core_claim: the paper is anchored to contemporary UK veterinary-submitted isolates, not a universal FCV map.
- implicit_premise: isolate fit is local and temporal, not automatically stable across countries or eras.
- relation_to_previous: sets the outer boundary.
- hard_details: the source card preserves a sampled UK veterinary-visiting population with FCV isolated from 122 of 1206 swabs.
- tension_or_surprise: even a useful breadth paper doubles as a reminder that field panels are contingent.

#### Unit 4: Neutralisation breadth should sit below challenge and persistence branches

- core_claim: the study is powerful for cross-neutralisation architecture, but weaker for challenge protection, disease reduction, or carrier-state control.
- implicit_premise: endpoint hierarchy matters more than whether the result sounds optimistic.
- relation_to_previous: defines the paper's module role.
- hard_details: the main preserved result is in-vitro antisera neutralisation, not longitudinal disease or carriage outcomes.
- tension_or_surprise: the paper often sounds stronger than it really is because the percentages are memorable.

#### Unit 5: This paper stabilizes one branch of the FCV vaccine map

- core_claim: `src-fcv-003` should anchor the breadth side of the FCV vaccine branch and be paired with challenge, cellular-immunity, and persistence papers.
- implicit_premise: no single vaccine paper can own FCV control language.
- relation_to_previous: places the source in the module.
- hard_details: breadth here complements `src-fcv-011`, `src-fcv-017`, and `src-fcv-022`.
- tension_or_surprise: the safest role is not `lead vaccine paper`, but `lead breadth paper`.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: FCV vaccine breadth is defensible but bounded

This paper gives the module one of its clearest field-isolate breadth anchors. It supports saying FCV vaccines can retain broad cross-reactivity against contemporary isolates while still leaving room for non-covered strains and drift pressure.

### Hard Information

- F9 antiserum neutralised 87.5% of tested isolates
- strain 255 antiserum neutralised 75% of tested isolates
- some isolates were not neutralised by either antiserum

## Theme: Neutralisation breadth is not the same as protection closure

The paper belongs high in endpoint architecture because it helps separate one family of evidence from others. It is strong enough to support `broad but incomplete coverage`, but not strong enough to own challenge, field-effectiveness, or carrier-state language.

### Hard Information

- in-vitro antisera neutralisation design
- current UK field-isolate panel
- no direct persistence or field-effectiveness endpoint in the preserved source layer

## Theme: FCV control claims must stay geography-aware

This source also blocks a lazy universal reading. The panel is UK-specific and era-specific, so it should support local breadth logic, not global or timeless coverage language.

### Hard Information

- sampled UK veterinary-visiting population
- prevalence measured inside that sampling frame

**Claim 2**
