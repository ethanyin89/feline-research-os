## Core Takeaway

`feline diabetes is useful as a type-2 diabetes model because it shares clinical, physiologic, and pathologic features with human T2DM while remaining a naturally occurring feline disease`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- comparative model branch
- obesity / declining insulin secretion / islet amyloid / beta-cell loss frame
- complication-system link, including neuropathy and retinopathy

## Source-Supported Facts

- The abstract frames feline diabetes as closely resembling human type 2 diabetes.
- Shared features include middle-age onset, obesity association, residual but declining insulin secretion, islet amyloid deposits, beta-cell mass loss, and organ-system complications.
- The abstract notes that pathological aspects can be experimentally induced.
- The abstract positions feline obesity physiology as useful for comparative human T2DM studies.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This is the model-value anchor for the diabetes module.
- Model claims should stay separate from direct clinical recommendations.
- The source supports keeping neuropathy/retinopathy in the complication map.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not make every human T2DM intervention transferable to cats.
- It does not replace feline clinical trials for treatment decisions.
