#### Unit 3: FCV is broader than a simple oral/URTD virus

- core_claim: although FCV is commonly framed as an oral and upper-respiratory pathogen, the review keeps gastrointestinal origin history, lower respiratory disease, limping syndrome, virulent systemic disease, and other biotypes visible.
- implicit_premise: FCV mechanism must allow multiple tissue and syndrome branches.

- hard_details: the section map explicitly includes limping disease, lower respiratory tract disease, virulent systemic disease, and other biotypes.
- tension_or_surprise: a narrow respiratory-only summary will understate the model this review is actually supporting.

#### Unit 4: Receptor/tropism and entry belong in the same story

- core_claim: host range, tissue tropism, receptors, and virus entry are a distinct FCV mechanism layer.
- implicit_premise: tissue reach is part of why FCV can produce heterogeneous clinical manifestations.
- relation_to_previous: shifts from phenotype breadth to mechanistic plausibility.
- hard_details: the review explicitly separates host range, tissue tropism, receptors, and virus entry as a major section.
- tension_or_surprise: this is why receptor-distribution papers should sit inside the mechanism spine rather than as detached extension sources.

#### Unit 5: Genomic structure, capsid variation, and replication explain control difficulty

- core_claim: ORF structure, VP1/VP2 organization, VPg-linked translation, replication complexes, and capsid antigenic variation all sit under the same FCV control problem.
- implicit_premise: vaccine pressure and outbreak behavior cannot be understood without replication and capsid logic.
- relation_to_previous: links molecular biology to the downstream vaccine/control branch.
- hard_details: the accessible summary describes a genome of about 7.7 kb, ORF1 polyprotein processing, subgenomic RNA encoding ORF2/ORF3, VP1/VP2 capsid structure, and VPg-driven translation.
- tension_or_surprise: this source upgrades the mechanism branch from `virus is variable` to `control difficulty is built into viral architecture`.
