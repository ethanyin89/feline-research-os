# Structured Abstract Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-ckd-034`
Title: Risk and protective factors for cats with naturally occurring chronic kidney disease
Journal: Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery
Year: 2017
DOI: 10.1177/1098612x15625453
Extraction Round: 2 (full Crossref abstract)

## Study Design

- Study type: age-matched case-control study
- Sample: 130 cats (101 CKD, 29 clinically normal controls aged ≥5 years)
- Data collection: questionnaire interviews, June 2004 to November 2014
- Location: Small Animal Hospital, Chulalongkorn University and veterinary hospitals in Bangkok Metropolitan area
- Analysis: univariable and multivariable (logistic regression with backward elimination)

## Key Quantitative Findings

### Univariable Analysis (Risk Factors)

- Male sex: associated with increased CKD risk
- Tap water: associated with increased CKD risk
- Outdoor lifestyle: associated with increased CKD risk

### Univariable Analysis (Protective Factors)

- Commercial dry cat food: associated with decreased CKD risk
- Filtered water: associated with decreased CKD risk
- Indoor lifestyle: associated with decreased CKD risk

### Multivariable Analysis

- Only commercial dry cat food remained statistically significant
- Cats fed commercial dry cat food had significantly decreased CKD risk compared with other diets

## Core Claims

### Claim 1: Commercial dry cat food may protect against CKD

- Support: multivariable logistic regression showed significant protective effect
- Implicit premise: diet composition or formulation matters for long-term kidney health
- Evidence level: B (source-supported conclusion from case-control design)
- Important caveat: "may be a potential protective factor" — not a proven causal claim

### Claim 2: Male sex, tap water, and outdoor lifestyle are univariable risk factors

- Support: univariable analysis showed association
- Implicit premise: these factors did not survive multivariable adjustment
- Evidence level: C (association that did not survive adjustment)

### Claim 3: Indoor lifestyle, filtered water may be protective

- Support: univariable association
- Implicit premise: confounded with diet in multivariable model
- Evidence level: C (did not survive adjustment)

## Boundary / Limits

- Case-control design with retrospective questionnaire; recall bias possible
- Small control group (n=29) limits statistical power
- Single geographic region (Bangkok); external validity unconfirmed
- 10-year data collection span may introduce temporal confounding
- "Commercial dry cat food" is a heterogeneous category; formulation details unknown
- Cannot isolate specific dietary components (protein, phosphorus, moisture, etc.)
- Does not distinguish between preventing CKD onset vs delaying progression

## Claim-Fit for CKD Module

### Safe to use

- Risk factor architecture framing: male sex, outdoor lifestyle, tap water as univariable signals
- Diet as a modifiable factor hypothesis
- Evidence that dietary factors deserve further investigation

### Not safe to use yet

- Universal dry food recommendation
- Causal claims about specific dietary interventions
- Risk ranking without replication in other populations
