## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: The paper broadens renal-injury logic beyond angiotensin II alone

- core_claim: aldosterone/MR signaling deserves to be treated as a serious renal-injury pathway, not a minor appendage to ACEI/ARB thinking.
- implicit_premise: RAAS injury is broader than classic angiotensin-II-centric framing.
- relation_to_previous: opening translational premise.
- hard_details: MR activation is supported as a powerful mediator of renal damage; strongest mechanisms include vasculopathy, oxidative stress, inflammation, and profibrotic growth-factor signaling.
- tension_or_surprise: this review is pushing the vault from a fibrosis-centric lesion map toward a more explicit mediator map.

#### Unit 2: Experimental support is stronger than feline clinical proof

- core_claim: MR antagonism has meaningful support in animal models and human CKD, but feline direct clinical validation remains limited.
- implicit_premise: therapeutic plausibility and clinical proof are not the same thing.
- relation_to_previous: defines the evidence-quality gradient.
- hard_details: spironolactone and eplerenone abrogate renal injury in model systems; human CKD trials show benefit on proteinuria and cardiovascular events, but not on mortality or progression endpoints.
- tension_or_surprise: the therapeutic-target story sounds strong until the endpoint ceiling is stated explicitly.

#### Unit 3: Feline CKD is similar enough to care, but not similar enough to assume

- core_claim: feline CKD shares relevant pathology with human disease, especially tubulointerstitial fibrosis, which makes MR biology worth examining.
- implicit_premise: shared fibrosis architecture creates translational relevance.
- relation_to_previous: explains why the review is talking about cats at all.
- hard_details: feline CKD shares many characteristics with human disease including tubulointerstitial fibrosis.
- tension_or_surprise: similarity justifies attention, not automatic adoption.

#### Unit 4: Experimental feline RAAS activation exists, but natural-disease signal is mixed

- core_claim: feline RAAS activation is supported in induced-CKD models, but naturally occurring CKD data are less clean.
- implicit_premise: biomarker and pathway behavior can differ between induced and spontaneous disease.
- relation_to_previous: moves from broad plausibility to species-specific caution.
- hard_details: plasma renin, aldosterone, angiotensin I, and angiotensin II increase after feline renal ischaemia/reperfusion injury; plasma renin activity and aldosterone may not differ in normotensive azotaemic CKD cats versus controls.
- tension_or_surprise: this is exactly the kind of mixed signal that should keep the branch below first-line promotion.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: MR is a credible second-generation mediator branch

This paper is valuable because it makes aldosterone/MR signaling a credible mechanism branch under the fibrosis-centered CKD map. It connects vascular injury, oxidative stress, inflammation, and fibroblast/ECM dysregulation into one candidate pathway set.

### Hard Information

- aldosterone-induced vasculopathy
- oxidative stress
- inflammation
- growth-factor signaling
- fibroblast proliferation

## Theme: MR antagonism is translationally interesting but not validated feline standard care

The review clearly points toward MR antagonists as a promising strategy, but the evidence grade remains below what would justify strong feline treatment promotion. This is a watchlist target branch, not a first-wave intervention tier.

### Hard Information

- spironolactone/eplerenone protective in models
- human benefit on proteinuria and cardiovascular events
- no hard mortality/progression endpoint proof

## Theme: Feline signal exists, but naturally occurring disease remains harder to read

The feline-specific section is useful because it prevents overpromotion. Experimental feline CKD shows RAAS activation, but natural-disease aldosterone results are not uniformly elevated, which means the pathway probably matters without being a simple circulating-biomarker story.

### Hard Information

- experimental feline RAAS activation after ischaemia/reperfusion
- no difference in plasma renin activity/aldosterone in one normotensive natural-CKD comparison

- implicit_premise: induced-pathway activation does not guarantee simple biomarker elevation in spontaneous disease
