# src-ibd-007 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`shared molecular disturbance is not the same thing as disease-class separation`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first duodenal gene-expression anchor in the IBD-versus-lymphoma boundary branch
- support for a shared chronic-enteropathy molecular abnormality layer
- a caution against overreading COX2 or MDR1 mRNA as practical disease separators

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 20 cats with IBD, 9 cats with low-grade alimentary lymphoma, and 3 healthy controls.
- The abstract states that MDR1 mRNA levels were lower in both IBD and lymphoma than in healthy cats.
- The abstract states that there was no difference in COX2 mRNA levels between groups.
- The abstract reports no correlation between gene expression, clinical signs, histologic severity, or between genes.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- MDR1 downregulation appears to behave more like a shared enteropathy-associated disturbance than a boundary discriminator.
- COX2 mRNA level does not currently support a strong practical separation between IBD and lymphoma.
- Molecular expression should remain beneath biopsy, histopathology, imaging, and site-selection logic in the workup hierarchy.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove that MDR1 or COX2 mRNA can distinguish IBD from lymphoma in practice.
- It does not show that these expression changes track disease severity well.
- It does not replace tissue diagnosis.
