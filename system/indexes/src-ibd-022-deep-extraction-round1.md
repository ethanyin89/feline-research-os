# src-ibd-022 Deep Extraction Round 1

## Core Takeaway

`fibrosis is a real chronicity-and-burden branch, not a minor pathology footnote`

## What This Source Most Strongly Adds

- the first structural chronicity anchor in the IBD module
- support for modeling fibrosis as part of disease burden and not just descriptive histology
- a bridge between chronic inflammatory enteropathy and tissue-remodeling architecture

## Quoted / Near-Quoted Facts

- The abstract reports 65 client-owned cats diagnosed with chronic inflammatory enteropathy.
- Intestinal fibrosis was identified in 38.5% to 100% of duodenal biopsy specimens and 65.2% to 100% of colonic biopsy specimens depending on detection method.
- Cats with intestinal fibrosis had significantly lower body weight and lower serum albumin concentration than cats without fibrosis.
- The abstract concludes that intestinal fibrosis should be considered an important part of histopathologic assessment in cats with chronic inflammatory enteropathy.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- Fibrosis belongs in a chronicity-and-burden branch adjacent to but not identical with routine inflammatory activity.
- Structural remodeling should not be flattened into the same layer as support biomarkers.
- Chronic enteropathy architecture in cats becomes more defensible when fibrosis is explicitly modeled.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove fibrosis cleanly separates IBD from lymphoma.
- It does not establish a specific antifibrotic treatment pathway.
- It does not prove every cat with chronic enteropathy has clinically meaningful fibrosis progression.
