## Comparison Analysis

### What src-ckd-029 Provides

- A 360-day phosphorus-control signal in advanced CKD (IRIS stage 3-4)
- Treated-group mean phosphorus decreased from 7.72 to 3.76 mg/dL
- Bicarbonate and ionized calcium monitoring data
- Completion and palatability signals (unblinded)

### What src-ckd-029 Does NOT Provide

- Survival benefit
- Renal recovery or disease modification
- Comparative binder-class ranking
- Ingredient-specific attribution (multi-ingredient product)
- Randomized or blinded assignment
- Independence from manufacturer conflict

### Key Quotes from Independent Sources

**src-ckd-003:**
> "It is unknown whether phosphate binders provide comparable survival benefit to a renal diet."

**src-ckd-006:**
> "Few published veterinary reports focus on the safety and efficacy of intestinal phosphate binders."
> "No phosphorus binders are licensed as medications for dogs or cats."
> "Dietary phosphorus restriction has stronger current support than phosphate binders in the feline evidence base."

**src-ckd-007:**
> This review imposes evidence-grading discipline and explicitly separates widespread practice from stronger evidence.

## Verdict

**src-ckd-029 does NOT upgrade the current treatment hierarchy.**

The existing vault position is correct:

1. **Diet-first phosphorus control** remains the baseline-supported intervention
2. **Phosphate binders** remain adjunctive when diet alone is insufficient
3. **Survival benefit** from binders is not established
4. **Product-specific or binder-class recommendations** are not justified

### What src-ckd-029 Can Support

- A guarded source-specific phosphorus endpoint signal
- The need for calcium and bicarbonate monitoring when calcium-containing binders are used
- An example of evidence-quality limitations in product-like CKD interventions

### What src-ckd-029 Cannot Support

- Product recommendation or dosing
- Comparative phosphate-binder ranking
- Survival or disease-modification claims
- Use in early-stage CKD (study was IRIS stage 3-4 only)

## Treatment Write-Back Decision

**No treatment write-back is justified from src-ckd-029 at this time.**

The source is correctly placed:
- `verification_status: deep_extracted`
- `decision_grade: no`
- Claim-fit judgment in the source card already constrains safe use

The synthesis-index treatment hierarchy is unaffected:
- S4 ("Renal diet is the clearest baseline-supported intervention") remains valid
- Phosphate binders remain in "Important but bounded adjunct branches"

## Remaining Phosphate-Binder Evidence Gap

For any future treatment write-back on phosphate binders, the vault would need:

1. Independent randomized controlled trial(s) in cats
2. Survival or disease-progression endpoint data
3. Ingredient-specific or binder-class comparison
4. Feline licensing pathway or regulatory precedent

None of these are currently available in the CKD corpus.

## One-Sentence Summary

src-ckd-029 provides a useful phosphorus-monitoring signal but does not justify upgrading binders above diet or adding product recommendations to treatment pages.
