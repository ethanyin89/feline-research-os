# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-ckd-014`  
Title: `Clinical management of feline chronic kidney disease in Portugal: a questionnaire-based study`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on accessible abstract and results summary text.

## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: Guideline awareness is high, but execution is uneven

- core_claim: many veterinarians know and report using IRIS guidance, but important parts of recommended management are still inconsistently implemented.
- implicit_premise: implementation gaps matter as much as awareness when translating CKD care into reality.
- relation_to_previous: opening practice-reality frame.
- hard_details: 92.7% reported IRIS guideline use; only 19.1% systematically measured SBP in all CKD cats.
- tension_or_surprise: the gap between awareness and execution is one of the highest-value findings here.

#### Unit 2: Renal diet dominates practice, but adherence and deployment are messy

- core_claim: renal diet is nearly universal in recommendation, but actual intake and rollout are often imperfect.
- implicit_premise: “recommended” does not mean “implemented effectively.”
- relation_to_previous: moves from monitoring gap to treatment-pattern gap.
- hard_details: 99.3% advised renal diet; 36.9% said it represented less than 75% of daily intake for most patients; often prescribed regardless of stage and without proper gradual transition.
- tension_or_surprise: even the strongest intervention in the vault carries real implementation friction.

#### Unit 3: Common prescribing patterns extend beyond strongest-evidence anchors

- core_claim: appetite stimulants, ACE inhibitors, calcium channel blockers, and phosphate binders are all frequently used in practice.
- implicit_premise: field behavior is broader and more heterogeneous than the evidence backbone alone would suggest.
- relation_to_previous: widens the implementation picture.
- hard_details: appetite stimulants 89.5%, ACE inhibitors 59.9%, calcium channel blockers 41.8%, phosphate binders 76.0%.
- tension_or_surprise: common use clearly outruns strongest current evidence in several branches.

#### Unit 4: Owner constraints are a translational barrier

- core_claim: recommended follow-up frequency is not always achievable because of owner-side constraints.
- implicit_premise: translational planning must include adherence and logistics, not only clinical idealism.
- relation_to_previous: defines the real-world ceiling.
- hard_details: 70.9% recommended reassessment every 2-3 months or more frequently, but only 35.7% could comply with that periodicity because of owner constraints.
- tension_or_surprise: the limiting factor is often implementation environment, not clinician intent.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Practice reality diverges from evidence and guideline ideal

This paper is useful because it quantifies the gap between what the vault treats as high-value logic and what gets carried out consistently in real clinics. Blood-pressure measurement is the clearest example.

### Hard Information

- 409 respondents
- 92.7% guideline use
- 19.1% systematic SBP measurement

## Theme: Renal diet is both dominant and operationally fragile

Renal diet remains the dominant practice intervention, which matches the vault's evidence hierarchy. But implementation is still weaker than the recommendation rate suggests because stage-independent use, poor transition, and limited intake are common.

### Hard Information

- 99.3% renal diet advised
- 36.9% intake <75% of daily food
- 73.4% advised regardless of stage

## Theme: Translation must include owner-side implementation friction

This paper adds a valuable translational constraint: even when the clinical plan is clear, follow-up cadence and diet adherence may fail because owners cannot sustain the recommended routine.

### Hard Information

- 70.9% recommended 2-3 month or more frequent reassessment
- 35.7% able to comply due to owner constraints
