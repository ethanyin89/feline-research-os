## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: The paper asks whether quantitative morphometry can really distinguish HCM

- core_claim: morphometry is being tested as a diagnostic discriminator between HCM and normal feline hearts.
- implicit_premise: structural recognition can be sharpened quantitatively rather than relying on impressionistic pathology.
- relation_to_previous: opening morphometry rule.
- hard_details: the summary states the aim was to identify diagnostically relevant morphometric criteria clearly distinguishing HCM from normal hearts.
- tension_or_surprise: the paper is testing which quantitative layer is actually useful, not just assuming all morphometry helps.

#### Unit 2: Gross morphometric differences are real

- core_claim: relative heart weight and several macrostructural areas are larger in HCM hearts.
- implicit_premise: gross structural measurements belong in the phenotype-definition branch.
- relation_to_previous: moves from study aim to positive result.
- hard_details: the summary reports increased relative heart weights and significantly increased ventricular-wall and septal areas in 15 HCM hearts compared with 15 controls.
- tension_or_surprise: macrostructure performs better than the micro-level many would expect.

#### Unit 3: Micromorphometric histology disappoints as a discriminator

- core_claim: histological morphometry did not show significant differences in cardiomyocyte size, branching, or fibrosis in this dataset.
- implicit_premise: not every plausible microscopic metric deserves diagnostic authority.
- relation_to_previous: constrains the enthusiasm generated by gross morphometry.
- hard_details: the summary reports no significant microscopic differences in cardiomyocyte diameter, area, branching, or fibrosis.
- tension_or_surprise: the study pushes back against the idea that myocyte hypertrophy is the principal measurable diagnostic change.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Morphometry is useful, but mainly at the macrostructural level

This paper is important because it prevents the endpoint branch from overvaluing cell-level micromorphometry. Gross structure looks more operational than histologic micro-measures here.

### Hard Information

- increased relative heart weight in HCM
- increased macrostructural ventricular and septal areas
- no significant histologic micromorphometric differences

## Theme: Diagnostic authority should not be granted to every plausible pathology metric

The study acts as a boundary paper against overcompression of histologic features into routine diagnostic criteria.

### Hard Information

- cardiomyocyte-size and fibrosis measures did not clearly separate groups in this dataset
