# CKD Renal Diet Route Memo

Date: 2026-04-08  
Scope: renal-diet archetype viewed through current route-level regulatory framework

This memo is the first product-specific route memo in the CKD vault.

It does not claim that current official sources already define a feline CKD renal-diet approval package in each jurisdiction.

It does something narrower:

- tests how one clean product archetype behaves across the current route map
- identifies where route-level framing is already useful
- identifies what still blocks a true jurisdiction recommendation

This page is an English route memo.
It is a route-fit working document, not a jurisdiction recommendation and not a bilingual-facing summary.

## Product Definition Used Here

### Product archetype

- purpose-built feline renal diet
- veterinary-positioned nutritional management product

### Best-fit claim style in the current vault

- supportive management
- progression-related burden reduction
- phosphorus-control contribution

### Claim style to avoid for now

- direct structural disease reversal
- strong disease-modification framing

## Why Renal Diet Is The Right First Route Memo

- it is the cleanest treatment archetype in the current vault
- it has the strongest current supportability among CKD interventions
- it is easier to describe operationally than most adjunct branches
- it forces an early distinction between nutrition-like and drug-like route logic

## What This Memo Changes

This memo makes one strategic point very clear:

`the best-supported treatment archetype is not automatically the best first regulatory archetype for the current official source set`

That matters because the current route-level regulatory corpus is heavily veterinary-medicine centered.

Renal diet is the clearest treatment product in the treatment layer, but it is not yet the cleanest regulatory product in the current official-source layer.

## Best Current Internal Read

### What we can say now

- renal diet is the strongest treatment archetype in the current CKD evidence layer
- route-level regulatory framing is already strong enough to show that product type matters before jurisdiction ranking
- current official regulatory sources are not yet well matched to a nutrition-positioned renal diet asset

### What we should not say now

- do not claim China, FDA, EMA, or VMD is already the best first route for a feline renal diet
- do not force a medicine-style route recommendation when the current source set may be mismatched to the archetype
- do not mistake this memo for product-category resolution; the nutrition-vs-medicine boundary is still part of the problem

## Route Readiness Ranking For This Archetype

## Decision Logic This Memo Supports

1. do not choose jurisdiction first
2. choose product category first
3. test whether the asset is being framed as:
   - veterinary medicine
   - nutrition-positioned management product
   - hybrid or borderline asset
4. only then decide which jurisdictional route memo is worth deepening

## Most Important New Insight

The first product-specific route memo reveals that:

`the current regulatory corpus is better aligned to drug-like CKD products than to renal-diet products`

So even though renal diet is the best-supported treatment branch, it is not the best archetype for stress-testing the current official regulatory source pack.

That is a route-fit observation, not a final product-strategy conclusion.
