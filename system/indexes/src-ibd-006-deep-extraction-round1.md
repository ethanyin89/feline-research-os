## Source-Supported Conclusions

- This source anchors the older FISH-based microbiota branch of feline IBD.
- The microbiota branch should be understood as a longitudinal mechanism layer spanning older targeted microbial profiling and newer frontier methods.
- Older dysbiosis studies can strengthen mechanism framing without becoming a first-line disease-class discriminator.

## What It Does Not Prove

- It does not prove microbiota changes are causal.
- It does not prove microbiota profiling is sufficient to distinguish IBD from lymphoma in routine practice.
- It does not outrank biopsy, imaging, histopathology, or biopsy-site strategy.
