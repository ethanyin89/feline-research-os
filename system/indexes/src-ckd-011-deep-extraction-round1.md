## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Fibrosis is the shared lesion-level answer when causes are still unclear

This review is valuable because it solves a specific problem in feline CKD interpretation. The upstream cause of CKD in many cats is still unknown, so if the vault insists on cause-first logic it will remain thin and unstable. The paper instead argues that renal tubulointerstitial fibrosis is the most frequently reported pathological diagnosis and the most reliable convergent lesion. That makes fibrosis a stronger organizing mechanism than any speculative initiating trigger.

### Key Quote
> the most frequently reported pathological diagnosis is renal tubulointerstitial fibrosis

### Hard Information

- ageing cats
- aetiology unknown in most cases
- renal tubulointerstitial fibrosis

## Theme: Fibrosis is relevant because it tracks function and creates a treatment target

The review goes beyond descriptive pathology and argues that fibrosis is the lesion best correlated with function in both humans and cats. It characterizes fibrosis as extracellular matrix accumulation in the interstitium and frames it as the final common pathway of kidney disease. Because of that, the paper treats fibrosis as an attractive therapeutic target and suggests that understanding the fibrotic process is necessary for future therapy development.

### Key Quote
> renal fibrosis provides an ideal target for the treatment of CKD

### Hard Information

- extracellular matrix accumulation
- final common pathway
- lesion best correlated with function

## Theme: The mediator layer is important, but species transfer must be controlled

The review explicitly admits that many fibrosis mediators are discussed in the wider literature but only a small fraction have been investigated in cats. This is exactly why the vault should elevate TGF-beta but keep a cautious tone: the review is telling us which mediator family matters conceptually, while also warning us that feline-specific validation remains limited. That means the mediator layer should be built as `promising but uneven`, not as settled cat-specific fact.

### Key Quote
