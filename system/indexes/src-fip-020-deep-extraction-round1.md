# Deep Extraction Worksheet

Source: `src-fip-020`  
Title: `Epidemiology of feline infectious peritonitis among cats examined at veterinary medical teaching hospitals`  
Method note: this worksheet is based on abstract-level epidemiologic details from PubMed and indexed abstracts, not full section-by-section extraction of the complete article text.

## Phase 0: Sequential Micro-Analysis

#### Unit 1: This is a referral-population enrichment paper

- core_claim: the study quantifies how often FIP appears in veterinary medical teaching hospital and diagnostic-laboratory populations.
- implicit_premise: risk architecture should separate broad signalment risk from referral-population enrichment.
- relation_to_previous: opening branch placement.
- hard_details: the study uses VMDB records from 1986-1995 and diagnostic-laboratory accessions from four labs.
- tension_or_surprise: this is not simply another susceptibility paper; it is a disease-burden-in-institutional-flow paper.

#### Unit 2: FIP is frequent enough in referral settings to matter operationally

- core_claim: FIP appears often enough in VMTH and diagnostic-laboratory populations to remain a high-priority disease in tertiary and necropsy-heavy settings.
- implicit_premise: recognition architecture should acknowledge institutional case enrichment, not just biological risk.
- relation_to_previous: moves from design to burden.
- hard_details: the abstract reports about 1 in 200 new feline accessions and 1 in 300 total feline accessions at VMTH, and about 1 in 100 accessions at diagnostic laboratories, represented cats with FIP.
- tension_or_surprise: the diagnostic-laboratory proportion is especially high, underscoring strong case enrichment.

#### Unit 3: The paper reinforces young, purebred, intact male enrichment

- core_claim: referral epidemiology reproduces a recognizable risk pattern rather than overturning it.
- implicit_premise: tertiary-population data can strengthen, not only distort, signalment-risk architecture.
- relation_to_previous: links institutional enrichment back to individual risk context.
- hard_details: cats with FIP were significantly more likely to be young, purebred, and sexually intact males, and less likely to be spayed females.
- tension_or_surprise: the institutional cohort still converges on a stable practical risk profile.

#### Unit 4: Temporal and regional stability matter

- core_claim: the proportion of new accessions with FIP did not vary significantly by year, month, or region of the country in this dataset.
- implicit_premise: stable burden can be more operationally important than dramatic fluctuation stories.
- relation_to_previous: adds epidemiologic consistency.
- hard_details: no significant variation among years, months, or regions in North America was reported.
- tension_or_surprise: the paper supports FIP as a persistent referral problem rather than a transient cluster pattern in this period.

#### Unit 5: This paper should anchor the institutional-risk branch, not generic prevalence

- core_claim: `src-fip-020` is best used to define referral and diagnostic-laboratory enrichment, not to stand in for general-population prevalence.
- implicit_premise: population frame matters when moving from epidemiology to recognition.
- relation_to_previous: final vault role.
- hard_details: the study is explicitly built from teaching hospitals and diagnostic laboratories rather than household/community sampling.
- tension_or_surprise: the same strength that makes this paper operationally useful also limits its generalizability.

## Phase 1: Theme Reconstruction

## Theme: Institutional epidemiology reinforces rather than replaces signalment-risk logic

The paper does not merely describe hospital bias; it shows that enriched settings still reproduce a stable young/purebred/intact-male pattern.

### Hard Information

- young cats over-represented
- purebred cats over-represented
- sexually intact males over-represented
- spayed females under-represented

## Theme: Stable burden is part of why FIP remains operationally important

This paper supports viewing FIP as a persistent referral problem rather than a purely sporadic anomaly.

### Hard Information

- no significant variation by month
- no significant variation by region

## Phase 2: Claim-Evidence Structure

### Referral-Epidemiology Key Points

**Claim 1**
- support: the paper quantifies proportions of FIP across VMTH and diagnostic laboratories
- details: this is a referral-enrichment study, not only a signalment-risk study
- implicit_premise: where cats enter care changes the practical recognition landscape

**Claim 2**
- support: diagnostic laboratories had especially high FIP proportions
- details: necropsy and histology-heavy settings concentrate disease burden
- implicit_premise: institutional flow can amplify the apparent prevalence of FIP

**Claim 3**
- support: young, purebred, intact male enrichment is reproduced in this cohort
- details: institutional data reinforce rather than erase practical early-risk weighting
- implicit_premise: signalment context remains reusable even in referral populations

### Boundary-Setting Key Points

**Claim 1**
- support: the study uses teaching-hospital and diagnostic-laboratory populations
- details: it should not be read as direct community prevalence
- implicit_premise: referral bias is part of the meaning of the results

**Claim 2**
- support: the paper finds stable proportions across time and region within the dataset
- details: this stability belongs to the studied institutional frame
- implicit_premise: stable referral burden does not automatically imply universal background prevalence
