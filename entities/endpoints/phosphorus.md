# Phosphorus

## Why It Matters

Phosphorus is one of the strongest bridge endpoints in the current CKD vault because it connects mechanism, progression, treatment, and survival-oriented interpretation.

## Current Notes

- Phosphorus should not be treated as a treatment-only marker.
- Current sources tie phosphorus to progression logic, structural injury context, and intervention decisions.
- Normal serum phosphorus does not always exclude earlier phosphate-retention biology, so interpretation needs context.
- In the current vault, phosphorus belongs in the top-tier endpoint set alongside creatinine, proteinuria, and blood pressure.
- The CKD-MBD calcium-disorder review reinforces that phosphorus belongs inside a wider mineral-regulation network that also includes PTH, FGF23, calcium homeostasis, and hypercalcaemia risk.
- Phosphate restriction remains important, but mineral management should not be framed as a one-variable optimization problem.
- Renal-diet phosphorus logic is currently stronger than binder / adjunct phosphorus logic in the feline evidence layer.
