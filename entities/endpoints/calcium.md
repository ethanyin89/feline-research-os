# Calcium

## Why It Matters

Calcium matters because feline CKD mineral burden is broader than phosphorus alone, and calcium dysregulation introduces real management tradeoffs into CKD-MBD interpretation.

## Current Notes

- Calcium belongs in the CKD-MBD interpretation branch rather than the first-wave default endpoint tier.
- The current vault supports increased hypercalcaemia risk in cats with CKD.
- Calcium should be read together with phosphorus, PTH, and broader mineral-homeostasis disturbance rather than as a standalone side marker.
- Dietary phosphate restriction may contribute to hypercalcaemia in some CKD cats, so mineral management is not a one-direction optimization problem.
- The current feline evidence layer supports calcium as a meaningful interpretive branch, but not yet as a top-tier routine CKD endpoint.
