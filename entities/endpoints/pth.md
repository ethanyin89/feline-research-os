# PTH

## Why It Matters

PTH matters because it helps explain phosphate-retention and mineral-balance disturbance before serum phosphorus alone tells the full story.

## Current Notes

- PTH is important mechanistically, especially in the phosphate-retention and secondary hyperparathyroidism discussion.
- It is not a first-wave routine endpoint in the current vault.
- Its main value right now is interpretive: it helps explain why apparently normal phosphorus does not always mean early mineral burden is absent.
- The newer CKD-MBD review makes the branch wider: PTH should now be read together with calcium dysregulation, FGF23, and broader mineral-homeostasis disturbance, not as a standalone side marker.
- PTH still belongs in a context layer rather than the first operational endpoint tier.
