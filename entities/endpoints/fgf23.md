# FGF23

## Why It Matters

FGF23 matters because it expands the feline CKD mineral branch earlier into disease evolution, potentially before overt azotaemic CKD or late-stage mineral abnormality is obvious.

## Current Notes

- FGF23 belongs in the CKD-MBD branch alongside phosphorus, PTH, and calcium dysregulation.
- The current vault supports FGF23 as an early mineral-regulator signal, not as a first-wave routine operational endpoint.
- Its main value right now is interpretive: it widens the mineral-burden story earlier than serum phosphorus alone may suggest.
- FGF23 should not yet be promoted into the top-tier endpoint set in the current vault.
- The branch remains biologically important but operationally immature.
