# USG

## Why It Matters

USG is a core practical CKD endpoint because it helps confirm renal azotemia in routine workup and supports earlier recognition when interpreted over time and in context.

## Current Notes

- USG is mandatory in the current diagnostic framing when renal azotemia is suspected.
- It is one of the most practical working markers in the current feline CKD corpus.
- USG helps earlier recognition, but it is not sufficient on its own to solve the early-detection problem.
- In the vault, USG belongs firmly in the routine operational core rather than only in a specialist workup.
