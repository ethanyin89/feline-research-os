# Feline Diabetes And Pancreatitis Comorbidity

## Question This Page Answers

How should pancreatitis be represented in the feline diabetes module? What is the relationship between diabetes and pancreatitis in cats?

## Core Takeaway

Diabetes and pancreatitis are bidirectional comorbidities that frequently coexist in cats. Concurrent pancreatitis complicates diabetes management by causing "brittle" glycemic control due to variation in pancreatic inflammation, and either disease may precede the other.

## Evidence Owners

- [src-diabetes-118](../../raw/papers/src-diabetes-118.md): **PRIMARY** — Review establishing bidirectional causality; pancreatitis causes brittle diabetes through inflammatory variation.
- [src-diabetes-010](../../raw/papers/src-diabetes-010.md): Comorbidity epidemiology anchor for feline diabetes-pancreatitis concurrence.

## Bidirectional Relationship Model

The diabetes-pancreatitis relationship is **bidirectional**:

| Direction | Evidence | Clinical Implication |
|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| **Pancreatitis → Diabetes** | Exocrine inflammation can damage endocrine tissue and impair islet function | May initiate or exacerbate diabetes |
| **Diabetes → Pancreatitis** | Hyperglycemia and metabolic changes may trigger pancreatic inflammation | Metabolic derangement complicates pancreatic health |
| **Concurrent disease** | 10-15 years of clinical observation shows frequent coexistence; heterogeneous underlying pathology | Both present simultaneously; not always clear which came first |

## Brittleness Phenotype: Key Clinical Finding

When diabetes and pancreatitis **coexist**, cats often exhibit **"brittle" glycemic control** characterized by:

- Variable glucose levels despite consistent insulin dosing
- Fluctuations driven by day-to-day variation in pancreatic inflammation
- Complication by secondary anorexia (if pancreatitis causes abdominal pain/nausea)
- More difficult clinical course than diabetes alone

**Clinical action:** Cats with difficult-to-control ("brittle") diabetes should be screened for concurrent pancreatitis; managing both conditions together may improve outcomes.

## Current Branch Meaning

Pancreatitis should appear in:

- **Risk and recognition:** May coexist with diabetes; should prompt bidirectional screening
- **Translation:** Diabetes management harder when pancreatitis coexists; consider pancreatic inflammation in resistant cases
- **Synthesis:** Blocks clean one-pathway diabetes story; requires complexity acknowledgment
- **DKA discussion:** Pancreatitis may increase management complexity; monitor both systems during crisis
- **Comorbidity monitoring:** Regular pancreatic assessment in diabetic cats with unexplained glycemic instability
