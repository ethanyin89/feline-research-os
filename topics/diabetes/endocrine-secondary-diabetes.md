# Feline Diabetes Endocrine-Secondary Disease

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This page now sits on a deep-extracted paper-card layer, but it is still not a full-text-compressed or decision-grade diagnostic or treatment-guidance page.

## Question This Page Answers

When should feline diabetes be treated as more than a default type-2-like disease frame?

## Core Takeaway

Type-2-like feline diabetes is the default frame, not the universal explanation. Hypersomatotropism, acromegaly, and other endocrinopathies need a named secondary-diabetes gate.

## Why This Branch Exists

[src-diabetes-013](../../raw/papers/src-diabetes-013.md) supports the structural point that many cats fit a type-2-like frame, while a meaningful subset have other diabetes etiologies such as hypersomatotropism, acromegaly, or hyperadrenocorticism.

[src-diabetes-020](../../raw/papers/src-diabetes-020.md) updates the branch by foregrounding hypersomatotropism-induced diabetes as a growth-hormone-related insulin-resistance problem that can be difficult to manage.

[src-diabetes-024](../../raw/papers/src-diabetes-024.md) matters because suspected hypersomatotropism exclusions affect how insulin-efficacy evidence should be read.

## Current Gate

Route to this page when the question involves:

- difficult glycemic control
- high insulin dose requirements
- variable insulin response
- unexpected non-remission
- suspected acromegalic phenotype
- treatment studies that exclude suspected hypersomatotropism cases

## Current Rule

Do not interpret insulin or diet response without checking whether secondary endocrine disease has been excluded, included, or ignored.

## Guardrail

This page preserves the secondary-diabetes gate. It does not provide a full IGF1 interpretation algorithm, diagnostic protocol, or treatment hierarchy for hypersomatotropism-induced diabetes.
