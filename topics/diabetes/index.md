## Current Conclusions

### quoted_fact

- The current source set spans pathogenesis, diet, remission, obesity, endocrine comorbidity, pancreatitis, epidemiology, treatment, models, and neuropathy.
- All 24 sources now have round-1 extraction worksheets, explicit full source-card depth, and `verification_status: deep_extracted`.

### source_supported_conclusion

- A first feline diabetes seed corpus of `24` candidate sources has now been mapped into the vault.
- Feline diabetes should be modeled as a metabolic-endocrine disease module, not only as a treatment module.
- The first-pass structure should separate pathogenesis, remission, comorbid drivers, nutrition, and drug therapy so that reversible-control language does not overrun chronic-risk language.
- Tier A extraction supports a core spine of insulin-resistance pressure plus inadequate insulin secretion, with obesity, pancreatitis, and endocrine disease as major branch pressures.
- The remission systematic review should control remission claims because the current compiled layer still reports moderate-to-poor evidence and no single predictor.
- The second extraction pass adds trial, treatment-safety, pancreatitis, and epidemiology anchors: low-carbohydrate/low-fiber diet comparison, glargine U300, SGLT2 inhibitors, diabetes-pancreatitis comorbidity, and UK/Australian Burmese risk signals.
- The completed card-level pass adds model-value, neuropathy, high-protein diet, alpha-glucosidase adjunct, older clinical-overview, species-specific pathogenesis, and 2024 hypersomatotropism-induced diabetes anchors.
- Obesity, hypersomatotropism/acromegaly, pancreatitis, diet, insulin choice, SGLT2 inhibitors, and neuropathy are likely to become separate branches rather than one blended management page.
- The memo layer now includes remission, diet architecture, endocrine-secondary diabetes, neuropathy, SGLT2 safety/regulatory boundary, SGLT2 primary FDA source control, SGLT2 current-label control, SGLT2 label-section comparison, treatment branch comparison, diagnostic/monitoring workup, obesity/body-condition, breed-risk, and pancreatitis owners.

### llm_inference

- The strongest early synthesis question is how to keep `remission potential` and `secondary endocrine/comorbidity drivers` in the same map without making either one the whole disease story.
- A good diabetes compile will probably center on `pathogenesis and remission boundaries before treatment enthusiasm`.
