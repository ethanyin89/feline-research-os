## Core Takeaway

FIP mechanism is strongest when read as a layered coronavirus-emergence problem: endemic enteric exposure creates the background, viral and host factors shape systemic spread, and overt FIP remains a clinicopathologic disease state rather than a direct synonym for any one mutation.

## Mechanism Hierarchy

### Layer 1: Enteric Coronavirus Background And FIP Emergence

FIP should begin with feline coronavirus ecology and enteric-coronavirus background, but that background is not itself the disease conclusion. The classical mutation-origin branch supports within-host emergence from endemic enteric coronavirus as the main spine.

**Lead sources:** `src-fip-003`, `src-fip-009`

**Current safe read:**
- endemic FCoV exposure is background risk, not FIP proof
- FIPV emergence belongs in the mechanism layer before it enters diagnostic interpretation
- mechanism-origin evidence should not be used as clinical certainty language

### Layer 2: Gut Competence Versus Systemic Competence

The 3c branch gives the mechanism page a functional split: intact 3c aligns with gut replication, while disrupted 3c appears compatible with systemic FIPV replication. This explains why systemic disease competence and enteric replication competence are not the same thing.

**Lead sources:** `src-fip-004`

**Current safe read:**
- gut replication and systemic disease competence should be separated
- poor gut replication of 3c-inactivated viruses helps explain why ordinary outbreak spread is not expected in most FIP settings
- 3c logic is mechanism shaping, not a one-gene diagnostic shortcut

### Layer 3: Systemic Spread Versus Overt Disease

Spike-change work sharpens the key boundary: viral changes may correlate with systemic spread without equaling overt FIP. Mutation utility papers belong here only as diagnostic-support context under the broader spread-versus-disease distinction.

**Lead sources:** `src-fip-010`, `src-fip-018`, `src-fip-022`

**Current safe read:**
- systemic spread is a crucial intermediate state
- mutation-related signals need clinicopathologic context
- mutation utility and mutation limitation must be read together

### Layer 4: Recombinant And Outbreak Pressure

The recombinant epizootic paper adds direct-transmission and recombination pressure to the mechanism map. It does not erase the classical within-host mutation model, but it prevents the vault from treating that model as the only possible emergence architecture.

**Lead sources:** `src-fip-014`

**Current safe read:**
- recombinant FCoV can create outbreak conditions outside the ordinary FIP model
- direct-transmission pressure belongs in the mechanism map
- one-route emergence stories are too brittle for FIP

### Layer 5: Effusive Immunopathology And Historical Morphogenesis

Older effusive-form immunology and experimental morphogenesis sources provide useful biological texture beneath modern branch logic. They should deepen interpretation of disease form and viral biology without becoming frontline operational authority.

**Lead sources:** `src-fip-007`, `src-fip-021`

**Current safe read:**
- effusive disease has an immunopathology context, not only a fluid-recognition shortcut
- experimental morphogenesis is a historical foundation layer
- these sources support mechanism depth, not current diagnostic leadership

## Guardrail

Do not turn mechanism coherence into diagnosis. The mechanism page can explain why FIP is branch-structured and why mutation evidence matters, but diagnosis still has to move through clinicopathology, disease form, bounded laboratory support, and branch-specific workup.

## What The Module Can Say Safely

- FIP is best modeled as a systemic-disease branch arising from feline coronavirus background
- enteric background, gut/systemic competence, systemic spread, and overt disease are separate layers
- mutation evidence is mechanism-relevant but overclaim-sensitive in diagnosis
- recombinant-outbreak evidence keeps the emergence model plural rather than single-route

## What The Module Should Not Say Yet

- do not equate FCoV exposure with FIP
- do not treat any one mutation as proof of overt FIP
- do not let systemic-spread markers substitute for clinicopathologic suspicion
- do not erase recombinant or outbreak logic just because classical mutation-origin evidence is strong
