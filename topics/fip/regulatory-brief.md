The product-archetype memo adds the missing next layer: baseline GS-441524, remdesivir plus GS package logic, and neurologic rescue should not be treated as one regulatory object.

The U.S. eligibility memo now tests the cleanest first object, baseline GS-441524 for non-neurologic naturally occurring FIP, against expanded conditional-approval logic. It holds the branch as `plausible-to-test`, not established route advice.

The active-control design map adds the next U.S. layer: active-control planning is the right design object after eligibility testing, but the current blocker is comparator identity, not just endpoint selection.

The comparator boundary memo adds the first FIP-specific FDA compounding source: FDA stated in 2024 that no FDA-approved drug was available to treat FIP in cats, while compounded GS-441524 remains unapproved / enforcement-discretion access context rather than clean active-comparator status.
