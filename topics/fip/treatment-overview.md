# Feline FIP Treatment Overview

## Question This Page Answers

What are the current evidence-based treatment options for feline infectious peritonitis? How has FIP treatment evolved? What do clinical protocols recommend?

## Core Takeaway

Feline infectious peritonitis treatment has been **transformed** by nucleoside analog antivirals, moving from a historically fatal disease to one with demonstrated remission rates of 75-100% depending on clinical form and treatment protocol. Two main antivirals (GS-441524 and remdesivir) with similar efficacy have enabled standardized clinical protocols, though treatment duration and monitoring remain critical success factors.

---

## Treatment Era Timeline

### Era 1: Preclinical Foundation (2018)
**Key source:** src-fip-035

- **Finding:** GS-441524 strongly inhibits FIPV in tissue culture and experimental infection studies
- **Significance:** Establishes molecular basis for antiviral efficacy
- **Boundary:** Preclinical; does not predict clinical pharmacokinetics or dosing

### Era 2: Early Clinical Efficacy (2019-2021)
**Key sources:** src-fip-030, src-fip-031

- **Landmark finding (2019):** GS-441524 efficacy/safety in naturally occurring FIP (Pedersen et al.)
  - Changed FIP from fatal to **treatable disease**
  - Established oral administration feasibility
- **Oral formulation (2021):** Multi-component oral GS-441524 proved effective
  - Improved accessibility vs IV/subcutaneous earlier formulations
  - Enabled long-term home treatment

### Era 3: Clinical Optimization (2023-2025)
**Key sources:** src-fip-029, src-fip-032, src-fip-033, src-fip-034, src-fip-036, src-fip-037

- **Long-term remission (2023):** Durability of remission in cats treated with oral GS-441524
  - Demonstrated sustained remission; not just temporary viral suppression
- **Neurological management (2025):** GS-441524 and remdesivir efficacy in neurological FIP
  - Rapid clinical resolution possible
  - CNS penetration established
- **Ocular involvement (2025):** Combination therapy for FIP with ocular/uveitis manifestations
  - Remdesivir + GS-441524 in observational series
- **High-dose induction (2025-2026):** 46-case series establishing dose optimization
  - High-dose induction phase followed by maintenance
  - Addition of mefloquine adjunctive therapy

### Era 4: Standardization & Alternatives (2026)
**Key sources:** src-fip-038, src-fip-039, src-fip-040, src-fip-041, src-fip-042

- **Remdesivir RCT (2026):** First randomized trial comparing remdesivir to GS-441524
  - **Non-inferiority established** — remdesivir equivalent to GS-441524 for non-effusive FIP
  - Randomized, double-blind design; highest evidence level
- **ABCD Guidelines (2026):** European expert consensus on FIP treatment protocols
  - Official clinical guidance from veterinary dermatology/internal medicine consensus
  - Protocol standardization
- **Remdesivir PK (2026):** IV vs oral remdesivir bioavailability compared
  - Oral absorption data supporting oral remdesivir feasibility
- **Remdesivir efficacy (2025):** Prospective study of 29 cats with oral remdesivir
  - Demonstrates real-world efficacy in clinical cohort

## Active Antivirals: GS-441524 vs Remdesivir

### Comparative Profile

| Feature | GS-441524 | Remdesivir |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Development** | Gilead antiviral, GS-derived | COVID-era broad-spectrum antiviral |
| **First FIP trial** | 2019 (Pedersen) | 2026 (RCT) |
| **Clinical experience** | 5+ years; thousands of cats | ~1 year; growing evidence |
| **Available forms** | Oral primary; IV/SQ historical | Oral (newer); IV established |
| **Efficacy (non-effusive)** | 75-100% remission | **Non-inferior to GS-441524** (RCT) |
| **Efficacy (effusive)** | High remission rates | Comparable in observational data |
| **CNS penetration** | Established | Established |
| **Neurologic FIP** | Effective (src-fip-033) | Effective; rapid resolution (src-fip-036) |
| **Ocular/uveitis** | Used in combination (src-fip-037) | Used in combination (src-fip-037) |
| **Cost/availability** | More established supply chains | Increasing availability |

**Clinical reality:** Both antivirals are effective; choice often depends on availability, cost, and veterinarian familiarity.

## Clinical Treatment Protocols

### Standard Treatment Architecture

Based on src-fip-034 (46-case series) and src-fip-040 (protocol standardization):

#### Phase 1: High-Dose Induction
- **Duration:** Variable based on clinical response; typically weeks to months
- **Dose:** High-dose regimen (exact dosing in ABCD guidelines src-fip-039)
- **Adjunctive therapy:** Mefloquine addition shown beneficial in some cases
- **Monitoring:** Weekly to bi-weekly clinicopathologic assessment

#### Phase 2: Maintenance
- **Duration:** Months to years (often 12-24+ months)
- **Dose:** Typically reduced from induction; protocol in src-fip-040
- **Monitoring:** Monthly to quarterly assessment
- **Discontinuation criteria:** Treatment termination protocols in src-fip-040

#### Phase 3: Post-Treatment Monitoring
- **Duration:** Indefinite follow-up recommended
- **Surveillance:** Watch for relapse signs
- **Remission durability:** Long-term follow-up (src-fip-029) shows sustained remission possible

### Form-Specific Considerations

| FIP Form | Treatment Notes | Key Source |
|----------|-----------------|------------|
| **Non-effusive** | Most responsive; high remission rates | src-fip-038 (RCT) |
| **Effusive** | Responds well; may need longer treatment | src-fip-029, src-fip-031 |
| **Neurological** | Rapid clinical resolution possible; continue treatment long-term | src-fip-033, src-fip-036 |

### Prognostic Factors

**Better prognosis:**
- Non-effusive form vs effusive
- Early treatment initiation
- Good baseline clinical status
- Owner ability to complete long treatment course

**Challenging cases:**
- Severe effusive disease
- Neurological involvement at diagnosis
- Immunocompromised cats
- Poor compliance with treatment schedule

## What Changed: FIP as Fatal vs Treatable

### Historical Context (Pre-2019)
- FIP was **uniformly fatal** within weeks to months
- No effective medical treatment existed
- Only supportive care available

### Modern Reality (2019-2026)
- FIP is now **treatable** with demonstrated remission
- **75-100% remission rates** achievable with appropriate treatment
- Treatment duration measured in months to years, not days/weeks
- Quality of life during treatment generally good
- Relapse uncommon with adequate treatment duration
