## Question This Page Answers

What does the current IBD corpus already allow us to say about later route-level or product-level regulatory thinking?

## Current State

- no jurisdiction-specific IBD route analysis is ready yet (official-source regulatory pack not yet ingested)
- the first product-archetype layer now exists
- the first claim-fit / route-fit boundary layer now also exists
- a stable recurring verification path now exists (2026-04-17)
- a feline-primary treatment hierarchy ladder now exists (2026-04-17) with graduated confidence per step
- the current module is ready for `product-type-first` regulatory framing, but not for jurisdiction recommendation

## What Exists Already

- enough seed material to support later diet-oriented and supportive-treatment route thinking
- enough disease-boundary complexity to justify delaying any product-route compression
- enough treatment tiering now exists to separate diet-first, medical-management, microbiota-directed, regenerative, and assay-like archetypes

## Current Conclusions

- the next regulatory gain should come from product-archetype clarity, not from forcing early jurisdiction ranking
- `diet-first chronic-enteropathy management` is currently the cleanest practical archetype
- `medical-management` may later become a cleaner route-fit test than the diet branch, even if its claim-fit remains weaker
- the current regulatory branch is now clearer as `best overall archetype` versus `future route-cleaner archetype`, not as one winner
- the distinction between those two winner-types is now explicit enough for a dedicated comparison memo
- microbiota-directed, regenerative, and assay-style branches are still too unstable for early route work

## Current Boundary

The IBD module should not move into route or product claims until diagnosis, pathology, and treatment evidence are all thicker.
