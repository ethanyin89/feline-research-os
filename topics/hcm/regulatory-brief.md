## Question This Page Answers

What does the current HCM corpus already allow us to say about later route-level or product-level regulatory thinking?

## Current State

- no HCM-specific regulatory corpus is ready yet
- the current HCM seed set is dominated by scientific and clinical literature, not official-source pathway material
- the module is not ready for jurisdiction recommendation

## Current Conclusions

- the next regulatory gain should come from later product-archetype clarity, not from forcing early jurisdiction ranking
- cardiology-device, biomarker-assay, and drug-like branches may later need to be separated before any route comparison
- the first product-type separation layer now exists, so future regulatory work no longer has to keep this branch split implicit
- current HCM treatment and diagnostic literature can support mechanism and translation mapping much earlier than regulatory route analysis

## Current Boundary

The HCM module should not move into route or product claims until phenotype recognition, endpoint hierarchy, and treatment evidence are all thicker.

## Gaps

- no regulatory source pack yet
- no product archetype memo yet
- no route memo yet
