## Current Synthesis Spine

- feline HCM currently organizes around `structural phenotype`, `genetics`, `biomarkers`, `remodeling`, `treatment uncertainty`, and a held-back regulatory boundary
- the first major compression problem is not one biomarker or one therapy, but how to separate `phenotype definition`, `screening augmentation`, `genotype pressure`, and `remodeling depth`
- broad cardiomyopathy framing matters because HCM should not be treated as the only myocardial phenotype in cats
- echocardiography and morphometry likely lead the operational branch earlier than biomarker-only papers
- biomarker and AI papers add depth, but should remain below structural phenotype authority until better bounded
- the frontier branch is now clearer: targeted therapy, novel biomarkers, and AI augmentation are adjacent but not interchangeable
- the AI branch is now also clearer as bounded augmentation pressure rather than replacement authority
- troponin I now fits best as `injury / severity pressure`, while NT-proBNP fits best as a `severe-disease flag`
- treatment should be modeled as a real but overclaim-sensitive branch because the seed set already contains both therapeutic-advance optimism and explicit evidence skepticism; notably, the enalapril paper itself called for prospective studies to prove efficacy, making treatment-skepticism source-backed rather than just rhetorical
- the treatment layer is now also clearer as a branch-comparison problem: `bounded management`, `selective targeted frontier`, `older conventional therapy`, and `evidence skepticism` are adjacent but not equivalent
- remodeling and right-ventricular involvement likely widen the disease map beyond a simple left-ventricular-thickening story
- end-stage HCM should be read as a remodeled phenotype, not just as more hypertrophy
- the comparative-model framing is now better grounded: src-hcm-003 provides the only quantified population estimate (10-15%) and explicitly claims genotypic and phenotypic similarity to human HCM; model similarity is not clinical equivalence
- the comparative-model branch is now stronger but still bounded: source-page-checked material ties model value to Maine Coon/Ragdoll MYBPC3 mutation contexts and defined endpoints, not to direct import of human treatment hierarchy
- the morphological definition from src-hcm-002 (hypertrophy of a non-dilated ventricle) remains stable across later reviews and is the most defensible phenotype anchor in the seed set
- the older HCM update now provides full-card historical overview depth across cause, epidemiology, pathophysiology, and therapy, but should not outrank newer genetics, biomarker, remodeling, or treatment-specific evidence
- the strongest current compiled compression now likely sits in two places: endpoint separation and treatment-evidence layering
- genotype-severity and diagnostic-workup are now also strong enough to act as reusable compiled branches
- phenotype-remodeling and cardiomyopathy-boundary are now also strong enough to act as reusable compiled branches
- all 24 HCM paper source cards are now explicit full source-card depth; remaining HCM gains are full-text confirmation or output-specific precision
