## Question This Page Answers

From disease biology to intervention, where are the strongest translational bridges in feline HCM and where are the easiest overclaims?

## Current Safe Reading

- the treatment branch is now better read as a branch-comparison problem than as a flat ranking problem
- `bounded management` remains the safest routine-facing treatment frame
- `selective targeted frontier` is a real translational branch, but not the routine leader
- `older conventional therapy` should stay visible as historical treatment structure, not as modern certainty
- `evidence skepticism` is now a core control branch, not just rhetorical caution

## Current Conclusions

### quoted_fact

- A 2025 review states that feline HCM diagnosis relies on combined clinical evaluation, laboratory testing, and imaging.
- The same review states that therapeutic management focuses on controlling clinical signs, slowing progression, and improving quality and life expectancy.
- The seed corpus includes a small-molecule sarcomere-contractility inhibitor study (MYK-461) focused on acute relief of left-ventricular outflow tract obstruction. [src-hcm-011]
- The seed corpus includes a paper explicitly questioning whether HCM treatment is based more on science or faith.
- The treatment-skepticism paper reports that little objective evidence supported many therapeutic decisions and that clinicians often prescribed despite limited documented support.
- The targeted sarcomere-inhibition paper is framed around acute relief of LVOT obstruction rather than broad long-term disease-control outcomes. [src-hcm-011]
- An older enalapril treatment study (1998) evaluated clinical response retrospectively in 19 cats with HCM and concluded that controlled prospective studies are needed to prove the efficacy of enalapril in this disease. [src-hcm-014]

### source_supported_conclusion

- The treatment branch should be modeled as `real but overclaim-sensitive`, not as settled.
- Mechanism-linked intervention papers belong in the translation branch, but they should not erase uncertainty about broader treatment hierarchy.
- Targeted sarcomere therapy is now a real source-backed frontier branch, but it should stay endpoint-bounded and below final routine hierarchy claims.
- The newer review anchor supports management-and-progression-control framing, not cure or reversal framing.
- The enalapril paper's own conclusion — that controlled prospective studies are needed to prove efficacy — is a direct source-backed anchor for the treatment-skepticism branch: even authors of early conventional therapy called for stronger evidence.
- The 1998 date of the enalapril study and its retrospective 19-cat design are structurally significant: conventional treatment history preceded modern evidence standards, not just modern molecular targets.
- Common prescribing behavior should not be mistaken for strong supporting evidence.
- The older conventional-therapy anchor is best read as historical treatment structure rather than modern recommendation leadership.
- The current evidence is now strong enough to compile a layered treatment memo, but still not a flat final intervention ranking.
- Current HCM treatment evidence can support translation mapping much earlier than route-level regulatory analysis.
- The safest current treatment architecture remains `bounded management + selective targeted frontier`.
- The targeted-frontier branch is now less title-thin and more explicitly bounded by acute physiologic endpoint scope (acute LVOT relief, not durable disease-control proof).
- The older conventional-treatment branch is now more explicit as historical context rather than hidden background.
- The treatment layer is now strong enough to support a dedicated branch-comparison memo even though final intervention hierarchy is still unresolved.

### llm_inference

- HCM may become the first module where `therapy skepticism` is structurally as important as `therapy innovation`.
- HCM translation should probably be modeled as `bounded management + selective targeted frontier`, not as one flat treatment page.
