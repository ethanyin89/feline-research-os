## Core Takeaway

Feline HCM is best modeled with genetics, structural phenotype, and remodeling biology all competing for top-level explanatory weight. The morphological definition (hypertrophy of a non-dilated ventricle) remains stable, but it sits on active remodeling biology rather than a static wall-thickening story.

## Mechanism Hierarchy

### Layer 1: Genetic Pressure

Genetics is a first-class branch in HCM. The current evidence supports stratified genotype pressure rather than binary mutation-presence.

**Dosage-sensitive severity:** Homozygous MYBPC3-mutant cats show stronger severity and penetrance than heterozygous cats.

**Age-related penetrance:** Mutation-positive disease should not be modeled as temporally flat. Age-aware penetrance belongs in recognition logic.

**Maine Coon/Ragdoll model:** MYBPC3 mutations and higher incidence make these breeds valuable naturally occurring model systems.

**Lead sources:** `src-hcm-004`, `src-hcm-012`

**Current safe read:**
- genetics belongs near the top layer of HCM explanation
- genotype pressure is better modeled as stratified, not binary
- mutation dosage and age-related penetrance make recognition more nuanced
- genetics deepens phenotype interpretation without replacing structural recognition

### Layer 2: Structural Phenotype

Echocardiographic structure is the lead operational entry point. The phenotype is heterogeneous rather than one uniform hypertrophy pattern.

**Morphological definition:** Hypertrophy of a non-dilated ventricle (stable across reviews).

**Heterogeneous patterns:** Small-cavity and variable hypertrophy patterns matter before deeper tissue interpretation begins.

**Lead sources:** `src-hcm-001`, `src-hcm-002`, `src-hcm-009`

**Current safe read:**
- phenotype is the front door to HCM recognition
- phenotype heterogeneity should not be flattened into one uniform pattern
- the morphological definition is durable but not sufficient for mechanism understanding

### Layer 3: Remodeling Biology

Remodeling is not a passive after-effect. The structural phenotype sits on active remodeling biology.

**Core remodeling features:**
- cardiomyocyte disarray
- fibrosis (interstitial and replacement)
- macrophage-rich interstitial change
- profibrotic/inflammatory mediator signaling

**Right-ventricular involvement:** HCM should not be treated as a left-ventricle-only story too early.

**Lead sources:** `src-hcm-019`, `src-hcm-020`, `src-hcm-022`

**Current safe read:**
- remodeling logic deepens phenotype interpretation rather than replacing it
- macrophage-linked inflammatory-profibrotic remodeling is a real inner layer
- right-ventricular involvement widens the disease map

### Layer 4: End-Stage Progression

Advanced HCM should not be modeled as simply "more hypertrophy." End-stage is characterized by remodeled phenotype.

**End-stage features:**
- increased left-ventricular fibrous tissue deposition
- left-sided dilation (chamber remodeling)
- intramural coronary arteriosclerosis

**Lead sources:** `src-hcm-024`

**Current safe read:**
- end-stage HCM is a remodeled phenotype, not just extreme thickness
- fibrosis functions as a bridge variable between mechanism and severity
- pathology-depth helps interpret phenotype evolution

### Comparative Model Value

Feline HCM affects 10-15% of the pet cat population and is genotypically and phenotypically similar to human HCM.

**Lead sources:** `src-hcm-003`, `src-hcm-016`

**Current safe read:**
- cross-species model value shapes how feline HCM can inform human-HCM research
- model similarity is not clinical equivalence
- model framing should not be used to import human intervention hierarchy

## Guardrail

Do not let one mechanistic slice dominate the page. HCM is not adequately explained by genetics alone, phenotype alone, or remodeling alone. The four-layer hierarchy (genetics → phenotype → remodeling → end-stage) is stronger than any single causal story.

## What The Module Can Say Safely

- genetics belongs near the top layer of HCM explanation
- genotype pressure is stratified (dosage-sensitive, age-penetrant)
- structural phenotype is the lead recognition entry point
- remodeling biology deepens phenotype interpretation
- end-stage HCM is a remodeled phenotype, not just extreme hypertrophy
- feline HCM has comparative model value for human HCM research

## What The Module Should Not Say Yet

- there is no safe one-mutation explanation of all feline HCM
- there is no safe universal genotype-to-prognosis rule across all breeds and variants
- mutation status should not be promoted into standalone diagnostic certainty
- remodeling should not outrank phenotype in first-pass diagnosis
- model similarity is not clinical equivalence
