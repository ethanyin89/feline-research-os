# Feline CKD Imaging Context

## Question This Page Answers

What role should imaging, especially ultrasonography, play inside feline CKD workup without being overpromoted?

## Current Conclusions

### quoted_fact

- The ISFM guideline includes diagnostic imaging as part of the minimum routine database when CKD is suspected.
- The ultrasonography review states that ultrasound is the reference imaging modality for the feline kidney.
- Ultrasonography is more accurate than radiography for feline kidney assessment and is more accessible than CT or MRI in ordinary practice.
- Ultrasound provides good visualization of renal size, shape, and internal architecture.
- Focal or multifocal renal disease is easier to identify sonographically than diffuse renal change.
- B-mode ultrasonography is limited for distinguishing benign from malignant focal lesions.

### source_supported_conclusion

- Imaging belongs in the CKD workup layer, not in the first-wave endpoint layer.
- Ultrasound is best understood as a structural-context and renal-differential tool.
- The strongest role of imaging in the current vault is to improve workup completeness and realism, not to replace biochemical or urinary CKD interpretation.
- Diffuse CKD remains harder to define sonographically than focal renal disease, which sets a clear boundary against overclaiming.

### llm_inference

- A practical rule for the vault is:
  ultrasound helps answer `what structural renal context are we dealing with?`
  more than
  `does this cat definitely have early diffuse CKD?`

## What This Page Says Clearly

1. Imaging is part of good feline CKD workup realism.
2. Ultrasound is the practical reference imaging modality for the feline kidney.
3. Ultrasound is more useful for structural context and differential support than for solving diffuse CKD interpretation on its own.
4. The vault should not confuse imaging context with first-wave CKD endpoint status.

## What We Should Not Overstate

- that ultrasound is a core CKD endpoint
- that diffuse feline CKD can be reliably resolved sonographically by itself
- that B-mode ultrasonography alone settles lesion type
