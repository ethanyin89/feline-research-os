## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This page is a branch architecture page based on three deep-extracted sources. It can define branch structure and marker boundaries. It cannot yet provide treatment protocols, survival estimates, or owner-facing management advice.

## Core Takeaway

Feline mammary carcinoma should be a first split-out branch because it carries three distinct evidence roles:

- comparative oncology and human breast-cancer model relevance
- TNBC-like / basal-like marker phenotype evidence
- COX-2 prognosis-marker candidate evidence

These roles should remain separate. Model relevance is not treatment guidance, and biomarker signal is not a clinical decision rule.

## Branch Architecture

### Comparative Oncology Role

`src-cancer-004` and `src-cancer-019` support mammary carcinoma as a translationally important branch. The safe claim is that feline mammary carcinoma can be useful for comparative oncology framing, especially around hormone-independent and TNBC-like biology.

**Boundary:** do not translate human breast cancer therapies into feline recommendations from model similarity alone.

### TNBC-Like / Basal-Like Phenotype

`src-cancer-019` evaluated 24 feline mammary adenocarcinomas using ER, PR, HER2, CK5/6, and EGFR.

Study-bound findings:

- 14/24 tumors were triple negative.
- 11/14 triple-negative tumors were basal-like.
- 19/24 tumors were basal-like by CK5/6 and/or EGFR marker logic.

**Boundary:** keep these as one-study findings, not universal feline mammary carcinoma rates.

### Luminal-AR-Like Subtype (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-056` evaluated AR, FOXA1, ER, PR, Ki-67, HER2, and CK14 by automated immunohistochemistry in a retrospective 180-cat FMC cohort with 2-year post-mastectomy follow-up.

Study-bound findings:

- 57/180 FMCs were luminal and 123/180 were triple-negative by ER/PR/HER2 status.
- AR overexpression was reported in 33/180 cases.
- FOXA1 index at or above 1% was reported in 64/180 cases.
- In triple-negative FMCs, an AR+ FOXA1+ CK14- subgroup (n=7) was associated with better cancer-specific survival than an AR+ FOXA1- CK14+ basal-like subgroup (n=46), independent of tumor size and nodal stage.

**Boundary:** This supports a luminal-AR-like model sub-branch and marker-prognosis caveat. It does not support AR-targeted treatment selection in cats.

### BRCA Boundary

`src-cancer-019` did not find tumor-specific BRCA1/BRCA2 abnormalities in the amplified subset.

**Boundary:** this does not prove BRCA is irrelevant in cats; it prevents claiming that feline TNBC-like mammary carcinoma is BRCA-driven from this source.

### COX-2 Prognosis Marker Candidate

`src-cancer-003` supports COX-2 as a feline mammary carcinoma prognosis-marker candidate, but the evidence is limited and method-sensitive.

**Boundary:** do not turn COX-2 into a treatment selection rule or owner-facing survival prediction.

### HER2 Model (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-013` (2005 Cancer Research) established FMC as a model for HER2-overexpressing human breast cancer:

**Molecular basis:**
- Feline HER2 kinase domain is 92% similar to human HER2
- Anti-human HER2 antibody cross-reacts with feline HER2

**Prevalence:**
- 36% of archival FMC samples strongly HER2-positive by IHC
- 55% (6/11) FMC samples show increased HER2 mRNA

**Translational implication:** The 92% homology and antibody cross-reactivity means HER2-targeted therapies developed for humans could potentially be tested in cats with naturally occurring HER2+ FMC.

**Boundary:** 36% HER2 positivity is from archival samples and may vary by population. Not treatment guidance.

### Immunotherapy Research Context (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-017` (2024 BBA Reviews Cancer) provides the most current immunotherapy landscape:

**Checkpoint targets under investigation:**
- CTLA-4
- LAG-3
- PD-1
- VISTA
- TIM-3

**TME context from `src-cancer-016` (2021):**
- TILs, TAMs, CAFs, and checkpoint markers well-characterized
- FMC validated as model sharing clinicopathological features with human breast cancer

**Boundary:** Checkpoint inhibitor therapy for FMC remains investigational. No treatment recommendations.

### Immune-Suppressed TNBC-Like Subgroup (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-043` (2020 Tumour Biology) evaluated 180 female cats with invasive mammary carcinoma treated by surgery only and followed for 2 years post-mastectomy.

**Cohort split:**

| Subgroup | Count |
|----------|-------|
| Triple-negative (ER-, PR-, HER2-) | 123/180 |
| Luminal (ER+ and/or PR+) | 57/180 |

**Regulatory T-cell findings:**

- Peritumoral regulatory T cells were more than 300 times more abundant than intratumoral regulatory T cells.
- Peritumoral and intratumoral regulatory T cells were associated with shorter disease-free interval and overall survival in both triple-negative and luminal tumors.
- In triple-negative basal-like (CK14+) tumors, a regulatory T-cell-enriched subgroup had poorer disease-free interval, overall survival, and cancer-specific survival than regulatory T-cell-poor tumors.

**Boundary:** This supports prognostic and immune microenvironment framing. It does not support recommending immunotherapy for cats.

### COX-2 Expression (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-034` (2009 Veterinary Pathology) molecularly characterized feline COX-2 and quantified expression in FMC:

**Expression prevalence:**
- **87% (35/40) of feline mammary carcinomas express COX-2**
- Expression confirmed by immunohistochemistry
- Strong expression in majority of tumors

**Molecular characterization:**
- Feline COX-2 gene cloned and sequenced
- COX-2 is the rate-limiting enzyme in prostaglandin biosynthesis
- Plays important role in inflammation and tumorigenesis

**Therapeutic implication:**
High COX-2 expression suggests potential for COX-2 inhibitor therapy (e.g., piroxicam, meloxicam), but this requires clinical trial evidence.

**Boundary:** Expression prevalence (87%) does not prove COX-2 causation or predict response to COX inhibitors. Do not recommend NSAIDs for FMC from this evidence alone.

### Novel Therapeutic Targets (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-032` (2018 BMC Cancer, open access) evaluated BB-Cl-Amidine as a novel therapeutic:

**Mechanism:**
- BB-Cl-Amidine inhibits peptidylarginine deiminase (PAD) enzymes
- PAD inhibition activates endoplasmic reticulum (ER) stress pathway
- ER stress activation leads to cancer cell death

**Efficacy:**
- Reduces viability of feline mammary cancer cells (in vitro)
- Reduces viability of canine mammary cancer cells (in vitro)
- Dose-dependent cytotoxic effect

**Boundary:** This is in vitro evidence only. No in vivo or clinical trial data. Do not recommend for clinical use.

### Focused Ultrasound Feasibility (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-045` (2023 Ultrasonics) tested thermal focused ultrasound (FUS) in a small canine/feline mammary cancer pilot.

**Protocol:**

| Element | Abstract-Level Detail |
|---------|-----------------------|
| Device | 2-MHz focused ultrasonic transducer with robotic positioning |
| Patients | 9 dogs and cats with superficial mammary cancer |
| Sequence | FUS ablation followed by immediate surgical resection |
| Pathology finding | Well-defined coagulative necrosis in all treated tumors |
| Safety signal | No off-target damage reported in the abstract |

**Boundary:** This is feasibility evidence only. The mixed-species n=9 design and immediate resection prevent clinical outcome claims.

### EMT Cell-Line Model (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-049` (2018 Scientific Reports) established TiHo-0906, a feline mammary cancer cell line derived from a tumor with anaplastic and malignant spindle cells.

**Model properties:**

- Epithelial/mesenchymal phenotype.
- High HMGA2 and CD44 expression.
- Copy-number variation regions included EMT-, breast-cancer-, and human metaplastic breast-cancer-associated genes such as AKT1, GATA3, CDK4, ZEB1, KRAS, HMGA2, and MYC.
- Low-passaged cells were two-fold more resistant to doxorubicin than high-passaged cells (IC50 99.97 nM vs 41.22 nM).

`src-cancer-050` (1989 Anticancer Research) adds historical adriamycin-sensitivity model evidence by comparing in vitro tumor-island testing with in vivo response.

**Boundary:** These are research-tool and historical drug-sensitivity findings. They should not be used as patient-level treatment selection rules.

### CAF And COX-2 Prognostic Markers (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-052` (2024 In Vivo) evaluated COX-2 and alpha-SMA-positive cancer-associated fibroblasts in 50 felines with malignant mammary tumors.

**Marker associations:**

| Marker | Abstract-Level Associations |
|--------|-----------------------------|
| COX-2 overexpression | Mitotic index, malignancy degree, lymph node metastasis, vascular invasion, recurrence, distant metastasis |
| alpha-SMA-positive CAFs | Mitotic index, lymph node metastasis, vascular invasion, recurrence, distant metastasis |

Both markers correlated with disease-free and overall survival and emerged as poor-prognosis predictors in the abstract.

**Boundary:** This strengthens the prognostic-marker layer, but it does not support treatment selection or NSAID recommendations.

### AKT/PTEN Pathway (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-090` (2012 Veterinary Pathology) evaluated AKT activation in 39 cats (27 malignant, 12 benign mammary tumors):

**Key findings:**

| Marker | Association |
|--------|-------------|
| p-AKT expression | Malignancy, dedifferentiation, recurrence |
| p-AKT positive tumors | Shorter disease-free period |
| AKT activation | HER2 expression, PTEN down-regulation |
| Feline AKT gene | High homology with human AKT |

**Translational relevance:** The AKT/HER2/PTEN axis in FMC parallels human breast cancer signaling, supporting the comparative oncology model.

**Boundary:** Prognostic marker evidence only. No AKT-targeted therapy recommendations for cats.

### Phosphoproteomic Profiling (Deep-Extracted)

`src-cancer-093` (2025 PLoS One) provides the first phosphoproteomic investigation of FMC. [Deep extraction worksheet](../../system/indexes/src-cancer-093-deep-extraction-round1.md).

**Study design:**
- 31 FMC samples (Grade 1: n=6, Grade 2: n=11, Grade 3: n=14) + 6 normal controls
- Phosphoprotein enrichment + LC-MS/MS analysis
- Mills classification for histological grading

**Key findings:**

| Finding | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Total phosphoproteins | 11,942 identified |
| Differentially expressed | 17 significantly downregulated in FMC vs normal |
| Inter-grade differences | No significant differences among tumor grades |
| PRKAG3-Ki67 association | Low PRKAG3 correlates with high Ki-67 (p=0.03) |

**Therapeutic target candidates:**

| Protein | Function |
|---------|----------|
| ABCC3 | ABC transporter, multidrug resistance |
| ACPP | Acid phosphatase, tumor suppressor |
| PPP1CA | Serine/threonine phosphatase, growth promoter |
| PRKAG3 | AMPK regulatory subunit, Ki-67 association |
| RNASEL | Endoribonuclease, tumor suppressor |

**Molecular subtype distribution:**
- Luminal B/HER2-negative: 35.5%
- Triple-negative (basal + normal-like): 35.5%
- Luminal B/HER2-positive: 16.1%
- HER2-positive: 12.9%

**Boundary:** First-study evidence with small sample (n=31). No clinical diagnostic or treatment recommendations. PRKAG3-Ki67 association requires validation in larger cohorts.

### CXCR4/CXCL12 Metastasis Axis (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-088` (2018 BMC Veterinary Research) characterized the CXCR4/CXCL12 chemokine axis in FMC metastasis:

**Expression patterns:**

| Location | CXCR4 | CXCL12 |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Primary tumor | Higher (p=0.0067) | Lower |
| Liver/lung metastases | Lower | Higher (p<0.0001) |

**HER2+ subtype distinction:**
- HER2+ tumors show higher CXCR4 in metastases (p=0.012)
- CXCL12-negative primary tumor = unfavorable prognosis in HER2+ FMC

**Boundary:** Chemokine axis characterization supports metastasis biology research but does not inform treatment selection.

### TIL/TAM Immune Microenvironment (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-081` (2022 Veterinary Pathology) analyzed tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes and macrophages in 73 FMC cases:

**Prognostic associations:**

| Marker | Finding |
|--------|---------|
| Stromal CD8+ TILs | Higher = better survival |
| CD163+ TAMs | Associate with undifferentiated tumors |
| TNBC-like subtype | Enriched for immune cell infiltration |

**Boundary:** This supports immune microenvironment characterization and prognostic stratification. No immunotherapy recommendations.

### Targeted Drug Delivery (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-101` (2021 Cancers) evaluated transferrin receptor 1 (TFR-1) targeting for FMC nanomedicine:

**Approach:**
- TFR-1 overexpressed in solid tumors (validated in humans)
- H-ferritin (HFn) nanocages recognize TFR-1 for selective binding
- Doxorubicin loaded into HFn nanocages for targeted delivery

**Boundary:** In vitro evidence only. No clinical trial data for TFR-1-targeted therapy in cats.

### Inflammatory Mammary Carcinoma (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-054` (2004 Breast Cancer Research) prospectively described three female cats with inflammatory mammary carcinoma.

**Case-series findings:**

- All three cases were secondary/postsurgical IMC.
- Clinical signs included rapid erythema, severe edema, extreme local pain and firmness, absence of subjacent mammary nodules, and extremity involvement.
- Histology showed highly malignant papillary mammary carcinomas and dermal tumor embolization of superficial lymphatic vessels.
- Cats were euthanized at 10, 15, and 45 days after diagnosis.
- The two necropsied cats had regional lymph node and lung metastases.

**Boundary:** This is a three-case description. It can mark a rare aggressive phenotype and comparative model signal, but not prevalence or management guidance.

### Prognostic Factors (Abstract-Level)

Two abstract-level sources establish prognostic factor hierarchy:

**From `src-cancer-012` (1984 foundational study, n=100):**

| Factor | Prognostic Value | Evidence |
|--------|------------------|----------|
| Tumor size | **Most significant** (P<0.0001) | Small tumors (1-8 cm³) best prognosis |
| Age | NOT prognostic | ≤10 vs >10 years, no significance |
| Breed | NOT prognostic | no significance |
| Surgery type | Mixed | affects DFI (P<0.01) but NOT survival |

**From `src-cancer-025` (2015 systematic review):**

| Factor | Evidence Level | Status |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Tumor grading | High reliability | Validated prognostic parameter |
| Lymph node invasion | High reliability | Validated prognostic parameter |
| Lymphovascular invasion | High reliability | Validated prognostic parameter |
| Tumor subtype | Promising | Needs standardized investigation |
| Tumor size | Promising | Needs standardized cutoffs |
| Staging | Promising | Needs standardized systems |
| Ki67, HER2, ER | May predict therapy | Needs consensus protocols |

**Key insight:** The field lacks standardization in methodology, weakening cross-study comparison.

### Metastatic Disease Outcomes (Abstract-Level)

`src-cancer-009` provides the largest published cohort of metastatic FMC (n=73, stage IV):

**Survival data:**

| Metric | Value | Context |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Overall mean TTP | 23 days | time to progression |
| Overall mean TSS | 44 days | tumor-specific survival |
| Symptomatic TSS | 14 days | cats with clinical signs at diagnosis |
| Asymptomatic TSS | 128 days | incidentally detected metastases |
| With pleural effusion | 16 days | major negative prognostic factor |

**Treatment modality comparison (abstract-level):**

| Treatment | n | Median TSS | Toxicity |
|-----------|---|------------|----------|
| MTD chemotherapy | 9 | 58 days | 66.7% |
| Metronomic chemotherapy | 15 | 75 days | 20% |
| Toceranib phosphate | 10 | 63 days | 30% |

**Boundary:** P=0.197 for treatment comparison — study was not powered to detect survival differences. Some cats survived >6 months despite overall poor prognosis.

## What The Module Can Say Safely

- Mammary carcinoma deserves a dedicated cancer branch.
- TNBC-like / basal-like model evidence exists, but should be marker-defined and study-bound.
- BRCA translation from human TNBC should be handled cautiously.
- COX-2 is a candidate prognosis-marker layer for feline mammary carcinoma, with standardization caveats.

## What The Module Should Not Say Yet

- Do not rank treatments.
- Do not give survival ranges.
- Do not state universal TNBC-like prevalence.
- Do not claim BRCA-driven mechanism closure.
- Do not recommend COX inhibitors from COX marker evidence.
