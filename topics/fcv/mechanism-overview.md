## Core Takeaway

FCV is a highly variable RNA virus whose mechanism branch holds genome plasticity, receptor/tissue-tropism logic, persistence biology, and pathotype diversification in the same frame. This architecture explains why FCV control remains difficult despite widespread vaccination.

## Mechanism Hierarchy

The FCV mechanism spine follows this order:

### Layer 1: Diversity and Evolution

FCV demonstrates remarkable genetic, antigenic, and clinical diversity. Outbreak vaccine-resistant strains occur frequently. Sequence variation is part of the control problem, not just a taxonomy detail.

**Lead sources:** `src-fcv-001`, `src-fcv-002`, `src-fcv-015`, `src-fcv-023`

**Current safe read:**
- FCV is a highly variable positive-stranded RNA virus
- continuing genetic and antigenic diversification maintains vaccine pressure
- sequence diversity explains much of the control difficulty

### Layer 2: Receptor Entry and Tissue Distribution

Receptor biology (feline JAM-A) and tissue distribution help explain why FCV is broader than a simple upper-airway virus. Multiple clinical biotypes fit under the same molecular frame.

**Lead sources:** `src-fcv-015`, `src-fcv-016`

**Current safe read:**
- JAM-A receptor distribution across feline tissues explains broad lesion diversity
- FCV should not be modeled as only an upper-respiratory virus
- receptor/tropism logic now sits under the mechanism spine rather than only under extension papers

### Layer 3: Persistence and Chronic Carriage

Persistence and chronic carriage are built into the FCV system model. Long-term transmission pressure is partly maintained by carrier-state biology.

**Lead sources:** `src-fcv-002`, `src-fcv-007`, `src-fcv-017`

**Current safe read:**
- persistence is a core FCV systems problem
- chronic carriers maintain population-level transmission
- acute-disease mitigation does not equal chronic-carrier control
- vaccination reduces disease burden more defensibly than it prevents carrier-state formation

### Layer 4: Pathotype Diversification

FCV generates multiple clinical forms, including virulent systemic disease (VS-FCV). Control strategy must account for heterogeneity in both virus and phenotype.

**Lead sources:** `src-fcv-001`, `src-fcv-007`, `src-fcv-015`

**Current safe read:**
- classical FCV and VS-FCV are distinct pathotypes requiring different framing
- pathotype diversity partly flows from genome plasticity
- severe-systemic disease exists but should not replace routine URTD as the default frame

### Extension Branches

Ocular and enteric disease extend the FCV disease map without replacing the core oral/respiratory presentation.

**Lead sources:** `src-fcv-020`, `src-fcv-021`

**Current safe read:**
- ocular-surface FCV is real and syndrome-aware
- enteric FCV association is visible but should not become a lead-cause shortcut
- extension branches widen recognition without becoming first-line diagnostics

## Guardrail

Do not let one mechanistic slice dominate the page. FCV is not adequately explained by sequence diversity alone, receptor biology alone, or vaccine escape alone. The four-layer hierarchy (diversity → receptor/tropism → persistence → pathotype) is stronger than any single causal story.

## What The Module Can Say Safely

- FCV is best modeled as a variable, persistence-capable, multi-phenotype viral system
- control difficulty is mechanistically connected to diversity and persistence
- receptor/tissue-tropism logic matters for understanding lesion diversity
- mechanism writing should explain control problems, not float separately from them

## What The Module Should Not Say Yet

- do not reduce FCV to a simple respiratory-virus description
- do not imply receptor distribution alone explains virulence
- do not treat persistence as synonymous with vaccine failure
- do not pretend the current source pack has a full strain-by-strain virulence map
