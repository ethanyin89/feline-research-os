## Current Synthesis Spine

- feline FCV currently organizes around `broad review/guidance`, `vaccine/immunity`, `epidemiology/recognition`, `therapy`, and `extension/tissue tropism` branches
- guideline and broad review sources (src-fcv-001, src-fcv-002, src-fcv-004, src-fcv-007, src-fcv-009, src-fcv-015) should control the disease shell and branch naming
- vaccine evidence is the thickest branch but requires careful separation: breadth of protection, challenge models, platform design, cellular vs humoral immunity, vaccine-failure interpretation, and persistence are not interchangeable claims
- VS-FCV (virulent systemic FCV) represents a distinct clinical entity that should not be collapsed into routine upper respiratory infection framing
- epidemiology sources provide field-level recognition detail and geography-specific vaccine-fit pressure, but should not override controlled study findings
- therapy exists as a real branch (src-fcv-008, src-fcv-014, src-fcv-018) but prevention/vaccination remains the primary control strategy
- tissue tropism and extension branches (musculoskeletal, systemic) widen the disease map beyond the classic oral/respiratory presentation
- context-style material (src-fcv-019) should remain low-weight and not drift into decision-bearing prose

## Best First Compiled Questions

## Current Working Hypothesis

FCV should remain the disease module where `vaccine/immunity complexity`, `VS-FCV distinction`, `carrier persistence`, and `therapy limitations` all stay visible at the top layer instead of collapsing into a simple vaccination-solves-everything story.
