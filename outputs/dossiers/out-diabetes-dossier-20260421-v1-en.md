## Core Frame

Feline diabetes should be treated as a mixed metabolic/endocrine disease with remission potential, not as a single remission protocol or a single product story.

The current internal architecture is:

- pathogenesis: type-2-like disease plus secondary endocrine and comorbidity branches
- recognition: diagnosis confirmation plus body condition, endocrine, pancreatitis/DKA, and monitoring gates
- endpoints: remission, glycemic control, insulin-dose change, complications, ketone risk, and label-defined safety monitoring
- treatment: diet, insulin, SGLT2, adjunct, frontier, and remission-control branches separated
- regulatory: U.S. Bexacat/Senvelgo FOI and current-label control without global overreach

## Key Guardrails

- Do not turn remission into the whole disease model.
- Do not turn diet into one carbohydrate slogan.
- Do not rank SGLT2 products or insulin protocols without stronger comparator evidence.
- Do not infer non-U.S. regulatory status from U.S. FDA or DailyMed cards.
- Do not promote the current compiled paper-card layer into decision-grade clinical guidance.
