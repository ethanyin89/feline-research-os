## User Question

What is the first usable internal dossier for feline FIP across disease framing, recognition, endpoint hierarchy, mutation-boundary, treatment transformation, and regulatory boundary?

## Executive Answer

Based on the current seed corpus, feline FIP is now usable as an internal research object, but only if the module preserves both `supportive diagnosis` and `treatment transformation` at the same time.

The strongest current organizing logic is:

- systemic disease emergence from feline coronavirus background
- recognition through composite suspicion rather than one-test proof
- endpoint hierarchy instead of marker flattening
- mutation utility held together with diagnostic limitations
- disease-form-aware treatment transformation
- regulatory boundary held back behind route and legitimacy ambiguity

The most important practical consequence is that the system should not ask, “Which test settles FIP?” The better question is, “Which recognition, mutation-support, disease-form, and treatment layers best support a bounded working diagnosis without pretending certainty?”

This English page is a derived dossier layer built on the working-English dossier. It should not be mistaken for a bilingual dossier or a decision-grade recommendation.

## Disease Framing

### quoted_fact

- Broad review material supports FIP as a supportive diagnosis rather than a one-test disease.
- Mutation-origin and pathobiogenesis work support systemic-disease emergence from feline coronavirus background.
- Recombinant-outbreak and spread-boundary work complicate any one-route mechanism simplification.

### source_supported_conclusion

- FIP should be modeled as a `supportive diagnosis + pathobiogenesis-aware disease module`.
- The module should preserve both `feline coronavirus background` and `systemic FIP emergence logic` without collapsing them.
- Mechanism should constrain diagnosis, not create diagnostic certainty.

## Recognition Layer

### quoted_fact

- Risk-factor and endemic multi-cat studies support age, patterned signalment, and ecology-aware exposure risk.
- Sydney and Taiwan clinicopathology series support practical staged suspicion logic.
- Effusive, non-effusive, and neurologic branches create different recognition pressures.

### source_supported_conclusion

- Recognition should be split into `risk context` and `suspicion-pattern recognition`.
- Clinicopathology currently belongs near the top of operational FIP recognition.
- Disease form should shift workup logic rather than being treated as a late severity footnote.

## Endpoint And Marker Hierarchy

### quoted_fact

- Acute-phase and immunoglobulin work supports supportive inflammatory laboratory context.
- Older serology remains historically relevant but weaker in current workup authority.
- Mutation-assay studies support both diagnostic utility and diagnostic limitation.
- CSF viral detection is much more useful in neurologic and/or ocular subgroup workup than in generic workup.

### source_supported_conclusion

- FIP endpoint logic should remain layered:
  - suspicion-support
  - clinicopathology / disease-form support
  - mutation / molecular support
  - neurologic-extension support
  - treatment-follow-up outcomes
- Mutation assays and CSF detection should both remain bounded by use-case.
- Treatment follow-up outcomes should stay separate from initial diagnostic support.

## Mutation Boundary

### quoted_fact

- Mutation-origin work supports emergence from feline enteric coronavirus background.
- Mutation-utility work supports real diagnostic strengthening.
- Mutation-limitation work supports strong caution against flattening mutation detection into definitive diagnosis.

### source_supported_conclusion

- Mutation belongs to both mechanism and diagnosis, but not in the same way.
- Positive mutation support and mutation limits must stay visible together.
- Mutation testing should strengthen workup, not replace composite recognition.

## Treatment Transformation

### quoted_fact

- Experimental and tissue-culture GS work supports a preclinical antiviral foundation.
- Natural-disease GS-441524 work supports real treatment transformation.

### source_supported_conclusion

- The treatment branch should be split into preclinical foundation, baseline efficacy, package logic, neurologic rescue, and durability.
- FIP treatment should be modeled as transformed, but not flattened into a universal efficacy claim.
- Neurologic disease and post-treatment durability are structurally important treatment branches.
- Remdesivir-plus-GS case series, neurologic treatment work, and long-term remission follow-up should be kept as separate package, rescue, and durability layers.

## Regulatory Boundary

### source_supported_conclusion

- Regulatory interpretation should currently stay at boundary-page level.
- Access-path, legitimacy, and route-level interpretation should not be smuggled in through treatment success language.
- FIP can support translation mapping far earlier than route-level regulatory planning.
- The current FIP topic layer has no jurisdiction-specific regulatory source pack, and the treatment branch is much thicker than the regulatory branch.
