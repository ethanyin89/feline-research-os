# Feline Diabetes Internal Dossier V1

## Executive Answer

Feline diabetes is now usable as a fifth disease module if it is kept as a layered truth object rather than a treatment leaderboard.

The current module should be organized around:

- mixed metabolic/endocrine pathogenesis
- obesity and body-condition sequencing
- remission as real but evidence-limited
- diet architecture rather than diet slogans
- treatment-branch separation
- diagnostic and monitoring gates
- complication and comorbidity visibility
- U.S. SGLT2 label control without global regulatory overreach

## Disease Framing

### source_supported_conclusion

- Feline diabetes should start from type-2-like metabolic disease but preserve secondary endocrine disease, obesity, pancreatitis, and complication branches.
- The disease should not be reduced to remission potential because chronic comorbidity and beta-cell/endocrine constraints remain structurally important.

### llm_inference

- Diabetes is the best current module for testing whether the vault can combine literature synthesis with live product-label boundaries.

## Recognition And Workup Layer

### source_supported_conclusion

- Workup should route through diagnosis confirmation, body-condition assessment, endocrine-comorbidity suspicion, pancreatitis/DKA context, treatment candidacy, and monitoring gates.
- Breed-risk signals, including Burmese predisposition in UK/Australian sources, should be used as risk context rather than mechanism proof.

## Endpoint Layer

### source_supported_conclusion

- Remission, glycemic control, insulin-dose change, body condition, neuropathy/complications, ketone risk, and label-defined safety monitoring are distinct endpoint layers.
- Neuropathy and microvascular pathology should remain complication endpoints rather than treatment claims.

## Treatment Layer

### source_supported_conclusion

- Diet, insulin, SGLT2, alpha-glucosidase adjunct, incretin/frontier, and remission-control branches should remain separated by role, evidence type, endpoint, and safety boundary.
- SGLT2 inhibitors are a real selected-cat branch but must stay governed by candidate selection, contraindication, DKA/euglycemic-DKA, residual beta-cell, and monitoring logic.
- Glargine U300 and diet studies support real treatment branches, but not a final superiority hierarchy.

## Regulatory Boundary

### source_supported_conclusion

- Bexacat and Senvelgo now have U.S. FDA FOI and current-label source cards.
- Those cards support U.S. source control, not global status, product superiority, or off-label complicated-diabetes claims.
- Non-U.S. status requires separate primary regulatory source checks.
