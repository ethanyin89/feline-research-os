## Executive Answer

Feline diabetes should currently be handled as a `reversible-potential metabolic disease with chronic endocrine and comorbidity constraints`.

The current corpus supports a layered architecture rather than a single remission, diet, insulin, or SGLT2 story:

- type-2-like metabolic disease is central, but not universal
- remission is real, but evidence quality blocks a simple predictor or protocol winner
- obesity and body condition are mechanism, recognition, and treatment-sequencing variables
- endocrine-secondary disease and pancreatitis can change the clinical frame
- neuropathy belongs as a complication and endpoint branch
- diet, insulin, SGLT2, alpha-glucosidase, incretin/frontier, and remission-control branches must stay separated
- U.S. SGLT2 label control is source-backed for Bexacat and Senvelgo, but it is not global status or comparative superiority evidence

## Evidence-Backed Points

### source_supported_conclusion

- Treatment should be organized by role, endpoint, and safety boundary before any flat ranking.
- SGLT2 interpretation must preserve candidate-selection, insulin-naive, DKA/euglycemic-DKA, and monitoring boundaries.
- Remission should remain a visible outcome branch without becoming the whole disease model.
- Diet and obesity belong together as staged sequencing questions, not as a single carbohydrate slogan.
- Regulatory statements should remain jurisdiction-specific unless new non-U.S. primary sources are added.
