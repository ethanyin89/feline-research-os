## User Question

Based on the current FIP seed corpus, what is the first usable internal view of feline infectious peritonitis across pathobiogenesis, recognition, endpoints, mutation-boundary, and antiviral treatment?

## Executive Answer

Current evidence supports treating feline FIP as a disease module built around `diagnostic ambiguity plus treatment transformation`, not as a one-test diagnosis and not as an older fatalism-only story.

The strongest current organizing conclusion is that feline FIP should be modeled through:

- systemic-disease emergence from feline coronavirus background
- composite and supportive recognition
- disease-form-aware workup
- mutation utility held together with mutation limits
- antiviral treatment transformation with distinct treatment sublayers

The current corpus also says four operational things clearly.

First, FIP recognition still belongs to a `supportive and composite` diagnostic frame. Clinicopathology and disease-form logic currently matter more than any single assay.

Second, the endpoint layer is best modeled as `layered support`, not as one global marker ranking.

Third, mutation-related testing is real but bounded. It adds diagnostic support, but it does not justify certainty language by itself.

Fourth, the treatment branch is now strong enough to change the whole module. But it still must stay split into preclinical foundation, baseline GS efficacy, package logic, neurologic rescue, and durability.

This English page is a derived output layer built on the working-English briefing. It should not be mistaken for a bilingual summary or a decision-grade recommendation.

## Evidence-Backed Points

### quoted_fact

- Broad FIP review material supports supportive diagnosis rather than one-test certainty.
- Mutation-origin and pathobiogenesis papers support systemic disease emergence from feline coronavirus background rather than direct equivalence between enteric coronavirus and overt FIP.
- Risk and clinicopathology papers support young-age, patterned signalment, multi-cat exposure, and clinicopathologic pattern recognition as important suspicion-building layers.
- Mutation-assay utility and limitation papers support both positive support value and meaningful diagnostic limits.
- CSF-based viral detection becomes much more useful in neurologic and/or ocular subgroups than in ordinary workup.
- GS-441524 and remdesivir-era papers support real treatment transformation in naturally occurring disease.
- Long-term remission follow-up and neurologic-treatment papers support durability and disease-form-specific treatment complexity as separate branches rather than one flat cure claim.

### source_supported_conclusion

- FIP should be modeled as `supportive diagnosis + antiviral treatment transformation`, not as diagnosis-only or treatment-only.
- The recognition branch should remain disease-form-aware and clinicopathology-led.
- Endpoint logic should remain layered:
  - suspicion-support
  - clinicopathology / disease-form support
  - mutation / molecular support
  - neurologic-extension support
  - treatment-follow-up outcomes
- Mutation-related assays should be presented as bounded workup support, not as definitive FIP proof.
- Modern treatment evidence is strong enough to justify a dedicated translational branch from day one.
- Neurologic rescue and long-term remission should remain separate from baseline GS-441524 efficacy claims.

### llm_inference

- FIP is currently one of the clearest modules for showing that `diagnostic ambiguity` and `treatment transformation` can coexist without contradiction.
