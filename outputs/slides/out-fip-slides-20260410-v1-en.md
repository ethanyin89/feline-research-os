# Core Disease Frame

- FIP should currently be modeled as:
  - supportive diagnosis
  - plus treatment transformation
- The strongest current top-line architecture is:
  - systemic disease emergence
  - composite recognition
  - mutation utility plus mutation limits
  - disease-form-aware treatment branching

Bottom line:

Do not model FIP as a one-test disease.

Recognition is composite, not assay-first.

# Mutation Boundary

- mutation studies matter
- but they do two different jobs:
  - explain mechanism
  - support workup
- they do not justify certainty language by themselves

Bottom line:

Positive mutation support and mutation limits must stay together.

# What Modern Treatment Changes

- treatment is no longer a thin side branch
- GS-441524-era evidence changes the whole translational map
- but treatment transformation does not erase diagnostic ambiguity

Bottom line:

FIP is now both harder to diagnose cleanly and much more actionable to treat.

# What We Can Say Now

## Defensible now

- supportive diagnosis still leads
- clinicopathology is operationally central
- mutation support must stay bounded
- treatment transformation is real
- neurologic rescue and durability are separate branches

## Not defensible yet

- one-test diagnostic certainty
- universal treatment efficacy across all forms
- route-level regulatory recommendation
