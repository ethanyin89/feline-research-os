## Quoted Facts

- `src-ckd-006`: phosphate-restricted diet in IRIS stage 2-3 feline CKD has evidence of beneficial clinical outcome.
- `src-ckd-006`: few veterinary reports focus on safety and efficacy of intestinal phosphate binders.
- `src-ckd-006`: no phosphorus binders are identified as licensed medications for dogs or cats in this reviewed source context.
- `src-ckd-003`: phosphate binders may help when diet alone is insufficient.

## Source-Supported Conclusions

- `src-ckd-006`: dietary phosphorus restriction has stronger current support than phosphate binders in the feline evidence layer and should stay above binder adjunct logic in the treatment hierarchy.
- `system/indexes/ckd-phosphorus-control-evidence-memo.md`: `PCE2` states that dietary phosphorus restriction is more strongly supported than phosphate-binder logic in the current feline evidence base.

## Boundary

- This supports a hierarchy claim, not a claim that binders are useless.
- Binder logic remains clinically relevant when diet alone is insufficient.
- This does not support product-superiority claims for any specific binder.
- CKD mineral management is broader than phosphorus alone and includes PTH, calcium, FGF23, and hypercalcaemia risk.

## Missing Evidence

- Feline-specific phosphate binder RCT data remains a gap for adjunct-product positioning.
- Route and category clarity remain weaker for binder adjuncts than for diet logic.
- Head-to-head diet vs binder evidence would make the hierarchy more decision-grade.
