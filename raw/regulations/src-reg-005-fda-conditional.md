# Conditional Approval Explained: A Resource for Veterinarians

## One-Line Summary

FDA explains that cats are a major species and that conditional approval for major species depends on special statutory circumstances rather than being a general shortcut.

## Why It Matters For CKD

This source prevents over-simplified assumptions that any cat indication can use conditional approval.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- FDA classifies cats as one of the seven major species.
- For full approval, a sponsor must provide substantial evidence of effectiveness.
- For conditional approval, a sponsor must show a reasonable expectation of effectiveness.
- Expanded conditional approval for major species depends on serious or life-threatening conditions or unmet health needs where demonstrating effectiveness would require complex or particularly difficult studies.

### source_supported_conclusion

- A feline CKD product does not automatically qualify for conditional approval just because the target species is a cat.

### llm_inference

- Any U.S. CKD pathway memo should explicitly test conditional-approval eligibility rather than assume it.

## Limits / Caveats

- This is an explanatory resource, not a sponsor-facing full submission guide.
