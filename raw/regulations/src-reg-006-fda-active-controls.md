# CVM GFI #204 Active Controls in Studies to Demonstrate Effectiveness of a New Animal Drug for use in Companion Animals

## One-Line Summary

FDA guidance discusses when active-control study designs are appropriate to demonstrate effectiveness for companion animal drugs used in cats, dogs, and horses.

## Why It Matters For CKD

This is directly relevant if a feline CKD product ultimately needs comparative efficacy studies rather than untreated or placebo-controlled designs.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- The guidance is directed at studies intended to provide substantial evidence of effectiveness for new animal drugs used in cats, dogs, and horses.
- FDA compares active-control studies with placebo or untreated concurrent controls to explain appropriate use.

### source_supported_conclusion

- U.S. pathway planning for feline CKD should include study-design strategy, not just endpoint selection.

### llm_inference

- If placebo or untreated designs become ethically or practically difficult in CKD, active-control logic may become important.

## Limits / Caveats

- This is study-design guidance, not an approval-route document.
