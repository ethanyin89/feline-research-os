# FDA FOI Summary: Senvelgo (velagliflozin oral solution), NADA 141-568

## One-Line Summary

Primary FDA FOI summary for Senvelgo, an oral velagliflozin solution approved to improve glycemic control in otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.

## Why It Matters For Feline Diabetes

- adds the second primary U.S. regulatory anchor for feline SGLT2 treatment
- anchors product form, dose logic, field-study endpoints, and early ketonuria monitoring
- prevents the SGLT2 branch from depending only on review abstracts or manufacturer pages

## Key Findings

- FDA approved Senvelgo under NADA 141-568 on August 10, 2023.
- The indicated population is otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.
- Senvelgo is a velagliflozin oral solution dosed once daily at 1 mg/kg, with or without food and regardless of blood glucose level.
- The FOI summary describes velagliflozin as an SGLT2 inhibitor that induces glucosuria by reducing renal glucose reabsorption.
- The pivotal field study enrolled 252 cats; 198 cats were included in the effectiveness analysis after early removals and exclusions.
- At Day 30, 175/198 cats in the effectiveness analysis were treatment successes.
- Ketonuria and diabetic ketoacidosis drove early removals from the effectiveness analysis.
- The FOI summary emphasizes diabetic ketosis, diabetic ketoacidosis, and euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis as major safety concerns.
- Ketonuria monitoring occurred during the first week and later scheduled/unscheduled safety checks.

## Limits / Caveats

- this source is a U.S. FDA regulatory source, not a global regulatory map
- it does not establish superiority over Bexacat, insulin, or diet-based management
- it should not be used to infer use in complicated diabetes or insulin-dependent cats
- full product insert and current Animal Drugs @ FDA records should be checked before label-level wording is promoted
