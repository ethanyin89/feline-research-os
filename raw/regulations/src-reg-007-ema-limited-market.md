# EMA limited-market efficacy and target animal safety guideline

## One-Line Summary

EMA provides a reduced-data framework for certain non-immunological veterinary medicines intended for limited markets under Article 23 of Regulation (EU) 2019/6.

## Why It Matters For CKD

This is the most relevant current EMA source for asking whether a feline CKD product could fit a limited-market logic rather than the full standard route.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- The guideline defines acceptable efficacy and target animal safety data requirements for products intended for veterinary limited markets under Article 23 of Regulation (EU) 2019/6.
- The current effective revision became legally effective on 20 September 2024.

### source_supported_conclusion

- The EU branch should test limited-market eligibility early, because it may materially change data expectations.

### llm_inference

- A feline CKD product could be strategically attractive in the EU if it plausibly fits limited-market logic, but eligibility must be checked explicitly.

## Limits / Caveats

- This source is route-specific and does not replace broader EU marketing authorisation requirements.
