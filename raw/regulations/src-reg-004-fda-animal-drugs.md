# FDA Regulation of Animal Drugs

## One-Line Summary

FDA states that animal drugs can reach market through full approval or, in some cases, conditional approval, with the latter based on a reasonable expectation of effectiveness rather than full substantial evidence.

## Why It Matters For CKD

This is the first official source for mapping whether a feline CKD product might fit full approval or a conditional approval logic in the U.S.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- Full approval requires substantial evidence of effectiveness.
- Conditional approval means the product is safe and has a reasonable expectation of effectiveness, but has not yet met the full substantial-evidence standard.
- Conditional approval is time-limited and can be renewed annually for up to five years total.

### source_supported_conclusion

- The FDA branch should be modeled as a fork between full approval and conditional approval, not as one undifferentiated U.S. pathway.

### llm_inference

- Whether a feline CKD indication can use conditional approval will likely depend on disease prevalence, seriousness, and study difficulty rather than species alone.
