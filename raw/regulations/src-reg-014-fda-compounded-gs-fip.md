# FDA Announces Position on Use of Compounded GS-441524 to Treat FIP

## One-Line Summary

FDA 2024 CVM update stating that no FDA-approved drug is available to treat FIP in cats, while describing enforcement discretion for patient-specific compounded GS-441524 under GFI #256 conditions.

## Why It Matters For FIP

- gives the FIP regulatory branch a direct U.S.-specific source for the approved-alternative gap
- separates compounded GS access from legal marketing status
- blocks the mistake of treating compounded or informal-market GS as a clean FDA-approved active comparator
- strengthens the need for a comparator-boundary memo before any active-control design is treated as decision-grade

## Key Findings

- FDA's May 10, 2024 CVM update says no FDA-approved drug is available to treat FIP in cats.
- FDA says it does not intend to enforce new animal drug approval requirements for GS-441524 products compounded for a specific cat patient under the listed GFI #256 conditions.
- FDA reminds stakeholders that animal drugs compounded from bulk drug substances are unapproved drugs and are not in fact legal in the same sense as approved products.
- FDA distinguishes patient-specific prescription compounding from office-stock nomination logic.
- As of May 10, 2024, FDA had not received a nomination for GS-441524 for office stock.

## Limits / Caveats

- this source is an FDA enforcement-position update, not a product approval or label
- it does not provide a clinical effectiveness comparison between compounded GS, sponsor-grade GS, remdesivir, or other antivirals
- it does not establish what FDA would accept as an active comparator in a pivotal FIP study
- it should not be used as owner-facing treatment advice
