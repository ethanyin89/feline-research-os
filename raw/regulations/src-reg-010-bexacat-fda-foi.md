# FDA FOI Summary: Bexacat (bexagliflozin tablets), NADA 141-566

## One-Line Summary

Primary FDA FOI summary for Bexacat, an oral bexagliflozin tablet approved to improve glycemic control in otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.

## Why It Matters For Feline Diabetes

- converts the SGLT2 branch from review-abstract regulatory signal into primary U.S. regulatory evidence
- anchors indication, dose form, target species, and safety-monitoring boundaries
- prevents SGLT2 route enthusiasm from outrunning FDA patient-selection and ketoacidosis cautions

## Key Findings

- FDA approved Bexacat under NADA 141-566 on December 8, 2022.
- The indicated population is otherwise healthy cats with diabetes mellitus not previously treated with insulin.
- The product is a 15 mg oral tablet given once daily to cats weighing at least 3.0 kg, with or without food and regardless of blood glucose level.
- Bexagliflozin is an SGLT2 inhibitor that induces glucosuria by reducing renal glucose reabsorption.
- FDA's FOI summary describes pilot, 6-month field, extended-use, and target-animal-safety evidence.
- In the pivotal 6-month field study, 64/77 cats in the effectiveness analysis were treatment successes at Day 56.
- The FOI summary repeatedly foregrounds serious safety concerns, especially diabetic ketoacidosis and euglycemic diabetic ketoacidosis.
- Cats previously treated with insulin, cats receiving insulin, and insulin-dependent diabetic cats are outside the intended use logic because of ketoacidosis risk.
- Screening and monitoring include kidney, liver, pancreatic disease, ketoacidosis, appetite, hydration, and other health status boundaries.

## Limits / Caveats

- this source is a U.S. FDA regulatory source, not a global regulatory map
- it does not establish superiority over insulin, diet, or Senvelgo
- it should not be used to infer use in complicated diabetes
- full product insert and current Animal Drugs @ FDA records should be checked before label-level wording is promoted
