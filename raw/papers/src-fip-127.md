# Efficacy of GS-441524 for Feline Infectious Peritonitis: A Systematic Review (2018–2024)

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete publication text. It is deep-extracted as a systematic review of clinical studies.

## One-Line Summary

Systematic review of 11 studies (650 cats) confirming an overall success rate of 84.6% for GS-441524 in FIP, with higher efficacy in combination therapies and lower in neurological/wet FIP.

## Why It Matters For Feline FIP

This review provides the highest level of consolidated clinical evidence (aggregating 650 cases) validating the efficacy and safety of GS-441524, confirming it as the first-line therapeutic standard for feline infectious peritonitis.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* **Aggregated Sample Size:** 650 cats across 11 studies (2018–2024).
* **Treatment Success Rate:** 84.6% overall recovery and survival.
* **Dosing Guidelines:** Standard dosage is 5–10 mg/kg once daily subcutaneous, with adjustments upward for neurological and ocular disease forms.
* **Risk Factors:** Lower success rates are associated with wet FIP and neurological involvement.

### source_supported_conclusion

* GS-441524 is highly effective at inducing clinical remission in FIP-infected cats, demonstrating that FIP is now a treatable condition.
* Antiviral combination therapies can enhance overall survival, whereas neurological FIP represents the highest therapeutic challenge due to the blood-brain barrier.

### llm_inference

* Failure to adjust GS-441524 dosage upward in cats displaying ocular or neurological signs increases the risk of treatment failure or relapse.
