# One-line Summary

Targeted sarcomere-inhibitor study that makes the HCM treatment branch mechanistically interesting from the start.

## Why It Matters For HCM

- links mechanism directly to intervention
- may later anchor a modern targeted-therapy branch

## Key Findings

- small-molecule inhibitor focus
- LVOT-obstruction relevance
- five-cat research-colony proof-of-principle design
- exposure-dependent physiologic effect on contractility, SAM, and LVOT pressure gradient
- acute endpoint signal, not long-term clinical-outcome proof

## Why This Study Matters

This source matters because it stops the HCM translation branch from being written as though feline treatment only consists of conventional symptom management plus skepticism. The abstract and open full-text record introduce a different kind of intervention logic: mechanism-proximal, sarcomere-directed, and tied to an identifiable physiologic endpoint.

That gives the module a real targeted frontier. But the paper is just as useful for the boundary it imposes. The endpoint named in the title is acute relief of left-ventricular outflow tract obstruction, which is clinically meaningful but narrower than long-term disease modification, durability, survival, or routine intervention leadership.

The study used five cats from a research colony with naturally occurring HCM and LVOT obstruction. MYK-461 was administered intravenously while echocardiography tracked contractility and obstruction physiology. The reusable signal is that direct sarcomere inhibition can reduce contractility and acutely relieve systolic anterior motion and LVOT gradients in a dose/exposure-linked way. That is a strong translational proof of principle because it isolates contractility as a modifiable driver of obstruction.

The limitation is equally important. Five research-colony cats under experimental conditions do not define routine companion-cat treatment. Acute relief of obstruction is not the same as chronic dosing safety, quality-of-life improvement, heart-failure prevention, thromboembolism reduction, survival extension, or real-world owner/veterinary implementation. The paper also has industry involvement and declared author conflicts, so it should be cited as a high-interest targeted-frontier source with explicit conflict visibility.

So this card should strengthen the translation branch without flattening it. The safest use is to show that targeted sarcomere therapy is real in feline HCM, while keeping acute endpoint success below final hierarchy claims.
