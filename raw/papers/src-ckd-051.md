# When Should We Start to Treat Feline CKD: Pre-Azotaemic Intervention

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

Abstract-weighted extraction. Full text needed for specific intervention recommendations and evidence strength assessment.

## One-Line Summary

2025 narrative review arguing for earlier CKD treatment initiation, citing SDMA-enabled early detection and evidence of pre-azotaemic weight loss and phosphorus abnormalities.

## Why It Matters For Feline CKD

This review addresses a fundamental clinical timing question: when to start CKD treatment. Traditional practice waits for azotaemia, but this paper argues for earlier intervention based on:
1. SDMA enables detection before creatinine rises
2. Weight loss and phosphorus problems exist in pre-azotaemic stages
3. Earlier intervention may slow progression

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- Increased awareness and availability of SDMA for detecting GFR reduction has led to earlier CKD detection.
- IRIS efforts to standardise diagnosis and staging enable study of interventions in sub-populations based on disease severity.
- Weight loss and phosphorus dyshomeostasis are present in pre-azotaemic stages.

### source_supported_conclusion

- This narrative review argues for considering treatment earlier than traditional azotaemic thresholds.
- Evidence is emerging for pre-azotaemic intervention targeting weight maintenance and phosphorus control.
