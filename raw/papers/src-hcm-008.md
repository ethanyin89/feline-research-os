# One-line Summary

Recent clinical-diagnostic and therapeutic review that bridges recognition and treatment branches while keeping both disease structure and therapeutic caution visible.

## Why It Matters For HCM

- likely compresses modern diagnosis and treatment thinking into one usable overview
- may help keep treatment enthusiasm tied to phenotype recognition
- now gives the HCM shell a safer rule that treatment should be framed as management and progression control rather than cure language

## Key Findings

- recent review
- abstract frames concentric but variable and often asymmetric hypertrophy
- abstract ties disease mechanics to diastolic dysfunction, cardiomyocyte disarray, and collagen-rich fibrosis
- abstract keeps diagnosis multimodal across clinical evaluation, laboratory tests, and imaging
- abstract frames treatment around clinical control, slower progression, and longer/better life rather than reversal

## Why This Review Matters

This review matters because it is the cleanest modern bridge between HCM recognition and HCM treatment in the current seed set.

Its first contribution is structural discipline. Even in a treatment-facing 2025 review, the disease still starts as a structural and pathophysiologic problem: variable hypertrophy, diastolic dysfunction, disordered cardiomyocyte alignment, and collagen deposition. That keeps translation tied to real disease architecture.

Its second contribution is workup discipline. The paper explicitly says diagnosis is based on combined clinical evaluation, laboratory testing, and imaging. That is useful because it supports a multimodal diagnostic story without letting one biomarker or one test take over the whole branch.

Its third contribution is bounded treatment language. The review does not sell cure. It frames therapy around control of signs, slower progression, and improved quality and life expectancy. That is stronger than symptom-only language, but still safely below reversal or definitive disease modification.

## Recognition-To-Translation Signal

The safest promotion from this source is:

- modern HCM translation should stay attached to phenotype and mechanism
- diagnosis is multimodal, but not flat
- fibrosis and diastolic dysfunction remain part of the core disease story
- treatment language should stay consequence-management and progression-bounding, not curative

That makes this paper one of the most useful broad bridge sources for HCM page-layer write-back.

## Limits / Caveats

- current card is abstract-weighted, not full-text reviewed
- The source is strongest for architecture and bounded modern framing, not for a fine-grained intervention hierarchy derived from full evidence review.
