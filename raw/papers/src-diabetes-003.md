# Feline Models of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

## One-Line Summary

Review of cats as models for type-2-like diabetes, useful for separating clinical disease knowledge from comparative model value.

## Why It Matters For Feline Diabetes

- connects feline diabetes to broader type 2 diabetes biology
- can help prevent the module from treating cats only as patients or only as models

## Key Findings

- first-pass metadata confirms this as a 2006 ILAR Journal review
- the review supports cats as a comparative type 2 diabetes model because disease can involve middle-age onset, obesity, residual insulin secretion, progressive beta-cell failure, and islet amyloid
- accessible evidence also notes complication relevance, including neuropathy and retinopathy branches
- model relevance should be separated from direct clinical management claims
- the source bridges spontaneous pet-cat disease and experimental model logic
- its main value is comparative architecture, not practical treatment ranking

## Limits / Caveats

- full text not reviewed; this is an abstract/summary-weighted extraction
- comparative model claims may not translate directly into treatment recommendations for pet cats
- do not import human type 2 intervention hierarchy into feline pages without feline clinical evidence
- do not treat experimentally inducible features as equivalent to naturally occurring clinical disease

## Comparative Model Logic

What can be promoted:

- feline diabetes has type-2-like comparative value
- obesity, residual insulin secretion, beta-cell decline, amyloid, and complications support model relevance
- naturally occurring feline disease can inform broader diabetes biology

What should be held:

- direct treatment transfer from humans
- model-first interpretation of pet-cat care
- claims that all feline diabetes maps cleanly to human T2DM
- experimental-lesion equivalence without design detail
