# SDMA vs Creatinine as Feline CKD Biomarkers

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

Abstract-weighted extraction. Full text needed for sensitivity/specificity values, detection lead time, and clinical thresholds. PMID 25231385.

## One-Line Summary

2014 JVIM study demonstrating SDMA detects feline CKD earlier than conventional creatinine measurement in 21 cats.

## Why It Matters For Feline CKD

This is a foundational biomarker validation study for SDMA in feline CKD. SDMA (symmetric dimethylarginine) has become a standard early-detection tool, and this study provides clinical evidence for its superiority over creatinine. Relevant for:
1. Diagnostic decision-making
2. IRIS staging refinement
3. Early intervention timing (connects to src-ckd-051)

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- SDMA has been shown to be an accurate and precise biomarker for calculating estimated GFR in humans.
- SDMA is a more sensitive biomarker than serum creatinine concentration for assessing renal dysfunction.
- Study included 21 cats with persistent CKD (retrospective design).

### source_supported_conclusion

- SDMA detects CKD before conventional creatinine-based diagnosis.
- SDMA may enable earlier intervention in feline CKD.
