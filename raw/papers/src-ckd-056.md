# FGF-23 in Early Nonazotemic Feline CKD

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

Title-only intake from sheet. Full text needed for FGF-23 concentration data, sensitivity/specificity, and comparison with other early markers.

## One-Line Summary

Study evaluating FGF-23 as potential early marker for CKD in cats before azotemia develops.

## Why It Matters For Feline CKD

FGF-23 is part of the phosphorus-CKD-MBD axis:
1. May detect kidney dysfunction before creatinine/SDMA changes
2. Particularly relevant for geriatric screening
3. Connects to phosphorus management decisions (see src-ckd-026 for FGF-23/hyperphosphatemia link)

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- Study examines FGF-23 concentrations in cats with early nonazotemic CKD.
- Compares FGF-23 levels between early CKD cats and healthy geriatric cats.

### source_supported_conclusion

- FGF-23 may be elevated before azotemia develops.
- Could serve as early marker for CKD in geriatric cats.
