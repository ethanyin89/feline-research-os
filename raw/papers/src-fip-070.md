# Retrospective study and outcome of 307 cats with feline infectious peritonitis treated with legally sourced veterinary compounded preparations of remdesivir and GS-441524 (2020–2022)

## One-Line Summary

This retrospective study of 307 cats with FIP demonstrates high treatment efficacy (88.6% survival at initial treatment end and 96% 6-month survival for those surviving the first 48 hours) using legally compounded remdesivir and GS-441524.

## Why It Matters For Feline FIP

It provides robust, large-scale clinical evidence supporting the effectiveness of legally sourced, regulated veterinary compounded antiviral formulations, addressing the historical reliance on unregulated black-market sources.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- The study evaluated 307 cats with FIP; 49.5% had abdominal effusive FIP, and 14.3% had neurological FIP.
- Survival rate at the end of initial treatment was 88.6%, and 84.4% at the longest follow-up point (up to 814 days).
- Six-month survival was 86% overall, which rose to 96% when excluding cats that died within the first 48 hours.
- Three treatment protocols were successfully used: remdesivir alone (33.9%), remdesivir followed by GS-441524 (55.7%), and GS-441524 alone (10.4%).

### source_supported_conclusion

- Legally formulated, compounded remdesivir and GS-441524 are highly effective therapies that can successfully resolve FIP.
- Early intensive care and support during the first 48 hours of treatment are crucial, as survival rates dramatically improve after this window.

### llm_inference

- Clinicians can confidently recommend transitioning from initial injectable remdesivir to oral GS-441524 to finish the standard 84-day course.

## Limits / Caveats

- Retrospective study design using data obtained via email advice services, which may introduce selection or reporting bias.
- FIP diagnosis in some cats was based on clinical suspicion and supportive findings rather than definitive histopathology or immunohistochemistry.

## Open Follow-Up Questions

- What are the precise clinical predictors for relapse, and how do they relate to initial dosage adjustments?
- How do treatment outcomes compare in cats with severe concurrent ocular or neurological lesions at presentation?
