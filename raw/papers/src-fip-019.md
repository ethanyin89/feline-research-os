# One-line Summary

Real-world modern treatment-series paper that likely extends the FIP module into remdesivir-plus-GS package logic.

## Why It Matters For FIP

- adds practical treatment-era depth beyond the first GS-441524 efficacy papers
- explicitly spans effusive and non-effusive disease forms
- now serves as the first deep-extracted real-world treatment-package anchor in the FIP module

## Key Findings

- abstract objective was to describe protocols, treatment response, and outcomes with injectable remdesivir and PO GS-441524
- cohort included effusive and non-effusive FIP, including ocular and neurologic involvement
- median dosage in the abstract was 15 mg/kg, with clinical response observed in a median of 2 days
- abstract reports 81.3% alive and in clinical and biochemical remission at 12 weeks

## Treatment-Package Role

This paper is not just another GS-441524 source. It describes a treatment package: injectable remdesivir plus oral GS-441524. That distinction matters because real-world FIP management often becomes protocol/package logic rather than a clean single-molecule efficacy question.

The cohort is also broader than the baseline GS anchor. It includes effusive and non-effusive disease and explicitly includes ocular and neurologic involvement. That lets the module widen its treatment map beyond the exclusions in the first natural-disease GS study, while still avoiding the mistake of treating this mixed-form case series as a controlled comparison.

The practical signal is strong. The abstract reports clinical response in 28/32 cats, remission at 12 weeks in 26/32 cats, median clinical response in 2 days, and 81.3% alive in clinical and biochemical remission at 12 weeks. Those details are reusable because they turn the treatment branch from generic optimism into a concrete real-world response story.

The safe compiled rule is: remdesivir-plus-GS package logic is an important practice-facing layer between baseline GS efficacy and neurologic rescue complexity. It is not proof of the single best protocol, and it should not erase case-mix and retrospective-design limitations.
