## Ecology-Risk Role

This paper should anchor the ecology-aware risk branch of the FIP module. Its title does not merely say that some cats are at higher risk. It specifies multiple-cat environments with endemic feline enteric coronavirus. That frame matters because FIP risk is not only a property of an individual cat; it is also shaped by the background ecology of coronavirus exposure, transmission pressure, density, and shared environment.

For wiki reuse, this card should sit beside `src-fip-005`, `src-fip-012`, and `src-fip-020`, but it should preserve its separate job. `src-fip-005` gives signalment and Australian population-risk structure. `src-fip-012` focuses on breed prevalence. `src-fip-020` captures referral and diagnostic-laboratory enrichment. This source is the one that makes risk architecture explicitly ecological: endemic enteric coronavirus in multi-cat settings is part of the risk environment, not just background trivia.

The compiled page should therefore avoid a flat `risk factors` list. A better structure is layered: exposure ecology, group-housing or multi-cat pressure, host signalment, breed/population structure, and referral-context enrichment. `src-fip-008` supports the first two layers. That makes it especially useful for shelters, breeding catteries, colonies, and multi-cat households where FECV circulation can change the pretest landscape.

The boundary is equally important. A multi-cat environment with endemic FECV does not diagnose FIP, and it should not be described as sufficient cause. It raises concern and changes how a clinician or reader interprets later clinicopathology, effusion, molecular, or treatment-response evidence. The source belongs before diagnosis in the recognition sequence, not inside a definitive endpoint branch.

This card is also important because it separates exposure pressure from host-population enrichment. A breed-risk paper might make the reader think in terms of inherited or population structure. A hospital-epidemiology paper might make the reader think in terms of referral bias. `src-fip-008` instead asks what happens when cats share an environment where enteric coronavirus is already endemic. That is a different causal texture and should be represented separately in diagrams.

The eventual wiki page should use this source to create a `setting risk` lane. That lane can include multi-cat density, endemic FECV background, and likely transmission pressure, while leaving age, sex, and breed to signalment lanes. Keeping those lanes separate reduces hallucination risk because it prevents one source from being forced to explain every risk pattern.
