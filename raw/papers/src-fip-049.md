## Abstract

Feline infectious peritonitis virus (FIPV) causes immune-mediated fatal disease in cats with no clinically effective vaccine protection. The methyltransferase (MTase) activity of coronavirus nonstructural proteins nsp14 and nsp16 affects virulence. Researchers rescued two mutant strains by mutating the MTase active sites of nsp14 (N415) and nsp16 (D129), named FIPV QS-79 dnsp14 and dnsp16.

**In vitro findings:**
- dnsp14 and dnsp16 exhibited similar syncytium formation and growth kinetics to parental strains
- Both mutants stimulated increased interferon and cytokine expression in macrophage cell lines

**In vivo findings (experimental infection in cats):**
- dnsp14 and dnsp16 showed significantly lower pathogenicity in cats
- **75% reduction in mortality** compared to wild-type virus
- dnsp14 produced high neutralizing antibody titers (>1:156) at both high (10^5 TCID50) and low (10^4 TCID50) doses

**Implications:** MTase-deficient FIPV mutants are attenuated and immunogenic, supporting potential as live attenuated vaccine candidates for FIP.
