# Prognostic factors for feline mammary tumors

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete abstract text and metadata. It is deep-extracted as a retrospective study.

## One-Line Summary

A retrospective study of 100 cats showing that tumor size is the primary prognostic factor (P < 0.0001) for feline mammary tumors, and that radical surgery increases disease-free interval (P < 0.01) but not overall survival.

## Why It Matters For Feline Cancer

This classic study establishes that feline mammary tumor size is the most important clinical predictor of outcomes. It also highlights a critical surgical trade-off: radical mastectomy delays local recurrence (disease-free interval) but does not alter long-term survival compared to conservative resection.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* "Tumor size was the most significant prognostic factor (P < 0.0001)."
* "Cats with small tumors (1 cm3 to 8 cm3) had the best prognosis."
* "The type of surgery, conservative vs radical, was significantly (P < 0.01) related to disease-free interval, but was of no significance in prolonging survival time."
* "Factors of no prognostic value were age (<= 10 years vs > 10 years) and breed."

### source_supported_conclusion

* Tumor size at presentation is the most critical prognostic factor in feline malignant mammary tumors.
* Mastectomy type (conservative vs radical) significantly impacts the disease-free interval but does not change overall survival time.
* Feline patient age and breed have no prognostic value.

### llm_inference

* Since surgery type does not prolong overall survival, palliative or conservative resections may be appropriate options for cats where radical mastectomy carries unacceptable surgical risk or would severely compromise quality of life.
