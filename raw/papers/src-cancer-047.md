# Feline Injection Site Sarcomas: Data from Switzerland 2009–2014

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card has abstract-level extraction from PubMed (PMID: 30213367). It is a 2018 study from the Swiss Feline Cancer Registry covering 2009-2014. This source directly addresses FISS time-trends and adjuvant hypothesis.

## One-Line Summary

Swiss registry study showing marked fibrosarcoma decrease after non-adjuvanted FeLV vaccine introduction in 2007, supporting adjuvant involvement in FISS.

## Why It Matters For Feline Cancer

This source directly answers the open synthesis question about FISS time-trends and causality. Combined with src-cancer-002 (Swiss registry 1965-2008) and src-cancer-004 (molecular mechanisms), it provides strong FISS branch foundation.

Key abstract findings:

- FISS first described in early 1990s
- Pathogenesis not conclusively elucidated
- Chronic inflammatory reaction at injection site hypothesized
- Alum-based adjuvants role controversial
- Marked decrease in fibrosarcomas 2009-2014 vs earlier period
- Drop occurred after non-adjuvanted FeLV vaccine introduced in Switzerland in 2007
- Supports alum-adjuvanted vaccines involvement in FISS genesis
- Non-adjuvanted vaccines might be safer for cats
