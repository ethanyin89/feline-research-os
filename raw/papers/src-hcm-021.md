## Why This Study Matters

This paper matters because it prevents the HCM module from collapsing into a self-contained taxonomy. A disease shell becomes brittle when every recognition page starts sounding like HCM is the whole myocardial-disease universe.

This source works against that. Its scope is broader: classification, pathophysiology, and clinical presentation of feline myocardial disease. The abstract adds the clinical stakes that make the outer frame necessary: myocardial disease is described as the most common cardiac disorder in cats, usually leading to congestive heart failure, with arterial thromboembolism as another severe associated outcome.

It also supplies a recognition-control rule. Diagnosis and classification are primarily echocardiographic, but phenotypic variability can make interpretation subjective, especially for less experienced echocardiographers. That means boundary pages cannot just list disease names; they need to explain why classification remains difficult and why structural confirmation matters.

That gives the HCM module a useful outer frame. It helps recognition pages remember adjacent disease categories and helps synthesis pages keep HCM inside a wider myocardial-disease map rather than treating every feline myocardial question as if it were automatically an HCM question.

That is why this source is structurally important even though it is not a core HCM mechanism anchor. Its job is not to dominate the disease spine. Its job is to stop the spine from overexpanding.
