# Mitochondrial dysfunction and mitophagy as a driver of chronic kidney disease; An area for future exploration in feline chronic kidney disease?

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete publication text. It is deep-extracted as a scientific review paper.

## One-Line Summary

Scientific review highlighting mitophagy dysregulation as a major driver of inflammation and renal fibrosis in other species, and proposing mitochondrial health as a key future therapeutic target for feline CKD.

## Why It Matters For Feline CKD

It introduces mitochondrial quality control and mitophagy (PINK1/Parkin-mediated pathways) into the feline CKD pathophysiological framework, highlighting a novel class of therapeutic targets (mitophagy modulators) to address the tubulointerstitial fibrosis bottleneck.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* **Pathophysiological Link:** Impaired mitophagy causes accumulation of defective mitochondria, generating excessive reactive oxygen species (ROS), which activates the NLRP3 inflammasome and drives renal fibrosis.
* **Feline Gap:** The exact state of mitophagy and mitochondrial damage in feline CKD kidneys remains unquantified and is a major research gap.
* **Target Pathways:** Review highlights PINK1/Parkin-dependent mitophagy and receptor-mediated (BNIP3/NIX) mitophagy as key pathways for therapeutic investigation.

### source_supported_conclusion

* Mitophagy dysfunction is a highly plausible biological driver of feline tubulointerstitial fibrosis.
* Targeting mitochondrial health and mitophagy clearance pathways represents a promising frontier for delayed-progression therapeutics in cats.

### llm_inference

* Future feline CKD clinical pipelines should screen mitochondrial-targeted antioxidants or mitophagy activators to target the tubulointerstitial compartment.
