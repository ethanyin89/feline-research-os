# Evaluation of chronic stress status and quality of life in cats suffering from chronic kidney disease and suspected feline infectious peritonitis based on hair cortisol concentration analysis and a questionnaire

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete publication text. It is deep-extracted as a clinical-welfare and stress-monitoring study.

## One-Line Summary

Feline cohort study (163 cats) confirming that late-stage CKD and suspected FIP lead to significantly higher hair cortisol concentrations (HCC) and lower quality-of-life scores (AWIS).

## Why It Matters For Feline CKD & FIP

This study provides the first direct, non-invasive physiological evidence of chronic stress in sick cats by linking biochemical hair cortisol levels with psychometric QoL scores, proving that disease severity dictates systemic stress burden.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* **Healthy Cats (n=61):** Median HCC of 181.24 pg/mg; median AWIS score of 1.24.
* **Early-Stage CKD (n=78 total CKD):** Median HCC of 183.56 pg/mg; median AWIS of 1.08.
* **Late-Stage CKD:** Median HCC of 330.15 pg/mg; median AWIS of -0.43.
* **Suspected FIP (n=24):** Median HCC of 896.27 pg/mg; median AWIS of -1.97.

### source_supported_conclusion

* Quality of life decreases (negative AWIS) and physiological chronic stress increases (higher HCC) significantly as chronic kidney disease progresses from early to late stages.
* Suspected FIP represents an acute, extremely stressful disease state with a major negative impact on quality of life and massive cortisol elevation (median ~900 pg/mg vs ~180 pg/mg in healthy controls).
* Hair cortisol concentration (HCC) correlates well with owner-reported questionnaire scores, showing its utility as an objective biomarker for feline welfare.

### llm_inference

* Incorporating stress reduction (e.g., environmental enrichment, veterinary-visit anxiety management) into CKD and FIP care protocols is biologically justified to mitigate chronic cortisol elevation.

### Methodology
* **HCC Extraction:** Abdominal hair was shaved, cortisol extracted using standard methanol washing, and measured via competitive enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA).
* **QoL Instrument:** Completed by owners, generating Average-Item-Weighted-Impact-Scores (AWIS) across multiple physical and behavioral axes.
