  Left ventricular (LV) longitudinal systolic function was investigated in cats with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM) compared to healthy control cats using tissue motion annular displacement (TMAD). The prospective case-control study included 21 cats with HCM and 26 healthy control cats. All subjects underwent comprehensive echocardiographic assessment, including two-dimensional, M-mode, pulsed-wave Doppler, tissue Doppler imaging, longitudinal strain, and TMAD. # Assessment of longitudinal systolic function using tissue motion annular displacement in cats with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy: a prospective case-control study

## One-Line Summary

This study demonstrates that left ventricular longitudinal systolic function, measured via tissue motion annular displacement (TMAD), is significantly decreased in cats with HCM compared to healthy controls, independent of breed, sex, age, or heart rate.

## Why It Matters For Feline HCM

It introduces TMAD as a sensitive, rapid, and reproducible echocardiographic biomarker for assessing early longitudinal systolic dysfunction, which often precedes radial systolic dysfunction or changes in standard fractional shortening.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- The prospective case-control study compared 21 cats with HCM to 26 healthy control cats.
- Both global TMAD (in mm) and % global TMAD were significantly lower in the HCM group compared to healthy controls (P <0.001).
- TMAD measurements were not significantly affected by demographic factors (breed, sex, age) or physiological variance (heart rate).

### source_supported_conclusion

- TMAD serves as a sensitive tool for identifying subclinical myocardial longitudinal dysfunction in feline HCM.
- Echocardiographic screening for feline HCM can benefit from TMAD evaluation, as it detects dysfunction when conventional global indices appear normal.

### llm_inference

- The use of TMAD can streamline the assessment of longitudinal systolic function in clinical settings due to its shorter processing time compared to conventional longitudinal strain measurements.

## Limits / Caveats

- The sample size is relatively small (21 HCM cats, 26 controls).
- Long-term prognostic value of TMAD (e.g., correlation with survival or congestive heart failure onset) requires longitudinal study verification.

## Open Follow-Up Questions

- Does TMAD perform similarly in distinguishing transient myocardial thickening (TMT) from true HCM?
- How does the presence of dynamic left ventricular outflow tract obstruction (DLVOTO) affect TMAD readings over time?
