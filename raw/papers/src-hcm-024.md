# One-line Summary

Pathology-staging paper that deepens HCM severity architecture beyond imaging alone and gives end-stage HCM a clearer remodeled phenotype.

## Why It Matters For HCM

- introduces morphometric and histopathological staging depth
- may later bridge phenotype recognition and severity architecture
- now gives the HCM shell a safer rule that end-stage HCM is marked by fibrotic and chamber-remodeling change, not just thicker walls

## Key Findings

- anatomopathological staging focus
- abstract supports repeatable quantitative pathologic assessment
- abstract ties end-stage progression to fibrosis, left-sided dilation, and intramural coronary arteriosclerosis

## Why This Study Matters

This paper matters because it gives the HCM module a more defensible late-stage architecture. Without a source like this, compiled pages tend to describe severe HCM as if it were simply more hypertrophy. That is too flat.

The staging signal here is different. The abstract frames end-stage progression around increased left-ventricular fibrous tissue deposition and couples that with left-ventricular lumen dilation, left-atrial dilation, and intramural coronary arteriosclerosis. In other words, later disease is described as a remodeled phenotype rather than a simple extension of earlier wall-thickening language.

That is exactly the kind of paper that prevents the endpoint branch from collapsing into imaging-only shorthand. It also gives the mechanism page a safer bridge between remodeling biology and visible advanced phenotype, while still keeping pathology depth below routine recognition authority.

## End-Stage Remodeling Signal

The strongest reusable promotion from this source is:

- pathology staging belongs in the depth layer of HCM architecture
- fibrosis is a leading end-stage signal, not just a side note
- chamber dilation and vascular remodeling belong inside advanced-disease interpretation
- severe or end-stage HCM should not be written as `same phenotype, only thicker`

This card is most useful when it pressures the module toward a layered severity map: routine recognition first, remodeled end-stage interpretation later.

## Limits / Caveats

- current card is abstract-weighted, not full-text reviewed
- The paper is strongest for late-stage architecture and branch placement, not for routine ante-mortem workup ranking.
- Abstract-level access does not justify turning pathology staging into a clinic-first diagnostic protocol.
