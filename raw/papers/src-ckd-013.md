# What outcomes should be measured in feline chronic kidney disease treatment trials? Establishing a core outcome set for research

## One-Line Summary

This core outcome set paper identifies which outcomes should minimally be measured in feline CKD treatment trials, making it one of the strongest current sources for trial-grade endpoint logic in the vault.

## Why It Matters For CKD

This is a direct bridge source between endpoint selection, treatment evaluation, and translational study design.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- There is currently no agreement on which outcomes are most important in feline CKD treatment trials to assist evidence-based decision making.
- To establish a core outcome set for feline CKD, the study used a systematic review plus two consensus methodologies, an electronic Delphi and an in-person consensus meeting, with an international panel of key stakeholders.
- The systematic review identified 104 unique published parameters.
- The eDelphi panel rated 24 of 104 parameters highly enough for inclusion and proposed an additional 20 parameters, of which 3 reached the inclusion threshold.
- The consensus meeting panel rated an additional 6 of 20 borderline parameters highly enough for inclusion.
- After streamlining, the final core outcome set totalled 29 parameters.
- These 29 parameters were grouped into 9 core themes: clinical examination, quality of life, serum biochemistry, complete blood count, urinalysis, total amount of food eaten, CKD progression, survival time, and cause of death.
- The paper states that this is the first core outcome set created for feline medicine.
- The authors state that use of the core outcome set should facilitate easier comparison of results between studies and reduce research waste.
- The paper gives formal trial status to owner-visible and burden outcomes that are easy to underweight in a lab-centered CKD workflow.

### source_supported_conclusion

- This source materially strengthens the vault's move from general endpoint discussion toward trial-grade outcome logic.
- Feline CKD treatment evaluation should not be reduced to a small lab-only panel. The core outcome set explicitly broadens the frame to include quality of life, food intake, progression, survival, and cause of death.
- The current vault should distinguish between `routine clinical markers` and `minimum trial outcomes`, because a treatment trial needs a wider outcome architecture than a day-to-day CKD workup.
- This source supports adding an explicit trial-outcome perspective to the translation layer, not just the endpoint layer.
- The core outcome set is best read as a minimum reporting architecture, not as a universal priority ranking for every intervention class.
- This paper reinforces that `what should be measured in trials` and `what should lead routine CKD workup` are related but different questions.

### llm_inference

- This paper is likely one of the most important new additions for making the CKD system more useful for efficacy-evaluation design.
- The current endpoint handbook can stay focused on operational markers, but the translation layer should now acknowledge that treatment trials require a broader outcome architecture.
- Later trial-design work should likely map intervention classes onto routine endpoints, context endpoints, and minimum trial outcomes rather than forcing one flat outcome list.

## Why This Paper Matters

This paper is one of the cleanest methodological upgrades in the CKD vault.

It changes the question from:

- which kidney markers do we usually follow?

to:

- what minimum architecture should make treatment trials comparable and decision-useful?

That change matters because inconsistent outcome choice can weaken the evidence base even when studies exist.

## Trial-Outcome Signal

The safest read from this source is:

- routine endpoint pages should stay compact
- treatment-trial design should stay broader
- quality of life, food intake, progression, survival, and cause of death are not optional afterthoughts in trial architecture
- minimum breadth is not the same as intervention-specific priority

This paper therefore upgrades the translation layer more than the day-to-day workup layer.

## Limits / Caveats

- Current extraction is still abstract-led and introduction-led, not full section-by-section extraction.
- The card does not yet list every individual parameter under each of the 9 themes.
- The core outcome set defines what should be measured minimally in trials, but does not by itself rank which outcomes best demonstrate efficacy for every intervention class.
