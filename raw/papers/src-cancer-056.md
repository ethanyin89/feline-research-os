## Abstract Summary

This retrospective cohort study evaluated androgen receptor (AR), FOXA1, and related markers in feline mammary carcinomas (FMCs), asking whether AR/FOXA1 coexpression can define a luminal-AR-like subtype.

**Study design:**

| Feature | Abstract-Extracted Detail |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Cohort | 180 female cats with FMC |
| Follow-up | 2 years post-mastectomy |
| Methods | Automated immunohistochemistry |
| Markers | AR, FOXA1, ER, PR, Ki-67, HER2, CK14 |
| Main question | Luminal-AR-like subgroup within triple-negative FMC |

**Findings:**

- In the 180-tumor cohort, 57 FMCs were luminal and 123 were triple-negative by ER/PR/HER2 status.
- AR overexpression was found in 33/180 cases, and FOXA1 index at or above 1% was found in 64/180 cases.
- AR overexpression and FOXA1 index at or above 1% were associated with longer disease-free interval, overall survival, and cancer-specific survival.
- Within triple-negative FMCs, an AR-positive/FOXA1-positive/CK14-negative subgroup (n=7) was associated with better cancer-specific survival than an AR-positive/FOXA1-negative/CK14-positive basal-like subgroup (n=46), independent of pathologic tumor size and nodal stage.
- The authors proposed the AR-positive/FOXA1-positive/CK14-negative group as a possible feline equivalent of human luminal-AR triple-negative breast cancer.

**Boundary:** This source supports subtype and comparative-oncology model claims. It does not establish AR-targeted treatment benefit in cats.

## One-Line Summary

AR and FOXA1 coexpression identified a small non-basal-like triple-negative FMC subgroup that may parallel human luminal-AR TNBC biology.
