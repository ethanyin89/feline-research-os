# Pathogenesis of Feline Diabetes Mellitus

## One-Line Summary

Older pathogenesis review that can show how feline diabetes mechanism framing developed before newer obesity, remission, and treatment literature.

## Why It Matters For Feline Diabetes

- helps distinguish durable mechanism ideas from older assumptions
- useful as a historical comparison against the 2013 pathogenesis review

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- Feline diabetes mellitus closely resembles human type 2 diabetes in pathophysiology.
- Diabetic cats show decreased insulin secretion in response to glucose challenge.
- Insulin resistance is a feature of feline diabetes, similar to human type 2 diabetes.
- Pancreatic islet amyloid deposition occurs in diabetic cats, derived from islet amyloid polypeptide (amylin).
- Beta-cell degeneration is a progressive feature of feline diabetes.
- Despite type-2-like features, many diabetic cats require insulin therapy.
- Diabetic ketoacidosis occurs more frequently in cats than expected for pure type 2 disease.

### source_supported_conclusion

- The 1995 review established foundational type-2-like framing that persists in current feline diabetes literature.
- A key paradox was identified: type-2-like mechanism but frequent insulin dependence and ketoacidosis.
- Islet amyloid/amylin biology was recognized early as pathologically significant in cats.
- Beta-cell degeneration was framed as progressive, supporting later early-intervention logic.

### llm_inference

- This source serves as historical cross-check against overly simplified human type 2 analogies.
- The insulin-dependence paradox remains clinically relevant for treatment planning.
- Amyloid/amylin deserves continued mechanism visibility despite newer obesity-focused literature.
