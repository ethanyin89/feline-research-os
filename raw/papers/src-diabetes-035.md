# Velagliflozin, a once-daily, liquid, oral SGLT2 inhibitor, is effective as a stand-alone therapy for feline diabetes mellitus: the SENSATION study

## One-Line Summary

The SENSATION study shows that once-daily oral velagliflozin is highly effective for feline diabetes mellitus, achieving glycemic control in 81% of cats by Day 180, but requires vigilance due to a 7.1% risk of DKA (higher in insulin-experienced cats).

## Why It Matters For Feline Diabetes

It represents a paradigm shift in feline diabetes management, validating oral SGLT2 inhibitors as an effective, injection-free first-line alternative to traditional insulin therapy in newly diagnosed cats.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- The trial evaluated 252 client-owned diabetic cats: 85% newly diagnosed (insulin-naïve) and 15% previously treated with insulin.
- Glycemic success was achieved in 81% of evaluable cats by Day 180.
- Improvements in clinical signs were noted in 88.6% (polyuria) and 87.7% (polydipsia) of cats.
- Overall incidence of ketonuria was 13.9%, and DKA was 7.1%.
- DKA incidence was 18.4% in insulin-experienced cats compared to 5.1% in insulin-naïve cats, with most episodes occurring in the first 14 days.

### source_supported_conclusion

- Once-daily oral SGLT2 inhibitor therapy with velagliflozin is highly effective for glycemic control in feline diabetes mellitus.
- Patients switching from insulin to velagliflozin require close monitoring due to a significantly elevated risk of DKA (nearly 4-fold increase compared to insulin-naïve cats).

### llm_inference

- Clinicians should prioritize velagliflozin for insulin-naïve diabetic cats, while exercising extreme caution and rigorous ketone monitoring if switching insulin-treated cats.

## Limits / Caveats

- Cats with severe systemic illness, cachexia, or pre-existing ketosis/DKA were excluded from SGLT2 inhibitor trial recruitment.
- Efficacy is dependent on remaining endogenous beta-cell function (SGLT2 inhibitors are non-functional in insulin-deficient type 1-like diabetes).

## Open Follow-Up Questions

- What are the long-term (>180 days) effects of SGLT2 inhibition on pancreatic beta-cell health and diabetic remission rates?
- Can biomarkers predict which insulin-treated cats can safely transition to oral velagliflozin?
