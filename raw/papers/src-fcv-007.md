# Feline Calicivirus Infection: Current Understanding and Implications for Control Strategies

## One-Line Summary

Newest broad FCV review synthesizing molecular biology, epidemiology, pathogenesis, clinical manifestations, vaccination, and control strategy.

## Why It Matters For FCV

- likely the best single modern control-strategy anchor
- gives the module an updated synthesis after the 2022 ABCD review
- now serves as the main FCV deep-extracted broad control review anchor

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- MDPI abstract reports FCV can evade the host immune response through different mechanisms.
- MDPI abstract reports some cats remain persistently infected or experience reinfection cycles with variants of the same strain or with distinct field FCVs.
- MDPI abstract reports these animals may become asymptomatic carriers and pose a significant transmission risk, particularly in high-density settings.
- MDPI abstract reports FCV diversity and evolution have led to new genetic, antigenic, and phenotypic variants, challenging disease control.
- MDPI abstract reports the role of vaccination and direct prophylaxis is critically evaluated and that an integrated approach is essential to mitigate transmission and disease burden.

### source_supported_conclusion

- This source is the current best FCV review for compressing the disease-control layer without flattening persistence, reinfection, and variant emergence into one vague `variability` sentence.
- The paper supports writing FCV control as a composite problem involving viral evolution, immune evasion, asymptomatic carriage, and high-density transmission pressure.
- The paper supports keeping vaccination inside a larger control frame rather than letting vaccine language dominate the whole module.
- The strongest safe read is `integrated control under carrier and variant pressure`.

## Control-Architecture Logic

What can be promoted:

- FCV control difficulty is driven by more than contagiousness alone; persistent infection, reinfection, and antigenic/phenotypic diversification all matter
- asymptomatic carrier cats deserve a central place in FCV transmission language
- vaccination belongs inside an integrated-control strategy, not as the whole answer

What should be held:

- any statement that vaccine presence alone closes FCV control
- any flattening of persistent infection and reinfection into one temporary post-acute phenomenon
- any replacement of narrower vaccine, challenge, persistence, or therapy papers with broad review rhetoric

## Operational Read

This paper matters because it gives the FCV module a modern top-layer control sentence. The
review does not merely restate that FCV is common and variable; it explicitly ties immune
evasion, persistent infection, reinfection cycles, asymptomatic carriage, and high-density
transmission into one coherent explanation for why control remains difficult.

That is why this source should sit above the narrower branch owners. `src-fcv-003`,
`src-fcv-011`, `src-fcv-017`, `src-fcv-018`, and related papers still control their own
endpoint families, but `src-fcv-007` now controls the sentence that explains why those
families need to be held together under an integrated strategy rather than reduced to one
vaccine or treatment ladder.

Its main value is architectural: it stabilizes `integrated control under carrier and variant
pressure` as the safest modern FCV top-layer frame.
