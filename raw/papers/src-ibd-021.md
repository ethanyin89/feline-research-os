## Broad Treatment-Landscape Role

This review belongs in the broad treatment-context branch. It explores therapeutic strategies in inflammatory bowel diseases using human and animal studies. That makes it useful for landscape thinking, but it should not be used as direct feline efficacy authority. The treatment branch must still be led by feline-primary sources, especially the hydrolysate-diet evidence in the current seed corpus.

For wiki reuse, this card can help organize strategy classes: diet, immunomodulation, microbiota-directed approaches, regenerative or cell-based approaches, and other comparative therapeutics. It is useful when the module needs to explain what kinds of interventions exist in the wider IBD field. It is not useful as a shortcut to rank treatments for spontaneous feline chronic enteropathy.

This source pairs naturally with `src-ibd-005`, which is a cross-species exploratory stem-cell paper. Both belong below feline clinical anchors. The review can provide a map of possibilities; the mouse-model stem-cell paper can give one example of exploratory immunomodulatory biology; the hydrolysate-diet paper remains the practical feline treatment anchor.

The safe compiled statement is: broad human-and-animal IBD therapeutic literature can widen the treatment architecture, but feline treatment claims require feline-primary evidence and careful separation from food-responsive disease.

Future full-text review should identify which therapeutic classes have feline support, which are extrapolated from dogs or humans, and which are only mechanistic or speculative. Until then, this card should stay in translation context.

This card is useful because the IBD treatment page otherwise risks looking artificially narrow. Diet-first management is currently the cleanest direct feline anchor, but a mature treatment map should also know where immunomodulatory, microbiota-directed, regenerative, and comparative strategies sit. The review can provide that vocabulary, while the evidence hierarchy decides what can be promoted.

The card should also prevent the opposite error: importing the entire human IBD treatment landscape into feline practice. Comparative breadth is not the same thing as route-fit or clinical readiness. For current outputs, this source should only support `landscape exists` language, not `strategy is recommended` language.

If later feline clinical studies appear, this review can help classify them into treatment families. Until then it is a map, not a proof source.

This is especially useful for preventing treatment pages from becoming a list of disconnected interventions. The review can supply categories, while feline-primary papers supply evidence strength and claim limits.

In current outputs, it should be cited only after the direct feline diet anchor, not before it. That order preserves practical clinical hierarchy while still acknowledging the broader treatment landscape safely.
