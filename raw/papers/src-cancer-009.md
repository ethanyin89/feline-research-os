abstract: "Objectives: Although feline mammary carcinomas (FMCs) are highly metastatic, the literature and treatment options pertaining to advanced tumours are scarce. This study aimed to investigate the clinical outcome of metastatic FMC with or without adjuvant treatment. Methods: The medical records of 73 cats with metastatic FMC (stage IV) were reviewed and included in this study. Metastatic disease was detected by distinct imaging techniques (radiography, ultrasound and CT) and confirmed by cytology and/or histopathology. Cats with adjuvant chemotherapy treatment (n = 34) were divided into three groups: group 1 (n = 9) cats receiving maximum tolerated dose chemotherapy; group 2 (n = 15) cats receiving metronomic chemotherapy; and group 3 (n = 10) cats treated with toceranib phosphate. The study endpoints were time to progression (TTP) and tumour-specific survival (TSS). Treatment-related toxicity was evaluated according to the Veterinary Co-operative Oncology Group’s Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events version 1.1 (VCOG-CTCAE). Results: Overall mean TTP and TSS were 23 and 44 days, respectively. Cats with clinical signs at the time of diagnosis had a lower TSS (14 days) than asymptomatic cats (128 days; P <0.001). Cats with pleural effusion had a lower TSS (16 days) than cats without (P <0.001). Median TSS was 58, 75 and 63 days in groups 1, 2 and 3, respectively (P = 0.197). Toxicity was observed in 66.7%, 20% and 30% of cats in groups 1, 2 and 3, respectively. Conclusions and relevance: To the best of our knowledge, this study includes the highest number of patients with metastatic FMC assessed. Despite the overall poor prognosis, some cats survived >6 months, indicating that adjuvant treatment may be an option to consider in metastatic disease. More studies are warranted for better understanding and management of stage IV patients."

# Metastatic feline mammary cancer: prognostic factors, outcome and comparison of different treatment modalities - a retrospective multicentre study

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete abstract text and metadata. It is deep-extracted as a retrospective multicenter study.

## One-Line Summary

A retrospective study of 73 cats with stage IV feline mammary carcinoma showing that presence of clinical signs and pleural effusion are negative prognostic indicators, while adjuvant chemotherapy may support survival >6 months in select cases.

## Why It Matters For Feline Cancer

Feline mammary carcinoma is highly metastatic. This study is the largest cohort of stage IV FMC cats published to date, providing essential baseline prognostic timelines and comparing toxicity profiles between MTD, metronomic, and toceranib chemotherapies.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* "Overall mean TTP and TSS were 23 and 44 days, respectively."
* "Cats with clinical signs at the time of diagnosis had a lower TSS (14 days) than asymptomatic cats (128 days; P <0.001)."
* "Cats with pleural effusion had a lower TSS (16 days) than cats without (P <0.001)."
* "Median TSS was 58, 75 and 63 days in groups 1, 2 and 3, respectively (P = 0.197)."
* "Toxicity was observed in 66.7%, 20% and 30% of cats in groups 1, 2 and 3, respectively."

### source_supported_conclusion

* Pleural effusion and the presence of clinical signs at diagnosis are major negative prognostic factors.
* Adjuvant treatment may be a valuable option to consider since select cats survived longer than 6 months.
* Metronomic chemotherapy is associated with significantly lower toxicity (20%) compared to maximum tolerated dose chemotherapy (66.7%) or toceranib phosphate (30%).

### llm_inference

* Because no statistically significant difference in TSS was found between treatment protocols (P = 0.197), patient-specific toxicity profiles and quality of life should guide the selection of chemotherapy protocol.
