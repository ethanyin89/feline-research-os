# One-line Summary

Right-ventricular involvement study that widens the structural phenotype branch of HCM.

## Why It Matters For HCM

- pressures a more layered disease map
- may later connect recognition, staging, and severity interpretation

## Key Findings

- right-ventricular involvement focus
- phenotype-depth relevance
- 151-control / 200-HCM retrospective echocardiographic cohort
- RV hypertrophy frequency depends on reference method: 47% by mean + 2 SD and 56% by allometric scaling
- RV wall thickness tracks LV hypertrophy severity and clinical status in the abstract

## Why This Study Matters

This paper matters because HCM descriptions can become too left-ventricle-centric too quickly. Once echo phenotype and wall-thickening language dominate the recognition branch, later structural interpretation can flatten into a one-chamber story.

This source pushes against that simplification with direct cohort data. It evaluated 151 healthy controls and 200 HCM cats by two-dimensional echocardiography, including right atrial diameter, RV free-wall thickness, and RV chamber diameter. The abstract reports increased mean RV wall thickness in HCM and gives two prevalence-like estimates for RV hypertrophy depending on reference method: 94/200 cats by a conventional mean + 2 SD upper limit and 112/200 by allometric scaling.

That does not make RV change a frontline diagnostic rule. It makes it a valuable depth anchor that widens advanced phenotype interpretation and severity architecture. The association with LV hypertrophy severity and with left-sided CHF means this source belongs near staging and clinical-status interpretation, not just in an anatomical footnote.

The paper is therefore structurally important in the same way pathology staging is structurally important: not because it replaces routine recognition, but because it stops the compiled page from sounding narrower than the disease actually is.

## RV-Depth Boundary

The safest reusable write-back from this source is:

- HCM structural phenotype should not default to a purely left-ventricular story
- right-ventricular involvement belongs in phenotype-depth and severity interpretation
- RV change should remain below frontline recognition authority
- this branch complements pathology staging rather than competing with it
- RV involvement can be described as common in this study, but threshold and prognostic claims require careful method context
- advanced phenotype pages should preserve the reference-method split

That is the right promotion level for this source: structural nuance without hierarchy drift.

## Limits / Caveats

- abstract-weighted: the 151-control / 200-HCM cohort size and right-ventricular measurement scope are abstract-backed; specific threshold values and prognostic data require full-text review
- this card currently supports phenotype-depth widening more strongly than prevalence or prognostic claims
- reference limits materially affect the RV hypertrophy count, so the page layer should not quote one percentage without the method
- association with CHF is not the same as proof that RV hypertrophy causes clinical signs
