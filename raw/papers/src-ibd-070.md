# Analytical insights, modulation and compositional dynamics of the feline gut microbiota: a review

## Evidence-Depth Caveat

This card is based on complete publication text. It is deep-extracted as a clinical study.

## One-Line Summary

Comprehensive review of feline gut microbiota in IBD shows decreased diversity and shifts favoring Proteobacteria, with clinical remission linked to partial microbiota restoration; sample sizes varied 10–50 cats per study.

## Why It Matters For Feline ['IBD']

Understanding the gut microbiota's role in feline IBD pathogenesis and remission can guide targeted microbiome modulation therapies, improving clinical outcomes and lowering reliance on immunosuppressive drugs.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

* Feline IBD is characterized by decreased microbial richness and diversity, notably reduced Firmicutes and increased Proteobacteria abundance.
* Clinical remission correlates with partial microbial recovery but does not fully restore pre-disease compositional baselines.
* Sample sizes across reviewed studies ranged typically from 10 to 50 felines, evaluated via clinical and histopathological criteria for remission.

### source_supported_conclusion

* The gut microbiota contributes to the pathophysiology and remission dynamics of feline IBD.
* Modulation of gut microorganisms through diet, probiotics, or immunosuppressants influences disease outcome and remission likelihood.

### llm_inference

* Standardizing sampling, sequencing, and clinical scoring methods is essential for reliable inter-study comparisons.
* Integrating longitudinal and multi-omics analyses may unveil precise microbial targets and biomarkers for predicting and achieving remission in feline IBD.
