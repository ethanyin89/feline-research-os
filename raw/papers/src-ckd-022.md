# Chronic Renal Changes After a Single Ischemic Event in an Experimental Model of Feline Chronic Kidney Disease

## One-Line Summary

This experimental-model study shows that a single unilateral 90-minute ischemic event in cats can produce persistent loss of function, tubulointerstitial lesions, fibrosis, and later glomerulosclerosis over 6 months, making it the strongest current experimental-model anchor in the vault.

## Why It Matters For CKD

The model layer is still thin. This paper could materially change that.

## Key Findings

### quoted_fact

- Previous work had already demonstrated renal fibrosis 70 days after a single unilateral in vivo renal ischemic event.
- This study evaluated renal function and structural changes 6 months after a 90-minute unilateral in vivo renal ischemic event.
- Six adult female cats underwent unilateral renal ischemia and renal function was followed for 6 months.
- Over time, there was a significant reduction in glomerular filtration rate and an elevation of serum creatinine of 31% and 42%, respectively.
- All cats had tubulointerstitial lesions characterized by segmental interstitial inflammation, tubular atrophy, and interstitial fibrosis.
- Ischemic kidneys had variable numbers of obsolescent glomeruli, consistent with development of atubular glomeruli and subsequent ischemic glomerulosclerosis.
- Histology and histomorphometry were evaluated on ischemic and contralateral nonischemic kidneys at 180 days after injury.

## Why This Study Matters

This study matters because it gives the vault something it previously lacked: a real feline experimental CKD-like model with both structural and functional follow-up.

The important gain is not just that fibrosis appears after injury. The study follows cats for 6 months after a single unilateral 90-minute ischemic event and shows persistent reduction in GFR, higher serum creatinine, tubulointerstitial lesions, and later glomerular consequences. That makes the model much more useful than a short acute-injury note.

It also helps the broader mechanism map. The source does not prove that spontaneous feline CKD is usually caused by one ischemic event. But it does show that ischemic injury can plausibly evolve into the same kinds of chronic lesion architecture the natural-disease reviews keep emphasizing.

## Limits / Caveats

- Current extraction is abstract-led plus visible methods/results content, not full section-by-section extraction.
- Experimental ischemia models may not generalize cleanly to spontaneous feline CKD.
- Small sample size and a unilateral injury design limit direct translation into clinical disease expectations.
- The source is strongest for lesion-progression plausibility and model architecture, not for clinical management guidance.
